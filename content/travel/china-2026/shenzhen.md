1 night, used in [[routes|Routes B, C, D, E]] as the mainland → Hong Kong exit. Shenzhen is China's pure-engineering city — fishing village in 1980, special economic zone in 1982, ~17.5 million people now. It's the cleanest modern-China contrast to whatever older cities came before it, but it's not really a multi-day tourist destination. **One night, an afternoon-evening of skyline and food, exit by HSR or metro to HK the next morning.**

## Getting in

- **HSR from anywhere on the eastern coastal line** lands at **Shenzhen North Station** (Longhua District). ~30 min metro to Futian / Luohu central.
- For Route E (Zhangjiajie variant): fly **DYG → SZX** (Bao'an airport), ~2 hr; airport metro Line 11 → central in ~50 min.
- **Don't overthink the hotel zone.** Stay in **Futian** (central, food-dense, walkable, metro everywhere) unless you have a specific reason for Luohu (= old downtown + closest to the Lo Wu border crossing).

## Day 1 (afternoon arrival) — skyline + food

- Arrive at Shenzhen North early afternoon, drop bags in Futian/Luohu.
- **Ping An Finance Center observation deck** (Free Sky 116F, 562 m). Tickets CNY 188 timed-entry, book via Klook. **Currently China's 2nd-tallest building** (Shanghai Tower is taller). Go in late afternoon to catch daylight, dusk, and city lights in one ticket.
- Walk **Coco Park / CBD** plazas at street level for the scale-of-the-skyline effect from the bottom up.
- Late afternoon: metro to **OCT-LOFT** (Nanshan) — old industrial complex turned creative district. Bookshops, design studios, indie coffee. The closest thing Shenzhen has to a "neighborhood with character."
- Dinner: **Tairyo Teppanyaki** (Japanese splurge, multiple branches), **Sha Wan Pao Pao** (Sichuan, viral), **Tan Yu** (fish-pot, casual), or — for the maximum-Shenzhen experience — **Hai Di Lao Hotpot** at MixC World with the noodle-dancing chefs and free manicures.
- Evening: rooftop drinks at **The St. Regis** (KK100 tower, 96F) or **Stallion Whisky Bar** (Futian). Or just walk **Talent Park** along the seafront for the Ping An Tower + skyline-against-Shenzhen-Bay view.

## Day 2 — exit to Hong Kong

Three real options to cross the border. Pick by where your HK hotel is and how much luggage you've got.

### Option 1 (recommended): West Kowloon HSR — fastest, cleanest

- DiDi or metro to **Futian Station** or **Shenzhen North**.
- HSR to **Hong Kong West Kowloon** — **~15 min from Futian, ~25 min from Shenzhen North**. Trains run every few minutes. CNY 75 (~$11) second class.
- **Both mainland exit and HK entry immigration happen inside West Kowloon Station** (the Mainland Port Area is a co-located zone). One transfer, no walk across a bridge.
- Best for: heavy luggage, anywhere central in Kowloon or HK Island.

### Option 2: Lo Wu metro border crossing — old-school, atmospheric

- Metro Shenzhen Line 1 to **Luohu** terminus.
- Walk through Mainland exit immigration → over the covered bridge → HK entry immigration at **Lo Wu** station.
- Hop on the HK East Rail Line into Kowloon (~45 min to Hung Hom).
- Total city-center-to-city-center: ~1.5 hours.
- Best for: a cheap fare (under HK$50), no pre-booking, more chaos and crowds.

### Option 3: Futian / Lok Ma Chau crossing — newer, less crowded

- Metro Shenzhen Line 4 or 10 to **Futian Checkpoint** → walk through to **Lok Ma Chau** on the HK side.
- HK East Rail Line into town.
- Best for: when Lo Wu is mobbed (common Sat/Sun mornings).

**My pick: Option 1 (West Kowloon HSR).** It's the fastest, the immigration line is by passenger class, you don't drag rollers across a footbridge, and West Kowloon dumps you straight into central Kowloon.

## Border + visa note

Shenzhen is your last mainland port. If you've been on **240-hour TWOV** (see [[routes#TWOV primer]]), you must clear the 240-hour clock before exiting — at any of:
- **West Kowloon HSR Station** (mainland-side immigration before boarding HK-bound train)
- **Lo Wu / Luohu** land border
- **Futian / Lok Ma Chau** land border

The 5-day **Shenzhen port visa (VOA)** is an alternative if you want extra Shenzhen-only days; it's issued at **Luohu, Huanggang, and Shekou** for US passport holders (~CNY 956 cash). But it's Shenzhen-municipality-only — useless if you're routing through Shenzhen with a multi-city itinerary. Stick with TWOV unless you're doing a HK+Shenzhen-only trip.

## Why it's only 1 night

Shenzhen has skyscrapers, malls, a couple of theme parks (Window of the World, Splendid China), and excellent food. It does not have density of historical-cultural sites, an old town, or natural drama. One night is enough to feel the contrast and stage the HK exit; two would be padding. The HK side has far more to do.
