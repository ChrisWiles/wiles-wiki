3 nights, Oct 27–30. This is the food city. Guangzhou is the home of Cantonese cuisine, and it's structurally different from Hong Kong — more sprawling, more working-city energy, less polished, way better value. Dim sum here is the real thing.

## Day 1 — Tue Oct 27 — afternoon arrival, Shamian Island

- Arrive Guangzhou South ~noon via the XRL. Metro Line 2 or taxi to hotel. Check in, quick lunch.
- Afternoon: **Shamian Island** — former British + French concession, 19th-century European architecture, cobblestone, massive banyan trees. Pure couple-walk material.
- Coffee at **Catch Specialty Coffee** on Shamian.
- Dinner: **Taotaoju** (founded 1880). Ground-floor **trolley dim sum** at night is the Guangzhou classic — aunties push carts of steamer baskets, you point. Expect a queue; it's worth it.

## Day 2 — Wed Oct 28 — dim sum + Canton Tower

- Breakfast: **Lianxiang Lou** (founded 1889). Morning yum cha is a full ritual here — lotus paste buns, har gow, phoenix claws, pork congee. Go before 09:00 to avoid the locals' morning tea crush.
- Morning: **Chen Clan Ancestral Hall** — ornate Qing-dynasty architecture, now the Guangdong Folk Arts Museum. Roof ridge sculptures are unreal. Open 09:00–17:30 (last entry 17:00), **closed Tuesdays**. We're visiting Wednesday Oct 28 — safe. CNY 10. Metro Line 1 or 8 → Chen Clan Ancestral Hall Station.
- Lunch: **Bingsheng Pinwei** (Haiyin Head Branch) — **Michelin Plate**, the best modern Cantonese in town. Roast suckling pig, crystal prawns, their signature steamed fish.
- Afternoon: **Yuexiu Park** + **Five Rams Statue** (Guangzhou's symbol — legend says five immortals descended on rams carrying rice to the city). Metro Line 2 → Yuexiu Park Station.
- Evening: **Canton Tower** — ride up just before sunset. Two options:
  - Outdoor observation deck (standard ticket)
  - **Bubble Tram** — Ferris-wheel-style pods on the *outside* of the tower. Book via Trip.com.
- After the tower: **Pearl River dinner cruise** (~CNY 180/person, 1.5 hr). Skyline from the water, both sides lit up.

## Day 3 — Thu Oct 29 — gardens + old town

- Breakfast at **Panxi Restaurant**. This is the most photogenic dim sum in China — Qing-era garden, lotus ponds, pushcart dim sum, you eat in a waterside pavilion. Go at 08:30.
- Late morning: walk **Liwan Lake Park** (connected to Panxi) → **Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street** — Cantonese snack street with Xiguan arcade architecture.
- Lunch: **Yinji Rice Rolls** for cheung fun (silky rice rolls with shrimp or beef), or wander the snack alleys — bowl of beef offal soup at any hole-in-the-wall.
- Afternoon: **Museum of the Mausoleum of the Nanyue King** (867 Jiefang North Rd, Yuexiu; Metro Line 2 → Yuexiu Park Station Exit E). 2,000-year-old royal tomb excavated in 1983, and the jade burial suit alone is worth the visit. Official site currently lists the **King's Tomb site at CNY 10** with real-name admission and normal closure on **Mondays**; Thursday is fine.
- Evening: head to **Dongshankou** — the artsy, coffee-shop, old-villa neighborhood. Low-key dinner at a bistro or a cluster of street-food joints. Cocktail at **Hope & Sesame** if open (hidden speakeasy vibe).

## Day 4 — Fri Oct 30 morning — fly to Hangzhou, transfer to Wuzhen

- Morning **CAN → HGH flight**, ideally 09:00–11:00 departure. See [[transport#Guangzhou → Wuzhen]].
- Pre-arrange a private **HGH → Wuzhen West Gate** transfer. Plan on **about 75–90 minutes** on the road rather than a quick hop.
- Check in at Wuzhen mid-to-late afternoon. On to [[wuzhen]].
