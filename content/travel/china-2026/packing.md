13 nights, late October–early November. Weather varies by region.

## Weather expectations

| City | Daytime | Nights | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hong Kong | 22–26 °C | 18–20 °C | Warm, humid, mostly dry |
| Guangzhou | 22–26 °C | 17–20 °C | Similar to HK |
| Wuzhen | 15–19 °C | 8–12 °C | Cool mornings, light jacket |
| Shanghai | 14–18 °C | 8–12 °C | Layers, light puffer for evenings |

Rain chance: low across all cities in late Oct–early Nov. Bring a travel umbrella, not a rain shell.

## Clothing

### Tops
- 4 t-shirts / breathable base layers
- 2 long-sleeve shirts
- 1 light sweater or fleece
- 1 casual nice shirt for fine-dining nights (Lung King Heen, Fu 1088, T'ang Court if booked)

### Bottoms
- 2 pants (1 travel pants, 1 jeans/nicer)
- 1 pair shorts (Hong Kong might still be warm enough)
- Hiking-capable pants for Dragon's Back — could be the same as travel pants

### Outerwear
- **1 light puffer / packable down** — essential for Shanghai + Wuzhen evenings
- Light rain/wind shell
- 1 scarf for Shanghai

### Shoes
- 1 pair low-profile trail runners or light hikers (Dragon's Back, lots of walking)
- 1 pair nicer leather sneakers (restaurants, evenings)
- Sandals/flip-flops (hotel, Lamma beach if swimming)

### Underwear / socks
- 7 of each — laundry at a mid-stay hotel (Guangzhou Four Seasons or Langham Shanghai) handles the rest

### Accessories
- Swimsuit (Big Wave Bay, hotel pool at Four Seasons/Langham)
- Sun hat or cap
- Sunglasses
- Small daypack

## Electronics

- **Universal power adapter** (HK: Type G, mainland: usually Type A / Type I hybrid sockets)
- Portable power bank (10,000 mAh, ≤100 Wh so it's flight-legal)
- Phone charging cables + wall bricks
- Kindle (flights are long)
- Camera if serious — Wuzhen at dawn deserves it

## Travel docs

- Passports (6+ month validity past Nov 5, 2026)
- Printed visa confirmation + hotel bookings
- Screenshots / booking numbers for trains and hotels, even though many trains use passport-based boarding
- Travel insurance policy
- $300 USD cash for emergency exchange
- Credit cards — at least one Visa + one Mastercard. Tell banks you're traveling.

## Digital setup (finalize before leaving)

See [[logistics#Connectivity]]. Short version:

- Holafly China+HK+Macau eSIM installed
- 2 VPNs installed and tested (NordVPN + Surfshark)
- Alipay + WeChat Pay set up, card linked, passport verified
- Offline Pleco + Google Translate Chinese pack + Amap installed
- Train and hotel confirmations saved offline in Apple Wallet + iCloud Notes

## Meds / health

- Ibuprofen, Tylenol, Imodium, Pepto
- Band-aids + blister tape (Dragon's Back)
- Any prescriptions with enough for the full trip + 3 days buffer
- Insect repellent (not a big issue in fall but Lamma/Lantau can surprise you)
- Contact lenses + backup glasses

## Optional but nice

- Collapsible water bottle (refillable at most hotels)
- Small laundry kit (Woolite sheets) for hand-washing
- A couple of energy bars for the long flights

## What NOT to bring

- Cash other than the $300 emergency USD — Alipay covers nearly everything
- Heavy winter coat — it's not that cold
- Big DSLR unless you love it — phone cameras are fine for most of this
- Laptop unless you need it for work
