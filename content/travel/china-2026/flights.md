Book an **open-jaw**: AUS → HKG outbound, PVG (Shanghai Pudong) → AUS return. Saves a full day of backtracking from Shanghai.

Search in Google Flights **multi-city** mode: "Austin → Hong Kong" on Oct 22, "Shanghai → Austin" on Nov 5.

## Outbound AUS → HKG (Oct 22 → Oct 23 arrival)

No nonstops from Austin.

| Route | Airlines | Total time |
|---|---|---|
| AUS → SFO → HKG | United | ~20h 35m |
| AUS → DFW → HKG | American + Cathay Pacific | ~20h 44m |
| AUS → LAX → HKG | United | ~22h |

Pick the **DFW → HKG leg on Cathay Pacific** if it prices reasonably — strongest long-haul product on the board. If not, optimize for the long-haul cabin and connection quality, not the shortest Austin hop.

Expected economy booking if bought intelligently: roughly **$900–1,300/person** round-trip equivalent.

## Return PVG → AUS (Nov 5)

| Route | Airlines |
|---|---|
| PVG → SFO → AUS | United (~28–30 flights/week) |
| PVG → ORD → AUS | American |
| PVG → NRT → DFW → AUS | JAL + AA codeshare — comfort bump |

Prefer the **JAL routing via Narita** if available for the return — nicer seats and meal service.

## Booking timing

- Start tracking in **late spring / early summer 2026** and set Google Flights alerts.
- Buy when you see a routing you actually want to fly. For a trip like this, cabin quality and sane layovers matter more than squeezing the last $80 out of the fare.
- Avoid separate tickets unless the savings are material and you are willing to own the missed-connection risk.

## Seat strategy

- Austin → US connection: 1–2 hrs, don't overthink.
- US → HKG leg: it's ~15 hrs. Pay for extra legroom or premium economy if the budget allows (~$300–500/person extra, worth it).
- Return: same logic in reverse.

## Luggage

- Checked-bag allowance varies a lot by airline and fare family. **Verify the exact fare rules before purchase** instead of assuming a two-bag allowance.
- Even if the fare includes more, use **one checked bag per person max**. The train days and station transfers get worse fast with oversized luggage.
- Carry-on backpack + one checked duffel per person is the sweet spot. See [[packing]].
