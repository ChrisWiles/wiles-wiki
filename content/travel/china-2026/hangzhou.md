2 nights, used in [[routes|Routes B, C]]. Hangzhou is the classic post-Shanghai slow stop — West Lake at dawn, longjing tea terraces in the hills, and Lingyin Temple tucked into a forested valley. 45-min HSR from Shanghai Hongqiao, so the transfer is trivial.

## Where to stay

Two real choices: **lakeside** (close to the action, walkable to causeways) or **temple-side** (Amanfayun, splurge-quiet in the Lingyin valley). Amanfayun is the trip-defining choice if budget allows. Otherwise Four Seasons West Lake is the all-around winner.

| Hotel | Area | Why | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Four Seasons Hangzhou at West Lake** (pick this) | West side of lake, in Yang Gong Causeway gardens | Pavilion-style rooms, on-lake terrace dining, in-house Jin Sha (1-Michelin Hangzhou cuisine) | ~$450 |
| Amanfayun | Lingyin valley, 20 min from lake | Restored stone village in a tea-and-temple forest, top-tier quiet | ~$1,200 |
| Hyatt Regency Hangzhou | East shore, Hubin Rd | Walking distance to lake + downtown shopping | ~$220 |
| West Lake Mansion / Xizi State Guesthouse | Lakefront south | Garden-state vibe, where state guests stay | ~$300 |

## Day 1 — Arrive, lake-walk, tea dinner

- HSR Shanghai Hongqiao → Hangzhou East (~45 min, CNY 73 second-class). Then DiDi/metro Line 5 west into town (~25 min).
- Check in. Late-morning walk along the **Bai Causeway** — start at the **Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao)**, cross to **Solitary Hill (Gushan)**, end at the **Zhejiang Provincial Museum** (free, closed Mondays).
- Lunch: **Grandma's Home (Wai Po Jia)** — Hubin branch on Hubin Rd. Massive, cheap, classic Hangzhou home cooking. Beggar's chicken, Dongpo pork, longjing-shrimp.
- Afternoon: **boat across West Lake** to **Three Pools Mirroring the Moon** (Xiao Ying Zhou). CNY 55. The classic Hangzhou view is from this island looking back at the city.
- Walk up to **Leifeng Pagoda** at sunset. Elevator up the inside, lake panorama from the top tier, golden-hour photos.
- Dinner: **Lou Wai Lou** — the storied Hangzhou-cuisine institution on the lake's north shore (since 1848). Get the West Lake Vinegar Fish and the longjing shrimp. Reserve.

## Day 2 — Tea + temple

- Early start. DiDi to **Meijiawu Tea Village** (~20 min from the lake). This is the working longjing-tea valley west of West Lake. Walk the terraces in the morning mist, sit for a tasting with a farming family at any of the small "农家" signs, pick up a tin of pre-Qingming first-flush if it's the season.
- Mid-morning: **Lingyin Temple** ("Soul's Retreat"). Combo ticket CNY 75 covers Feilai Feng grottoes + the temple. Get there by 09:30 before tour groups arrive. The Mahavira Hall holds one of China's biggest seated Sakyamuni statues; the Feilai Feng cliff carvings outside are 5th–14th century.
- Lunch: **Fa Yun** at Amanfayun (open to non-guests if you book). Vegetarian, Buddhist-temple-adjacent, in a restored stone hamlet.
- Afternoon: optional — **Hefang Old Street** (Qing-era pedestrian street, snack stalls, herbal-medicine shops) and **Hu Qingyu Tang** Chinese-medicine museum. Touristy but a fast taste of old-Hangzhou commerce.
- Evening: **Impression West Lake** outdoor music-and-light show on the water (Zhang Yimou, the same director as the Beijing Olympics). 2 hr. CNY 320. Skip if it's raining; it's outdoors on the lake itself.
- Dinner: **Green Tea Restaurant (Lu Cha)** — original branch in the Lingyin Rd area. Cheap, packed, very local Hangzhou cuisine.

## Day 3 — Exit (morning)

- Sunrise walk on the **Su Causeway** if you want one last lake moment.
- HSR onward — either Hangzhou East → **Huangshan North** (~1.5 hr, see [[huangshan]]) or Hangzhou East → **Tongxiang/Wuzhen** (~30 min) depending on the route.

## Booking notes

- **Lou Wai Lou** and **Jin Sha** (at Four Seasons): book 1–2 weeks ahead, especially for dinner with a lake view.
- **Impression West Lake**: book 3–5 days ahead via Klook or the official site; pay attention to weather.
- **Lingyin Temple**: timed-entry reservations required via WeChat mini-program for peak season; off-peak walk-up is fine.
- **HSR Shanghai ↔ Hangzhou**: 15-day booking window on 12306. So many trains there's no urgency unless you want a specific morning.
