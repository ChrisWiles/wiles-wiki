3 nights, used in [[routes|Route E]] only. Zhangjiajie is the quartz-sandstone-pillar landscape in northwestern Hunan that James Cameron used as the visual reference for Pandora's Hallelujah Mountains in *Avatar*. The park (Wulingyuan Scenic Area, UNESCO since 1992) and nearby Tianmen Mountain are the real reason to come — both are flat-out singular landscapes that don't look like anywhere else.

## Honest feasibility check — read this first

Zhangjiajie is **geographically off-axis** for any east-coast route. It's in Hunan, ~1,200 km from Shanghai, ~1,000 km from Hong Kong, and there is **no good HSR option** — the only practical way in is to fly.

**What it costs to include:**
- 3 nights minimum to make it worthwhile (1 day Wulingyuan, 1 day Tianmen Mountain, 1 day buffer / Yuanjiajie)
- 2 internal flight days against the 240-hour TWOV clock (Shanghai → Zhangjiajie ~2.5 hr; Zhangjiajie → Shenzhen ~2 hr)
- ~$400/person extra in flights vs. an all-HSR alternative

**TWOV eligibility:** Hunan **is** in the 24-region TWOV list per the November 2024 expansion. Zhangjiajie Hehua (DYG) airport is referenced as a 240-hr port in major travel-info sources, but I couldn't find direct National Immigration Administration confirmation of DYG specifically. **Safest play: enter mainland via Shanghai (PVG) on TWOV, then internal flight to/from DYG, exit mainland via Shenzhen or West Kowloon HSR — that keeps entry/exit ports on the verified list.** Don't plan to enter China through DYG.

**What gets cut to make room:** Route E drops Hangzhou and Huangshan entirely. You get Shanghai (4n) + Zhangjiajie (3n) + Shenzhen (1n) + HK (4n) = 12 nights. See [[routes#Route E — Zhangjiajie variant]].

**Verdict:** Worth it if Kaylee is firm on this. The landscape is genuinely unlike anywhere else in China. But it's a real trade — you lose West Lake and Yellow Mountain to fit it. Don't try to add it on top of Route B or C; the math doesn't work inside 240 hours.

## Getting there + getting around

- **Fly in**: PVG/SHA → **DYG (Zhangjiajie Hehua)**. ~2h 30 min, 2–3 flights/day on Shanghai Airlines, Juneyao, China Eastern. One-way ~$200–250/person economy. Book 4–6 weeks ahead.
- **Fly out**: **DYG → SZX (Shenzhen)** ~2 hr, multiple daily nonstops on China Southern, Air China, Shenzhen Airlines. One-way ~$160–220.
- Local: DYG airport → Wulingyuan town (the park base) is a 50-minute drive (~CNY 200 by DiDi, or pre-booked transfer via your hotel).
- Inside the parks: cable cars, electric buses, and the Bailong glass elevator do most of the heavy work. **No driving is required or possible inside the park boundaries.**

## Where to stay

There are three different bases to choose from. Pick **Wulingyuan town** for the main park days, **central Zhangjiajie city** for the Tianmen Mountain day, or split (which is what I recommend for 3 nights).

| Hotel | Area | Why | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pullman Zhangjiajie** (pick this, n1–2) | Wulingyuan town, walking distance to the Forest Park gate | Best business-luxury option near the gate, in-house breakfast for early starts | ~$150 |
| Crowne Plaza Zhangjiajie Wulingyuan | Wulingyuan town | Adjacent to the park entrance, kid-friendly | ~$130 |
| **Hilton Garden Inn Zhangjiajie** (pick this, n3) | Central Zhangjiajie city, near Tianmen cable car | The Tianmen cable car base is in the middle of the city — staying here saves a 45-min commute on day 3 | ~$110 |
| In-park homestays (Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mtn) | Inside park boundaries | Crazy views, very basic rooms | ~$60–100 |

## Day 1 — Arrive, Wulingyuan / Forest Park, sunset at Yuanjiajie

- Morning flight PVG → DYG. Land midday, transfer ~1 hr to **Wulingyuan town**. Drop bags at the Pullman.
- Buy the **4-day park ticket** (CNY 224, includes Wulingyuan Forest Park + Yangjiajie + Tianzi Mountain + most internal eco-buses). The ticket scans your face for re-entry across consecutive days.
- Enter the park at the **Wulingyuan Standard Gate**. Take the eco-bus to the **Bailong Elevator** base.
- Ride the **Bailong Elevator** — the 326 m outdoor glass elevator that runs up the cliff face (CNY 72, ~2 min). One of the most-photographed pieces of infrastructure in China.
- Top out at **Yuanjiajie** — this is the "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" zone. Walk the rim trail: **First Bridge Under Heaven**, then the **No. 1 Pillar of the Southern Sky** (the actual Avatar-rebranded peak).
- Sunset on Yuanjiajie. Eco-bus back down through Tianzi → cable car or bus to Wulingyuan town.
- Dinner: **Liu Xiao Mi (六小米)** or any local Tujia-cuisine spot in Wulingyuan town. Tujia food is the local minority cuisine — clay-pot stews, smoked pork, pickled vegetables, and a lot of chili.

## Day 2 — Tianzi Mountain + Grand Canyon glass bridge

- Early start. Re-enter the park (face scan on the same multi-day ticket).
- Eco-bus → **Tianzi Mountain** cable car → top. This is the wider, more spacious part of the pillar zone — **He Long Park**, **Imperial Brush Peak**, **Holding Hands Rock**. Less crowded than Yuanjiajie.
- Walk down through **Helong Park** and the **Ten-Mile Gallery** monorail.
- Lunch: at the **He Long Park** food court (decent), or carry your own (better).
- Afternoon: exit Wulingyuan → drive 30 min south to the **Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge** (separate ticket, CNY 138). The bridge is 430 m long, 300 m above the canyon floor, with glass panels you can shuffle across. The full canyon hike below the bridge is 3+ hours; you can ride the elevator + tram if energy is low. **Bridge has timed entry and 8000-visitor daily cap — book 1–3 days ahead.**
- Back to Wulingyuan town for dinner.

## Day 3 — Move to central Zhangjiajie, Tianmen Mountain

- Drive 40 min from Wulingyuan to **central Zhangjiajie city**. Drop bags at **Hilton Garden Inn**.
- Walk to the **Tianmen Shan Cable Car** base — the 7.5 km cable car (one of the longest passenger cable cars in the world) runs straight from the city center up to the top of Tianmen Mountain. Park ticket CNY 261 (covers cable car + shuttles + the cave climb).
- At the top: walk the **summit ridge loop** — the **Tianmen Mountain Glass Skywalk** (and 3 other clifftop glass walkways), the **Heaven's Garden** Buddhist monastery, the **Ghost Valley** drop-off.
- Down side of the mountain (after the summit loop): take the **escalator and tunnel system** down through the mountain to the **Tianmen Cave** ("Heaven's Gate") — the 131 m natural arch punched through the mountain. Walk the 999 stone steps from the cave down to the base.
- At the base of the steps: shuttle bus down the **99-bend Tongtian Avenue** — the iconic switchback road, 11 km of hairpins. The shuttle ride is the photo op.
- Sunset back in central Zhangjiajie.
- Dinner: **San Xia Jia** (三下家) Tujia restaurant in the center; or **Chengyou Tujia** for an upscale-Tujia tasting menu.

## Day 4 — Exit

- Morning: late breakfast, optional walk in the city if time allows.
- DiDi to **DYG airport** (~20 min from central Zhangjiajie).
- Flight DYG → SZX. Land in Shenzhen mid-afternoon. See [[shenzhen]] for the onward leg.

## Booking notes

- **Wulingyuan park 4-day ticket**: buy at the gate or via the official "张家界国家森林公园" WeChat mini-program. Face-scan re-entry, multi-day valid.
- **Tianmen Mountain ticket**: book 1–2 days ahead via WeChat or Klook; cable-car slots are timed.
- **Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge**: 8000/day cap, book 1–3 days ahead.
- **Flights** PVG/SHA → DYG and DYG → SZX: book 4–6 weeks ahead. Prices spike sharply 2 weeks out.
- **Pullman Zhangjiajie**: book direct or via IHG; usually has availability 2–4 weeks out except during Chinese holidays.

## What to pack

- Real hiking shoes — the in-park trails are mostly paved or stepped, but they're long, often wet, and slippery in fall
- Light rain jacket — the area is wet ~140 days/year, even in October
- Layers — top of Tianmen Mountain is 1,500 m, 10–15°C colder than the city
- Power bank — long days, lots of photos, limited charging

## What can be skipped

- **Yellow Stone Village (Huangshi Zhai)** — a fourth Wulingyuan zone. Worth it only with a 4th night; cut for time.
- **Baofeng Lake** — pretty, but lower-impact than the pillar zones. Skip.
- **Charming Xiangxi show** (evening) — Disneyfied Tujia minority show. Skip unless the trip needs an evening anchor on Day 3.
