2 nights, used in [[routes|Route B]] only. Xiamen (Amoy) is a coastal city in Fujian with a hot-and-humid subtropical climate, a former-treaty-port island offshore (Gulangyu), and the best Hokkien food on the mainland. It's the "warm beach week" stop on the train route — the trade-off vs. heading straight to Shenzhen is one extra long HSR leg in exchange for a real change of scenery and cuisine.

## Getting there

- **HSR Huangshan North → Xiamen North** (~5h 30, CNY 470 second-class) is the long leg if coming from Route B. Coffee + book + 5G data on the train.
- **HSR Xiamen North → Shenzhen North** (~3h 30, CNY 280) is the easy onward leg.
- Both stations are 30–40 min by metro / DiDi from the central waterfront area.

## Where to stay

Two real zones:
- **Gulangyu Island** (the colonial museum-island, no cars, all ferries): atmospheric but slow — every supply run is a ferry ride, and the island shuts down at night when the day-trippers leave. Best for one quiet night.
- **Shapowei / Yundang Lake / Zengcuoan** on the main island: convenient base for everything else.

## Day 1 — Gulangyu

- Morning ferry to **Gulangyu** from the **Dongdu / Cruise Terminal** (residents and tourists use different terminals — tourists go from Dongdu, ~20 min, CNY 35 round-trip). Buy the timed-entry ferry ticket online 1+ day ahead via the official Gulangyu WeChat mini-program; same-day walk-up tickets are limited.
- Walk uphill to **Sunlight Rock (Riguangyan)** — the highest point on the island. Combo ticket CNY 100 covers Sunlight Rock + Shuzhuang Garden + Piano Museum + Organ Museum.
- Wander **Longtou Road**, **Fujian Road**, and the narrow lanes between the early-20th-century consulates and merchant villas. Skip the touristy snack alley and head for the residential side streets.
- Lunch on the island: **Zhang San Feng Milk Tea** (snack), then **Babycat Bakery** for fresh stuffed-meat pies, or sit-down at one of the small Hokkien spots near Longtou Lu.
- Afternoon: **Shuzhuang Garden** + the **Piano Museum** (the island had 600+ pianos in the 1930s, the densest concentration in Asia).
- Sunset back on the mainland: walk **Yan'wu Bridge / Bailuzhou Park** along the harbor.
- Dinner: **Wu Zai Tian Tian Man** (Mr. Wu's) for sand-tea noodles (sha-cha mian) and oyster omelet (oh ah jian) — Xiamen's two signature dishes. Multiple branches; the Zhongshan Lu one is the busiest.

## Day 2 — Temple + coast + sunset

- Morning: **Nanputuo Temple** at the foot of Wulao Peak — active Buddhist monastery from the Tang dynasty, free entry. Try the **vegetarian restaurant** inside the temple grounds; the mushroom-and-tofu set is the move.
- Walk **Xiamen University** next door (still one of the prettiest campuses in China — colonial Western-Chinese hybrid architecture, lotus pond, free entry if you book the day before via WeChat).
- Lunch: **Wang Ji Yu Pi Hua Sheng Tang** — peanut soup (hua sheng tang) and savory peanut paste, a Xiamen specialty.
- Afternoon: **Shapowei** (沙坡尾) — old fishing-village harbor turned hipster district with cafés, art studios, and the **Art West Lane**. Spend 2 hours bar-/coffee-hopping.
- Late afternoon: **Hulishan Fortress** + walk south to **Yefengzhai / Huandao Road** beachside promenade. The eastern coast road has actual beach.
- Sunset bike-ride along **Huandao Road** (Meituan bikes via Alipay) toward **Zengcuoan**.
- Dinner: **Da Zhong Pai Dang** or any clam-shack along Zengcuoan's **Hou Anlu** — clams cooked 5 ways, blood cockles, fried-rice with shrimp paste. Very local, very loud.

## Day 3 — Exit (morning)

- Coffee + final dim-sum breakfast at **Hai An Café** (waterfront, opens 07:30).
- DiDi to **Xiamen North Station**.
- HSR onward to Shenzhen North (~3h 30).

## Booking notes

- **Gulangyu ferry ticket** — required to book a timed slot 1–7 days ahead via the official WeChat mini-program. Walk-up tickets are tiny in supply.
- **Xiamen University campus** — non-students need to register a visit slot via WeChat 1 day before. Free.
- **Nanputuo Temple** — free, no booking, busy on weekends.

## Why it might not be worth it

Xiamen adds **two long HSR legs** to a route (Huangshan→Xiamen 5h 30, Xiamen→Shenzhen 3h 30 = ~9 hours of train, plus station time and the eaten travel days). The payoff is real — a distinct cuisine and the colonial-island day — but if the trip is feeling rushed, this is the slot to drop. [[routes|Route C]] does exactly that.
