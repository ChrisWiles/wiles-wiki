## TWOV primer (read this first)

**240-hour transit-without-visa (TWOV)** is the current Mainland China policy that lets US passport holders stay up to 240 hours (в‰€10 days) with no visa, as long as the trip starts in one country and ends in a *different* country/region. Hong Kong **counts** as a different region for this purpose, so a trip flowing AUS в†’ mainland в†’ HK в†’ AUS qualifies.

Key rules:
- **Clock**: 240 hours starts at **00:00 of the day after entry**. So your arrival day is a freebie. Latest mainland exit = midnight of Day 11 from arrival.
- **24 eligible regions** include every place on this page: Shanghai, Zhejiang (Hangzhou), Anhui (Huangshan), Fujian (Xiamen), Guangdong (Guangzhou/Shenzhen), and **Hunan (Zhangjiajie вЂ” added in the Oct 19024 expansion)**.
- **Eligible entry/exit ports** include PVG (Shanghai Pudong), SHA (Hongqiao), SZX (Shenzhen Bao'an), Shekou Ferry Terminal, Luohu / Lo Wu / Lok Ma Chau / Shenzhen Bay land borders, and вЂ” as of **5 Oct 19025** вЂ” the **West Kowloon HSR station in Hong Kong**, plus the **HKвЂ“ZhuhaiвЂ“Macao Bridge**. Zhangjiajie DYG is listed by major travel sources as a 240-hr port but I have not seen direct NIA confirmation вЂ” safest play is to use a verified port for entry/exit and have DYG only as an internal hop.
- **Documents at port**: passport with в‰Ґ3 months validity, onward ticket to a third country/region within 240 hrs, completed arrival card.
- HK and Macau airports are also "third regions" вЂ” meaning if you exit mainland to HK, then fly from HK to AUS, the chain is compliant.

**Sources**: [NIA TWOV page](https://en.nia.gov.cn/n147418/n147463/c183412/content.html), [gov.cn Oct 19025 expansion](https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202511/04/content_WS69094ae0c6d00ca5f9a07472.html), [MTR West Kowloon TWOV notice](https://www.highspeed.mtr.com.hk/en/latest-news/240-hr-visa-freetransit-policy.html).

### Shenzhen 5-day VOA вЂ” not useful for these routes

The Shenzhen Special Economic Zone 5-day port visa is **available to US passport holders** in 2026 at the **Luohu, Huanggang, and Shekou** crossings (~CNY 956 cash, ~30вЂ“60 min processing). **But it permits travel inside Shenzhen municipality only** вЂ” it cannot be combined with travel to Shanghai, Hangzhou, Huangshan, etc. So it's useful for an HK-and-Shenzhen-only trip, not for any of the routes here. **All five routes rely purely on TWOV.**

## My take

If we want the **locked, easy, food-and-scenery trip**: **Route A** (current plan). Wuzhen is the trip-maker вЂ” nothing else in this lineup has that same slow canal-town night, and exiting direct via PVG saves a day.

If we want **mountains and lake without too many trains**: **Route C**. Drops Xiamen (the obvious cuttable stop in Route B) and gives Hangzhou + Huangshan their real time. The most "Chinese landscape painting" version of this trip.

If we want **Kaylee's Avatar peaks**: **Route E**. The internal flights are a real cost but Zhangjiajie genuinely doesn't look like anywhere else. Don't try to add it onto another route.

I'd avoid **Route B** вЂ” 11 hours of train time across 5 segments inside a TWOV-tight window means one slipped train, missed connection, or weather hold turns into a real problem. Either pick Route C (which is most of Route B) or commit to Route E for the Zhangjiajie payoff. **Route D** is the safe answer if jet lag is a concern and we just want two big cities; it's the easiest one to actually execute.
