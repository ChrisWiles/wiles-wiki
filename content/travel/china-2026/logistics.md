Everything to sort before leaving Austin. Start ~3 months out.

## China L visa

Apply 6–8 weeks before departure.

- Use the **New COVA System** (launched 30 Sept 2025): [us.china-embassy.gov.cn](https://us.china-embassy.gov.cn/eng/lsfw/zj/qz2021/202509/t20250920_11712385.htm).
- US citizens typically get a **10-year multiple-entry L visa**, ~30 days per entry.
- Since 2024, standard L applications no longer require round-trip tickets or hotel bookings.
- Flow: online form → upload passport scan + photo → wait for "Passport to be submitted" status → print the confirmation → physically submit passport to the **Chinese Embassy Visa Office in Washington, D.C.** or use a visa service. Since the 2024 jurisdiction adjustment, **Texas is handled by the Embassy in Washington**, not Houston.
- Cost: $140 visa fee + ~$66 service fee ≈ **$206/person**. Processing ~4–5 working days.
- For Austin travelers, a visa runner / mail-in agent is usually more realistic than an in-person D.C. drop.

## China Arrival Card

New as of November 2025, free, required for mainland entry.

- Fill it in **before entering mainland China** through NIA's official site / app / mini-program, or use the smart devices / paper card on arrival.
- Does **not** apply to Hong Kong — separate immigration system.
- Best move: complete it the **evening before** the [[transport#Hong Kong West Kowloon → Guangzhou South|HK → Guangzhou train]] so West Kowloon is just border formalities, not paperwork.

### Accommodation registration

- If you stay in normal hotels, they handle registration automatically.
- This only matters if you stay in an apartment / homestay: foreigners staying outside hotels must register accommodation with local police / the NIA system within 24 hours.

## Payments

### Alipay + WeChat Pay (mainland)

Set up before leaving the US. Link cards and verify passport at home, not at an airport.

- **Alipay**: Me → Bank Cards → add Visa/Mastercard. Works at ~95% of mainland merchants since late 2023.
- **WeChat Pay**: bind Mastercard / Visa / Amex in the Wallet.
- Small / rural vendors sometimes only accept WeChat → install both.
- Back-up: bring ~$300 USD cash to exchange at the airport for hard RMB.

### Hong Kong is different

Octopus card + contactless credit cards work everywhere. Alipay / WeChat are not needed on the HK side.

## Connectivity

### eSIM

- Buy a **regional roaming eSIM** that explicitly covers both **Hong Kong and mainland China**. Holafly, Airalo, Nomad, and Trip.com all offer workable options; compare price and tethering limits closer to departure.
- A mainland-only eSIM is the easy way to strand yourself without data on the Hong Kong side.
- Some roaming eSIMs route traffic outside mainland China and make blocked apps easier to use, but **do not treat that as a guaranteed VPN replacement**.

### VPN

- Install **two VPNs** (NordVPN + Surfshark) on phone and laptop **before** leaving the US. VPN provider sites are blocked from inside the firewall.
- Test them on hotel wifi before the trip.

### Maps

- Google Maps is unreliable in mainland China — caches badly, transit data wrong.
- Install **Amap** (高德, has English mode) + Apple Maps (works ok).
- Baidu Maps is the best but Chinese-only.

## Other

- Passport valid ≥6 months past return date.
- Travel insurance: Chase Sapphire Reserve coverage is usually enough. Upgrade to IMG Patriot or similar if pre-existing conditions.
- Offline tools: **Pleco** dictionary, Google Translate offline Chinese pack, **Baidu Translate** (better camera OCR for menus than Google).
- Power adapter: mainland China commonly uses **Type A / Type I hybrid sockets** (some hotels also accept Type C). Hong Kong uses **Type G**. Bring a real universal adapter, not a single-country plug.

- [ ] Train tickets on 12306 / MTR app, QR screenshots saved.
- [ ] Alipay + WeChat Pay tested with a small US purchase.
- [ ] Regional HK + mainland eSIM installed + tested.
- [ ] VPNs installed and tested.
- [ ] Pleco + Amap + offline Google Translate Chinese pack downloaded.
