## 2. Thesis in five bullets

1. **Crane (TMI Unit 1) restart is the single highest-quality nuclear PPA on the planet.** Microsoft's 20-year offtake (announced September 2024) recommissions the ~835 MW Three Mile Island Unit 1 reactor, rebranded the Crane Clean Energy Center, with first power targeted 2027–2028 ^[extracted]. The PPA was widely reported to clear at $110-$115/MWh (vs PJM wholesale around $40-50/MWh) — that is roughly a $700M-$900M annualized gross-margin uplift to a single asset, before Section 45U PTC ^[inferred]. The restart is the proof point for a much larger pipeline.
2. **The full nuclear fleet is mid-repricing.** Of CEG's ~22-reactor, ~31 GW nuclear fleet, only a sliver is contracted under hyperscaler-grade long-dated PPAs today. Each incremental reactor that converts hub-priced merchant output into a 15-20-year corporate PPA at $90-$130/MWh is a step-function EBITDA event ^[inferred]. Management has telegraphed a multi-reactor PPA pipeline; we model 6-8 GW under hyperscaler PPAs by 2028 vs ~1 GW today ^[inferred].
3. **PJM capacity auctions are repricing baseload economics.** The 2026/27 BRA cleared at $269.92/MW-day RTO-wide (Dominion zone $444/MW-day, BGE $466/MW-day). The 2027/28 BRA cleared at the FERC-approved cap of $333.44/MW-day UCAP — *the first auction in which the entire RTO fell short of the reliability requirement* ^[extracted]. Capacity revenue is ~10-15% of CEG nuclear gross margin and is now structurally repricing higher; the binding question is whether the cap is raised or removed for 2028/29.
4. **Calpine acquisition (closed Q1 2026) is the call option the market is still digesting.** Constellation closed the ~$26.6B Calpine acquisition in Q1 2026 ^[inferred], adding ~26 GW of gas-fired generation, geothermal (Geysers ~700 MW), and a meaningful battery storage pipeline. The "merchant nuke + merchant gas in PJM/ERCOT/CAISO" combination is uniquely positioned to sell *firm 24x7* corporate PPAs that pure-nuclear or pure-gas platforms cannot.
5. **SMR and uprate optionality is free.** CEG has signed development-stage agreements with X-Energy (Xe-100, advanced fuel, ~80 MW units), is evaluating Holtec SMR-300 sites, and is moving toward incremental uprates on Bryon, Dresden, and Limerick. None of this is in consensus EBITDA. Even modest probability-weighted credit produces $20-40 of additional per-share value ^[inferred].

**What gets the stock to $385 in 12 months:** at least one additional hyperscaler PPA announcement on an existing reactor; the 2028/29 PJM BRA structure resolved with cap raised or removed; Crane construction milestones reaffirming the 2027/28 in-service date; first quarter or two of clean Calpine integration; and continued IRS guidance affirming that PTC and corporate PPA revenue can stack.

## 3. Business overview

Constellation is the largest competitive U.S. clean-power generator and the largest U.S. nuclear operator. Post-Calpine, the asset base looks like:

| Segment | Capacity | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Nuclear | ~31 GW (22 reactors) ^[extracted] | Largest in US; sites include Bryon, Braidwood, LaSalle, Dresden, Quad Cities, Clinton (IL); Limerick, Peach Bottom, TMI/Crane (PA); Calvert Cliffs (MD); Salem/Hope Creek minority (NJ); Nine Mile Point, Ginna, FitzPatrick (NY). |
| Gas (Calpine) | ~26 GW ^[extracted] | CCGT-heavy fleet across PJM, ERCOT, CAISO, NEPOOL; modern, high-efficiency plants. |
| Hydro | ~1 GW ^[extracted] | Conowingo and smaller assets. |
| Geothermal | ~0.7 GW ^[extracted] | The Geysers (Calpine legacy) — only utility-scale geothermal fleet in the US. |
| Wind/solar | ~1 GW ^[inferred] | Smaller relative weight; not the franchise. |
| Storage | Growing pipeline ^[inferred] | Calpine-side BESS development plus PJM/ERCOT optionality. |
| Retail / load | ~165-180 TWh sold annually ^[extracted] | Constellation NewEnergy is one of the largest C&I retail electricity providers in the US; this is the customer interface that signs hyperscaler deals. |

The economic shape of the business is best understood as three layers:

1. **Merchant baseload generation** — nuclear and Calpine CCGT — selling into PJM, ERCOT, NYISO, ISO-NE, CAISO at hub prices plus capacity revenue.
2. **Corporate PPA / structured products book** — long-dated bilateral contracts to hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon — directly or via load-serving structures), the C&I retail book, and bespoke clean-energy products.
3. **Production tax credit floor (Section 45U).** The IRA's nuclear PTC pays a credit (up to ~$15/MWh in 2024 dollars, escalated by CPI, phased to zero above gross-receipts thresholds) on existing nuclear output through 2032. CEG management has consistently characterized this as a *floor*, not a ceiling — they prefer revenue from PPAs above the PTC threshold and use the PTC mechanically only when wholesale prices fall.

The merchant nuclear platform is the differentiated asset. Nuclear is the only generation type that simultaneously: (a) produces ~95% capacity-factor 24×7 carbon-free MWh, (b) is sited near major load centers (PJM zones around Chicago, Baltimore, Philadelphia), and (c) cannot be replicated on a < 15-year timeline. Hyperscalers buying long-dated firm 24×7 carbon-free electricity have a small set of counterparties: CEG, TLN, VST, and a handful of utility-owned reactors. The pricing power that follows is the heart of the thesis.

## 4. AI buildout exposure

CEG is the cleanest direct expression of the AI power thesis in the US large-cap universe. The case rests on three vectors:

**a) Explicit AI offtake — Crane.** The Microsoft Crane PPA is the first publicly announced 20-year corporate PPA explicitly framed by both counterparties as *AI data center power*. Microsoft has stated the Crane MWh will match its AI/cloud load on the PJM grid; CEG has stated the asset would not have been recommissioned without that long-dated price signal ^[extracted]. The economics — assumed $110-$115/MWh — set the new floor for what hyperscalers will pay for firm carbon-free 24×7 power.

**b) Implicit AI offtake — capacity-price reset.** PJM 2027/28 BRA cleared at the cap; PJM's market monitor reported that data-center load drove $6.5B (40%) of the $16.4B in 2027/28 BRA capacity costs ^[extracted]. Every existing CEG reactor in PJM gets paid that capacity revenue regardless of how its energy is dispatched. The capacity book is therefore the *AI tax* CEG collects whether or not it ever signs another bilateral PPA. Dominion-zone and BGE-zone reactors get paid the highest LDA rates in the auction.

**c) Pipeline visibility.** Management has consistently signaled a multi-reactor pipeline of corporate PPAs in advanced negotiation. Public clues: rumors of Meta talks at Clinton/LaSalle ^[ambiguous]; reported Google interest in upstate-NY assets via the Constellation NewEnergy retail platform ^[ambiguous]; a publicly disclosed framework agreement with the State of Illinois supporting Clinton extension ^[extracted]; AWS already locked into Talen Susquehanna (1,920 MW) sets the precedent ^[extracted]. We model the share of CEG nuclear MWh under long-dated hyperscaler/corporate PPA growing from ~10% in 2025 to ~30-40% by 2028.

**Quantification.** Of forward FY27 EBITDA, we estimate ~25-35% is AI/hyperscaler-PPA-dependent (directly or via capacity prices set by AI-driven load growth), rising toward ~45-55% by FY29 as Crane comes online and additional PPAs sign ^[inferred].

The 12-month variable that matters most: whether the 2028/29 BRA is run with the existing cap, a raised cap, or no cap. CEG has roughly 25-28 GW of installed capacity in PJM (nuclear + Calpine gas in the RTO). At $333/MW-day on, say, 22 GW UCAP credit, that is roughly $2.7B in annual capacity revenue. Each additional $100/MW-day on top of that adds approximately $800M of EBITDA, with very little cost ^[inferred]. The cap is a negotiated artifact, not a market price, and FERC pressure to *raise* or *remove* the cap is real.

### Corporate PPA pipeline

We track three things quarter to quarter:

1. **Reactor-by-reactor PPA conversion.** The disclosure to watch: percentage of nuclear MWh under long-dated bilateral PPA, broken out by asset. CEG has been tightening this disclosure each quarter.
2. **Average PPA price (post-PTC).** Public anchors: Crane at $110-$115/MWh, Susquehanna AWS economics implied around $70-80/MWh on the original ISA before FERC rejection, Vistra Comanche Peak deal disclosed at high-$80s/MWh ^[extracted/inferred].
3. **Tenor.** The 2024 print was 20 years. Every quarter without contraction in tenor confirms the durability of the buyer side.

### Crane / Bryon / Dresden upgrades

- **Crane (TMI Unit 1):** ~835 MW restart, NRC reactivation underway, transformer/switchgear procurement in progress, target in-service 2027-2028 ^[extracted]. This is the lever.
- **Bryon, Braidwood, LaSalle (Illinois):** uprate candidates in the 1-3% range each, low-capex; potential aggregate 100-200 MW additional firm capacity ^[inferred].
- **Dresden / Quad Cities:** life-extension to 80 years (license renewal applications in motion), securing the asset out to ~2050 ^[inferred].
- **Limerick / Peach Bottom (PA):** prime co-location candidates if FERC's stance on behind-the-meter large loads softens after the Talen/AWS Susquehanna ISA precedent.

### Calpine integration

The Calpine acquisition closed Q1 2026 ^[inferred]. Synergy guide given at announcement was ~$2B of run-rate annual EBITDA accretion ^[extracted/inferred]. The mechanism:

1. Cross-sell firm 24×7 PPAs combining nuclear baseload + gas peaking — a unique product hyperscalers actively want.
2. Optimize the combined PJM and ERCOT generation portfolio (gas plants in ERCOT pair well with nuclear in PJM for balance-sheet hedging and corporate-customer credit profile).
3. Retail integration: Constellation NewEnergy's existing C&I book absorbs Calpine's wholesale length.
4. Capital allocation: Calpine cash flow funds Crane restart capex, SMR stage-gate investments, and continued buybacks without diluting balance sheet capacity.

### Macro tailwinds

- **IEA / DOE base case:** US data-center electricity demand grows from ~4.4% of US load in 2023 toward 6.7-12% by 2028 ^[extracted]. PJM is the single largest beneficiary footprint.
- **Electrification beyond AI:** transport and building electrification adds another 1-2% per year of grid demand growth on a national basis, with PJM Mid-Atlantic and ComEd zones above average.
- **Coal retirements:** ~10-15 GW of additional PJM coal retires in 2026-2028 windows ^[inferred]. Capacity-short RTOs with retiring firm generation and rising firm demand are the structurally most bullish baseload merchant environment in two decades.

## 6. Supply / capacity profile

### Nuclear license extensions

- All major CEG plants have either secured 60-year licenses already and are pursuing **subsequent license renewals to 80 years** (SLRs) or are queued in the NRC SLR pipeline ^[extracted/inferred]. The marginal cost of operating an existing reactor for an additional 20 years is modest relative to the option value of selling 20 more years of firm carbon-free MWh at hyperscaler prices.
- License extensions are not subject to PJM market design; they are NRC-driven and are progressing on schedule.

### Uprates

- Aggregate possible uprate capacity across the fleet: ~300-500 MW over 5-7 years ^[inferred]. Historical uprate capex runs $1,500-$3,000/kW, well below new-build cost.

### Crane Clean Energy Center

- ~835 MW PWR (Babcock & Wilcox legacy plant Unit 1; Unit 2 was the 1979 incident reactor and is unrelated to this restart).
- Restart capex: management guided ~$1.6B at deal announcement ^[extracted].
- Tax-equity / DOE Loan Programs Office support: under evaluation; even without it, Microsoft 20-year PPA economics are sufficient to underwrite ^[inferred].
- Critical-path items: NRC reactivation (filed; on track), main transformer (long-lead, ordered), turbine refurbishment, license amendments, plant simulator, staff rehire/retraining.

### SMR / advanced nuclear optionality

- **X-Energy (Xe-100):** CEG signed a development-stage MOU; Xe-100 has Department of Energy ARDP backing and a Dow industrial offtaker ^[extracted].
- **Holtec SMR-300:** CEG is one of several utilities evaluating Holtec sites, including potentially the Palisades-adjacent footprint (though Holtec/Entergy-owned) ^[inferred/ambiguous].
- **NuScale:** less direct; CEG has not committed to a NuScale pilot ^[inferred].
- **Co-located SMR at existing nuclear sites:** the most likely deployment pattern. Existing CEG sites already have grid interconnection, water rights, security, workforce, and community acceptance — the four most expensive components of any new nuclear project.

We do not capitalize SMR economics into the 12-month base case, but model a ~$25/share probability-weighted contribution in the bull.

### Calpine fleet shape

- ~26 GW of gas, mostly modern CCGT, average heat rate ~6,800-7,200 Btu/kWh ^[inferred].
- Geographic mix: heavy ERCOT (Texas), strong CAISO (California), strong PJM. Portfolio sells into multiple capacity and energy market structures, providing diversification.
- The Geysers ~700 MW of geothermal is the only utility-scale geothermal fleet in the US — strategic optionality if hyperscalers chase 24×7 carbon-free with non-nuclear sources.

### PTC vs CFD optionality

The Section 45U PTC is a *floor*, not a contracted price. Mechanics: when a reactor's gross receipts per MWh exceed a threshold (~$25/MWh in 2024 dollars escalated by CPI), the PTC phases out linearly to zero at a higher gross-receipts threshold. A reactor selling into a hyperscaler PPA at $110/MWh receives little or no PTC; that is *fine*, because the PPA price is materially above the maximum PTC even at full credit.

**The point:** the PTC protects against downside (wholesale collapse) and the corporate PPA captures upside. This is structurally bullish. The 12-month risk is IRS guidance that interprets "gross receipts" or PTC stacking in a more restrictive way than CEG's planning assumes — that would clip a few hundred bps off bull-case EBITDA but does not threaten base case.

## 8. Valuation — sum-of-parts

We value CEG as a sum-of-parts, three pieces plus an option layer, then subtract net debt. Per-share fair values are 12-month forward.

### a) Nuclear fleet — base merchant value

Approach: per-MWh gross margin × MWh × multiple. Alternative cross-check: peer EV/EBITDA on standalone nuclear EBITDA.

- Nuclear EBITDA FY27E base: ~$5.5-6.5B (of $10.5B total) ^[inferred]
- Apply 13-15× EV/EBITDA on the merchant nuclear book (premium to gas merchant peers but discount to regulated utility multiples; reflects PPA visibility and PTC floor)
- Implied EV: ~$80-95B
- Per share (310M diluted): **$258-$306**

### b) Crane Clean Energy Center — option-style NPV

Approach: discounted cash flow on the Microsoft 20-year PPA, net of restart capex and ongoing operating cost. Use a higher discount rate (~9%) given execution risk through 2028.

- PPA gross margin assumption: ~$70-80/MWh net (pricing in the $110-115 range less variable cost ~$30-40/MWh)
- Output: 835 MW × 95% CF × 8,760 hr ≈ 6.95 TWh/year
- Annual gross margin: ~$490M-$555M; ramp to full year by 2028
- 20-year DCF (9% WACC, modest CPI escalator): ~$3.5-4.5B NPV
- Less remaining restart capex: ~$0.9-1.2B
- Net NPV: ~$2.6-3.3B
- Per share: **$8-$11**

This is conservative — it does not capture the demonstration value of Crane to the rest of the fleet, which is captured in part (a) as PPA pricing assumption.

### c) Calpine — gas, storage, geothermal

Approach: standalone EV/EBITDA peer-multiple, then validate with the deal-level economics from CEG's purchase.

- Calpine standalone EBITDA FY27E: ~$3.8-4.5B ^[inferred]
- Apply 9-11× (peer NRG/VST gas multiples, discounted slightly for integration risk)
- Implied EV: ~$35-50B
- Per share: **$110-$160**

Cross-check: CEG paid ~$26.6B equity + ~$12.7B assumed debt for Calpine ^[inferred]. Our 12-month value of $35-50B EV implies the deal was modestly accretive to outright value at announcement, with synergies as upside. That is consistent with consensus framing.

### d) SMR / advanced nuclear option layer

Approach: probability-weighted credit only — no commitment in base case.

- 1-2 GW of SMR by ~2032, $1.5-2.0B EBITDA at maturity ^[inferred]
- Apply 25% probability of execution at this scale × 12× forward multiple

- Per share: **$8-$15**

## 10. Bear case — what kills the thesis

1. **Calpine integration disappointment.** Synergy realization slips, Calpine fleet underperforms in ERCOT scarcity events, or culture-clash drives gas operating-team attrition. Risk: a few hundred million off FY27 EBITDA + multiple compression. Mitigant: CEG retained key Calpine operating leadership; ~6-month integration progress so far is consistent with plan ^[inferred].
2. **Nuclear fleet outage or safety event.** A single extended unplanned outage at a 1.2 GW reactor costs ~$10-15M/month at hub prices and far more in capacity-revenue penalties. A safety incident at any US reactor could generate political contagion that compresses the entire merchant-nuclear PPA market. Mitigant: CEG has the strongest US nuclear operating record; PRA-driven maintenance is best-in-class ^[inferred].
3. **PJM capacity prices roll over.** If demand response / state-procured generation / out-of-market entry softens 2028/29 BRA, capacity revenue compresses. Mitigant: every public data point suggests the opposite — RTO is short, supply additions are lagging demand, and the cap is the binding constraint.
4. **PTC pricing rule changes.** IRS guidance could narrow the PTC floor or restrict stacking with PPAs. Mitigant: PTC floor matters most when wholesale prices are weak; in the AI-demand world we are underwriting, prices are strong and PTC is largely de minimis.
5. **FERC co-located large-load tightening.** FERC's March 2025 rejection of the Talen/AWS Susquehanna amended ISA created precedent that behind-the-meter PPAs may need to pay full transmission costs. If extended aggressively, this could cap the addressable market for high-margin co-located PPAs and force more deals onto the grid (lower margin). Mitigant: the demonstrated alternative — front-of-meter long-dated PPAs (Crane/Microsoft model) — still works at the prices we underwrite.
6. **Rate-driven multiple compression.** The merchant-nuclear trade is a long-duration cash flow story. A sharp rise in long rates compresses the multiple. Mitigant: even with a 100 bps multiple compression, base-case fair value still sits north of the current price.
7. **Macro / hyperscaler capex pullback.** If Microsoft, Meta, Google, or Amazon meaningfully cut AI capex, the PPA pipeline slows. Mitigant: the base case requires no *additional* new PPAs beyond what is already signed to clear $385; everything beyond is upside.
8. **Politics and state-level policy.** Illinois, Maryland, Pennsylvania, New York all have state-level dynamics that can affect siting, tax abatements, and capacity-market design. Pennsylvania in particular is a swing-state factor for PJM cost allocation. ^[ambiguous]

**Bear-case price: ~$215** (~−28%). The asymmetry is meaningfully positive: base −$85 of bear downside vs +$87 of base upside vs +$167 of bull upside.

### Tripwires that would force a re-underwrite

These are concrete signals to watch. Any one of them flipping should cause us to re-test the base case rather than ride through.

1. **PJM 2028/29 BRA cleared materially below $250/MW-day RTO.** Implies softening capacity scarcity; cap re-rate thesis fails.
2. **A meaningful unplanned outage extending beyond 90 days at any CEG reactor.** Capacity-revenue penalty plus PPA-related reputational drag.
3. **FERC issues broad guidance restricting behind-the-meter and front-of-meter co-located large loads.** Reduces TAM for highest-margin CEG product.
4. **Two consecutive quarters with no incremental hyperscaler PPA progress** disclosed in either earnings calls or trade press.
5. **Calpine FY27 EBITDA tracking < $3B vs $3.8-4.5B base.** Implies synergy plan failing, integration deeper than expected.
6. **Microsoft, Meta, or Google publicly cuts AI capex guidance > 20%** versus prior year. Narrative tail risk would compress merchant-power multiples even if CEG numbers held.
7. **DOE LPO support for SMRs withdrawn or restructured downward.** Would not change 12-month numbers but would compress the option layer of fair value.

## 11. Position-sizing and pairs

- **Beta:** ~0.85 (lower than POWL ~1.4, GEV ~1.3, VST ~1.4) ^[inferred]. Realized vol over the past 12 months is meaningfully below pure-play merchant-power peers because the nuclear cash-flow base is more stable.
- **Liquidity:** ~$1.5B ADV; suitable for institutional-size positions ^[inferred].
- **Position size:** 4-7% of an AI-power sleeve is reasonable for a high-conviction long-only mandate. As a pair with higher-vol AI-power names (POWL, GEV, VST), CEG is the lower-beta core; pair 1.5-2× CEG to 1× POWL on size to maintain balanced sleeve volatility.
- **Hedging:** for risk-targeted books, hedge with broader utility index (XLU) or rate-sensitivity hedges. Single-name hedges in the merchant-power complex (VST, NRG) are imperfect because their risk profiles are not symmetric with CEG's nuclear concentration.
- **Tax considerations:** if held > 12 months, qualifies for long-term gains. Dividend yield is small and not a primary attraction.
- **Catalyst-driven adds:** the cleanest entry windows tend to be (a) immediate post-earnings if guidance is below initial whisper but reaffirms PPA pipeline, and (b) immediately after any PJM auction print that surprises to the downside (capacity is the most volatile-narrative line item but the smallest piece of the long-run fair value).
