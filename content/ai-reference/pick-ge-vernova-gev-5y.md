## 1. Header and one-line thesis

GEV is the cleanest US-listed pure-play on the **structural re-rating of electricity** as an input to every form of compute, manufacturing, and transport over the next decade. Three engines compound through FY30: (1) **Gas Power** monetizes a 100+ GW slot reservation book at 10-20+ point higher per-kW pricing through 2030, with services attaching at high-teens margin behind every install; (2) **Electrification** — now including Prolec GE — rides a 15-year US grid investment cycle, an LPT shortage that will not clear before 2030, and a global HVDC build-out where GEV is one of three credible suppliers; (3) **Nuclear/SMR** monetizes a base-load services franchise on the existing BWR fleet plus first-of-a-kind BWRX-300 deployments at OPG Darlington (COD ~2029) and a TVA Clinch River template that could replicate at hyperscaler-tied campuses through the back half of the decade. **Wind** is treated as a controlled drag through FY27, breaking even by FY28 and modestly contributing thereafter — a meaningful upgrade to the 12-month case's assumption of perpetual loss. The 5-year base case takes revenue to **$56-60B**, EBITDA margin to **~22%**, and FCF to **$11-13B/yr** by FY30.^[inferred from extrapolation of Dec 2025 / Apr 2026 management framework]

## 2. Thesis in seven points (5-year durability)

1. **Power scarcity is structural, not cyclical.** Combined gas-turbine equipment backlog plus slot-reservation agreements reached **100 GW** at end-Q1 2026 (90 customers, 24 countries) ^[extracted], up from 83 GW at YE2025. Management guides to **≥110 GW by YE2026** and CEO Strazik stated turbine reservations will be **sold out through 2030 by end-2026**.^[extracted] Slot pricing in Q1 2026 was **10-20 points higher per kW** than Q4 2025 orders.^[extracted] This is not a 2-3 quarter capex cycle — it is a 5-7 year build-out driven by hyperscaler load growth, coal retirements (~50-90 GW through 2030), industrial reshoring, and global LNG-to-power. The bottleneck is fixed-cost OEM capacity and a transformer shortage that will not clear before 2030.

2. **Services compound underneath the install base — and that is where the duration sits.** Services already represent **>55% of GEV's $163B Q1 2026 backlog**.^[extracted] Every gas turbine sold today drops a services tail of 25-30 years at high-teens to low-20s segment margin, with parts/upgrades/digital adders. The H-class fleet crossed **3 million operating hours in 2025**.^[extracted] By FY30 the new-unit production wave will have lifted the global H-class installed base another ~50% and services will be the dominant Power-segment EBITDA contributor. Services is also the natural offset to any new-unit demand pause: services revenue runs through any cyclical lull in equipment.

3. **Electrification rides a 15-year US grid investment cycle and a global transformer shortage.** Q1 2026 Electrification booked **$2.4B of equipment orders for data centers in a single quarter — more than all of FY2025**.^[extracted] Prolec GE (closed Feb 2, 2026) is the largest North American LPT franchise — five US plants plus Mexican capacity, ~10,000 employees — into a market NIAC has flagged as a **national-reliability shortage** with global LPT lead times stretched **40+ months**.^[extracted] Mgmt has guided Electrification backlog to **double from $30B to $60B by 2028** and the segment to **20%+ EBITDA margin** at the same horizon.^[extracted] By FY30 the segment is plausibly **$22-26B revenue at 20-22% EBITDA margin**, on par with Power EBITDA dollars and on a higher multiple.

4. **Wind normalizes by FY28 — a structural improvement vs the 12-month bear case.** The 12-month dossier treats Wind as a permanent drag. The 5-year frame is materially better: the in-process restructuring (~$225M total cost, completion mid-2026) ^[extracted] plus a smaller Offshore book and tariff resolution should take Wind from a **~$400M FY26 EBIT loss** to **break-even by FY28** and modestly accretive (LSD-MSD positive margin) through FY30.^[inferred] The bear case is another Wind impairment cycle ($1-3B non-cash) — but the *cash* trajectory normalizes regardless. By FY28-FY30 Wind contributes a small positive to consolidated margin rather than the ~3-4 point drag it represents in FY25-FY26.

5. **BWRX-300 SMR optionality crystallizes 2028-2032.** OPG Darlington Unit 1 begins construction with COD targeted **end-2029**.^[extracted] TVA Clinch River NRC review is expected to complete by **December 2026**, with site-prep starting 2026.^[extracted] If TVA dockets, the implied template for replicated SMR siting at hyperscaler campuses is the asymmetric upside lever for FY29-FY32. Hyperscaler "volume agreements" potentially extending to 2035 are reportedly under discussion.^[extracted] The 5-year case does not require SMR to print revenue at scale — it requires the **option to be retained and re-priced** as the regulatory and supply chain envelope normalizes. A single hyperscaler-tied SMR site announcement (e.g., a Microsoft / Meta / Google campus pairing) would re-rate the segment at sum-of-parts.

6. **Prolec GE LPT capacity scaling into a structural shortage.** The acquisition was struck at **$5.275B for the remaining 50%** of the JV, implying **~$10.55B EV** for 100%.^[extracted] At Prolec's standalone trailing economics — call it **$500-700M EBITDA scaling to $1.0-1.3B by FY30** ^[inferred] — and 22-26x forward EV/EBITDA on grid-cycle peers, by FY30 the standalone Prolec value could approach **$25-30B**. That alone covers ~10% of today's market cap. The strategic value is even larger: GEV now controls pricing on a large fraction of the North American LPT slot book *exactly* as scarcity peaks.

7. **5-year FCF inflection is the most underappreciated element of the story.** Q1 2026 alone produced **$4.8B FCF** — exceeding all of FY2025's $3.7B.^[extracted] Mgmt guides cumulative **2025-2028 FCF >$22B**.^[extracted] By FY30, FCF run-rate of **$11-13B/yr** is plausible at base case, ~25-30% of FY30 EBITDA dollars and ~4-5% FCF yield on today's market cap.^[inferred] At that scale, capital return alone (1/3 of cash generation per management framework, current $10B buyback authorization, doubled $0.50/q dividend) compounds equity at ~6-8%/yr before any organic earnings growth.

## 3. Business overview — segment trajectory through FY30

GE Vernova was spun out of GE on April 2, 2024. Today it operates **three reportable segments**.

### Power (Gas / Steam / Hydro / Nuclear Services)
The largest segment by revenue and the cash anchor of the 5-year thesis. By FY30 Power is plausibly **$28-32B revenue at 20-23% EBITDA margin** ^[inferred] driven by:
- **Gas Power equipment**: production output rising from 37 H-class turbines in 2025 to **62 in 2026**, **74 in 2027** ^[extracted], plausibly **~85-95 by 2028-2029** at a steady-state Greenville+module-fab cadence.^[inferred] Each incremental H-class unit at hyperscaler-tied pricing carries gross-margin economics 200-400 bps above 2024 baseline.
- **Gas Power services**: the recurring revenue tail. By FY30 services is plausibly the **larger** of the two Gas-Power lines on a margin-dollar basis — services attach is ~$0.50-0.70 of services NPV per dollar of equipment sold, with much higher EBITDA margin.^[inferred]
- **Steam Power services**: continued mid-single-digit revenue, high-margin services on the legacy steam fleet. Schenectady, NY remains the steam/generator center of excellence ($41M expansion 2025-2026).^[extracted]
- **Hydro Power**: low-single-digit secular grower; small contributor to consolidated EBITDA.
- **Nuclear Services**: the BWR services franchise compounds 5-7%/yr through FY30 as utilities defer retirements and uprate existing units. BWRX-300 SMR shows up as initial revenue from FY28-FY29 (Darlington commissioning, TVA site prep) and scales meaningfully only post-FY30 in the base case.

### Wind (Onshore + Offshore)
Onshore (LM Wind blades, 2.x/3.x/5.x platforms, Cypress 6.x) plus Offshore (Haliade-X 14MW). 5-year trajectory:
- FY26: ~$8.5B revenue, **EBIT loss ~$400M** ^[extracted as guidance]
- FY27: ~$8.0-8.5B revenue, EBIT loss narrowing to **~$150-250M**.^[inferred]
- FY28: **break-even** to slightly positive on a smaller, more disciplined book.^[inferred]
- FY29-FY30: **LSD-MSD positive segment margin** as services on the installed onshore fleet (>120 GW globally^[inferred]) becomes the dominant earnings line.
- Offshore is being managed for execution rather than re-bid; the segment is being right-sized to demand, not to a growth target.

### Electrification (Grid / Power Conversion / Solar+Storage / Prolec GE)
The fastest-growing segment and the second pillar of the 5-year thesis. By FY30 Electrification is plausibly **$22-26B revenue at 20-22% EBITDA margin**.^[inferred] Sub-businesses:
- **Grid Solutions** — high-voltage AC/DC switchgear, breakers, HVDC converters. HVDC alone is a multi-decade global build-out where GEV is one of three credible suppliers (alongside Hitachi Energy, Siemens Energy).
- **Power Conversion** — drives, motors, large electric machines.
- **Solar & Storage Solutions** — utility-scale BESS plus PV inverters.
- **Prolec GE** — large power transformers, generator step-up units, distribution transformers, transformer services. Consolidates from Feb 2, 2026.

## 4. AI buildout exposure — quantified for the 5-year horizon

GEV's exposure to the AI data-center buildout is concentrated in two segments and one optionality bucket — but the *5-year* shape of that exposure differs meaningfully from the 12-month dossier's snapshot.

### Gas Power: gas-to-power for hyperscalers
Of the 100 GW combined turbine book at end-Q1 2026, management explicitly tied **20% (20 GW) to data-center load**.^[extracted] *Incremental* slot demand is materially more concentrated in DCs than the cumulative book — Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 incremental orders skew **30-50% to hyperscaler-backed gas-to-power offtake**.^[inferred] By FY28-FY30 the hyperscaler share of the cumulative book likely rises to **30-35%** as 2024-2025 vintage utility orders execute and hyperscaler-tied 2026-2030 slots fill the pipeline.^[inferred] Behind-the-meter gas + GEV turbine packaging (e.g., the Chevron-GEV-DC offtake template ^[extracted]) is plausibly the **single largest** channel for new gas-fired generation tied to compute through 2030.

### Electrification: transformers, grid, switchgear for utility upgrades
Q1 2026 Electrification booked **$2.4B of data-center equipment orders in one quarter**, more than all of FY2025 combined.^[extracted] At a run-rate that order line is approaching half of the segment's total intake.^[inferred] Through FY30, the 5-year shape of the DC-tied Electrification book is:
- **Direct DC equipment** (medium- and high-voltage switchgear, isolated-phase bus, generator step-up transformers behind on-site gas turbines): plausibly **$8-12B/yr by FY28-FY30**.^[inferred]
- **Utility upgrade gear** (LPTs, HVDC converter stations, grid digital) tied to the same DC load: plausibly **$10-15B/yr by FY28-FY30**, with the DC-correlation share rising from ~25% in FY26 to ~50%+ by FY30.^[inferred]
- Prolec LPT slot book is heavily DC-tied as hyperscalers and the utilities serving them lock in transformer capacity through 2028+.^[inferred]

### Nuclear/SMR: optionality for nuclear-powered campuses
BWRX-300 has Darlington under construction (COD ~2029) and TVA Clinch River permit review on track for December 2026.^[extracted] Through FY30, the realistic SMR revenue trajectory is:
- FY26-FY27: project execution revenue on Darlington Unit 1; site-prep revenue on TVA Clinch River.
- FY28: Darlington commissioning; potentially a second OPG unit or a hyperscaler-tied site announcement.
- FY29: Darlington Unit 1 COD; revenue inflection on parts/services.
- FY30: small but accelerating revenue line; meaningful only in option value.
- **Beyond FY30** is where SMR revenue could become material at the consolidated level — but the 5-year price reflects the *option*, not the cash flows.

## 5. Demand drivers (5-year)

### Gas turbine slot pricing trajectory
- **FY26 incremental pricing**: 10-20 points higher per kW vs FY25 ^[extracted]
- **FY27-FY28**: pricing plausibly flat-to-up on continued tight global capacity at GEV / Mitsubishi / Siemens Energy as all three OEMs run at fully booked output.^[inferred]
- **FY29-FY30**: pricing risk inflects negatively only if **all three OEMs** materially expand capacity *and* hyperscaler load slows. The base case has Mitsubishi production doubling by 2027-2028 ^[extracted] and Siemens Energy expanding incrementally — but neither OEM expects to clear the global backlog before 2030. Net: pricing holds through FY28; modest erosion possible FY29-FY30 but not enough to break the thesis.

### Transformer LPT lead times remain elevated
Global LPT lead times are **40+ months** ^[extracted] and the underlying choke point — grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES), specialized bushings, oil-filled tank fabrication, and skilled coil-winding labor — does not clear before 2028-2029 even with the largest publicly announced capacity programs (Hitachi Energy, Hyundai HI, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Electric, MEPPI, plus Prolec at GEV). The implication for FY26-FY30 pricing power is meaningful: incremental LPT pricing rises ~MSD-HSD/yr through FY28 and only flattens by FY29-FY30.^[inferred]

### SMR tax credits + DOE LPO
The IRA/IIJA-era nuclear PTC, ITC, and DOE Loan Programs Office support remain substantively in place at the 2026 baseline. ^[extracted] Through FY30, the policy backdrop for first-of-a-kind SMR commercial deployments is supportive — though execution risk (NRC, supply chain, cost overruns) is the binding constraint, not policy. By FY30 there is plausibly **2-4 BWRX-300 units under construction globally** ^[inferred], with parts/fuel/services revenue scaling thereafter.

### Offshore wind reset
Offshore wind globally has been reset by tariffs, interest rates, and project economics. By FY27-FY28, the surviving project pipeline is smaller, more disciplined, and at higher tariffs. GEV's Haliade-X book is being executed-down rather than rebid; new Offshore order intake is muted. Net implication: Wind segment losses narrow not because Offshore *grows* but because the loss-making book runs off and Onshore + services stabilize.

### Nuclear services compounding
US nuclear fleet uprate and license-extension activity, plus international BWR services, drive Nuclear Services revenue ~5-7% CAGR through FY30 at high-teens to low-20s segment margin.^[inferred] This is a quiet, durable contributor that does not require any SMR success to compound.

### Cross-thesis demand frame
- **IEA / DOE outlook**: incremental ~80-120 GW of US gas-fired capacity additions through 2030 driven by data-center load and coal retirements.^[inferred from trade-press synthesis cited in [[us-power-region-deep-dive]]]
- **Wood Mackenzie**: ~600 GW of US data-center plans, ~183 GW with utility supply agreements as of late 2025.^[extracted via trade press, see [[electrical-equipment-deep-dive]]]
- **US grid investment**: $2-3T cumulative through 2035 across T&D, generation, and behind-the-meter ^[inferred industry-consensus range]
- **Global HVDC**: $300-500B cumulative through 2035 across Europe, China, India, US.^[inferred]

## 10. Bear case — 5-year structural risks

The bear case has six components for the 5-year horizon, in order of decreasing probability and severity:

1. **Wind further impairments through FY27 (P ~30%)**. Despite the in-process restructuring, an additional **$1-3B of Wind goodwill and asset impairments** could land across FY27-FY28 if Offshore project losses extend or Onshore demand stays soft. The drag is non-cash but optically painful and would compress reported EBITDA margin and force a multi-quarter "show-me" period. The 5-year cash trajectory normalizes regardless, but the equity narrative could be set back 12-18 months.

2. **Gas-turbine pricing rolls in FY28-FY30 (P ~20%)**. The market has priced in steady Gas Power margin expansion through FY30. If **all three OEMs** materially expand capacity (Mitsubishi 2x, Siemens Energy +30-50%, GEV +50-100% per the Greenville trajectory) and hyperscaler load decelerates, slot pricing could **flatten by FY28 and erode 5-10% by FY30**. The 12-month version of this risk is "one bad quarter"; the 5-year version is "structural pricing topping" that compresses Power-segment forward EBITDA from $6.5B to $5B and the segment multiple from 20x to 14-16x.

3. **SMR delays push commercial revenue to post-FY30 (P ~25%)**. NRC delays, supply-chain bottlenecks at forging vendors, or first-of-a-kind cost overruns at OPG Darlington could push BWRX-300 commercial revenue from FY29-FY30 to FY32+. The 5-year option value compresses but does not zero out — SMR remains a real long-dated asset, just a later one. Equity impact is principally on the option value embedded in the sum-of-parts ($5-15B), not on cash-generating segments.

4. **Electrification multiple compression (P ~20%)**. If transformer market eases (e.g., GOES electrical-steel capacity catches up faster than expected, or Asian OEMs accelerate import substitution into the US), Hubbell/Eaton/GEV-Electrification multiples compress 3-5 turns from FY28-FY30. Prolec is the most exposed sub-business. By FY30 base-case Electrification EV of $94B (incl Prolec), a 4-turn compression is ~$15B of equity value.

5. **Prolec integration risks (P ~15%)**. $5.275B acquisitions integrate well or they don't. 5-year integration risks include: cross-border Mexico labor/legal complexity, IT integration, salesforce conflicts with existing GEV grid-solutions reps, accounting harmonization, and inability to capture pricing power as a captive supplier vs an arms-length JV. Worst-case scenario: Prolec runs at the lower end of expected economics through FY30 ($600-800M EBITDA vs $1.0-1.3B base).

6. **Capital allocation discipline slips (P ~10%)**. The 5-year case rests on management returning ~1/3 of cash generation to shareholders. If management instead pivots to large-scale transformative M&A (e.g., a multi-billion-dollar BESS or grid-digital acquisition at peak cycle multiples), the 5-year IRR compresses meaningfully. Strazik's track record to date is conservative — Prolec was a known-entity bolt-on, not a transformation — but capital-allocation discipline at peak cycle is always a watch item.

### Combined bear-case quantification

If three or four of the above land within the 5-year horizon (e.g., Wind impairment + Gas Power FY28 pricing roll + SMR delay + Electrification multiple compression), FY30 EBITDA estimates roll to $9.3B and forward multiples compress to ~14x blended, implying **~$500/share** vs current $1,069. That is a -53% terminal capital outcome plus ~7-8% cumulative dividends + buyback, for a -12%/yr 5-year IRR. Probability of that compound bear: ~10-15%, but the asymmetry warrants the position-sizing discipline below.

### 5-year tripwires

Watch list (cross-referenced in [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]):
- Two consecutive years of Power-segment EBITDA margin decline from peak
- Hyperscaler capex guidance cuts of >15% across two or more of the top 5 in a single fiscal year
- Wind segment cash burn worse than guided for two consecutive years
- Prolec working-capital deterioration or margin disappointment in Q2/Q3 2026
- A Mitsubishi or Siemens Energy slot-pricing disclosure that contradicts GEV's pricing narrative (e.g., a peer cutting per-kW pricing 5%+)
- BWRX-300 NRC docket setback at TVA Clinch River
- Any large transformative M&A announcement above $5B at peak-cycle multiples

## 11. Position-sizing notes — 5-year

GEV is a **large-cap volatile growth name**. Trailing 1-yr beta is approximately **1.2-1.4**.^[inferred] At a ~$287B market cap it is one of the largest pure-play AI-power-infrastructure names available on US exchanges, with ample liquidity (~$2B/day notional turnover).^[inferred]

### Basket construction — 5-year

GEV occupies the **center** of the long-duration electrical-equipment / power-infra basket:

- **GEV** — power generation OEM + grid + transformers + nuclear option; AI-DC primary beneficiary; basket cornerstone.
- **ETN** ([[pick-eaton-etn-5y]]) — broad electrical-diversified anchor; lower beta, full-stack exposure to data-center electrical content and reshoring.
- **POWL** (Powell Industries; small-cap MV-switchgear leverage to the same thesis; 12-month and 5-year companions in the picks list) — small-cap convexity to incremental DC backlog.
- **CAT** ([[pick-caterpillar-cat-5y]]) — back-up power gensets and reciprocating engines for behind-the-meter DC power; correlated demand driver but lower margin.
- **CEG** ([[pick-constellation-energy-ceg-5y]]) — nuclear utility complement to GEV's nuclear-services franchise; PPA-side exposure to hyperscaler nuclear demand.

A balanced 5-year basket (e.g., **35% GEV / 25% ETN / 15% POWL / 15% CAT / 10% CEG**) gives:
- Generation OEM + T&D + datacenter electrical + reciprocating engines + nuclear PPA exposure.
- Mix of large-cap stability and small-cap convexity.
- Multiple-expansion upside in the small-cap component if hyperscaler capex re-accelerates.
- Diversification across the gas / transformer / nuclear demand levers.

### Sizing discipline (5-year)

- **Sleeve weight**: GEV at ~30-35% of an electrical-equipment / power-infra sleeve; ~5-8% of total-portfolio book at conviction-high.^[inferred guidance]
- **Stop-loss / re-evaluation triggers**: violation of any two of the 5-year tripwires above; any year with two consecutive quarters of Power-segment margin compression without explicit one-time explanation; an SMR setback at TVA combined with an OPG Darlington schedule slip.
- **Hedging**: pair against an index short on rate-sensitivity events; consider a tactical TLT/utility-ETF long if rates spike; consider partial hedge on the gas-turbine demand cycle via a paired short on a higher-beta DC-power name in 2028-2029 if pricing-roll signals appear.
- **Re-balancing cadence**: trim to sleeve weight at each 25% absolute appreciation; add at each 15% drawdown to the sleeve weight, given the long-duration thesis.
