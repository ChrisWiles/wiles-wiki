A post-AGI economy should be understood as a set of scenarios, not a single forecast. The central question is whether advanced AI remains a powerful complement to human labor or becomes a broad substitute for labor across cognitive, administrative, creative, scientific, and eventually physical work.

If labor stops being the main scarce input, the core economic problem changes. Instead of asking mainly how people earn wages, society has to ask how people obtain claims on output, [[ai-capital-ownership-and-purchasing-power|capital]], compute, land, housing, energy, and other scarce resources.

## Basic Shift

Today's household purchasing power mostly comes from wages. Firms own productive assets, hire labor, pay wages, sell goods and services, and households use wage income to buy those goods and services. Capital owners receive profits, rents, interest, and capital gains.

AGI strains that structure if firms can produce more output with less human labor. Productivity can rise while income shifts from labor to capital. Markets may still allocate scarce goods through prices, profits, competition, and consumer choice, but markets alone may no longer distribute income broadly if most people cannot sell economically valuable labor.

Acemoglu and Restrepo's task model is the clean theoretical frame: automation displaces labor from tasks and can reduce labor share, while new labor-using tasks can reinstate labor demand. The post-AGI risk is that AI may also perform many newly created tasks, weakening reinstatement. ^[inferred]

The main scarce inputs would likely shift from ordinary labor to capital, compute, energy, chips, robotics, land, data, intellectual property, brand trust, regulatory permissions, and security.

## Why Abundance May Not Be Enough

Abundance alone does not guarantee broad prosperity. If production becomes dramatically cheaper but productive assets are owned by a small set of firms, investors, or states, purchasing power can concentrate even as total output rises.

This is why "AI makes many things cheap" and "AI concentrates power" can both be true. Digital services, tutoring, coding, writing, and entertainment may become cheaper while housing, land, energy, healthcare capacity, legal permissions, and positional goods remain scarce.

## Institutional Requirement

A stable post-labor economy likely requires some combination of:

- Broad capital ownership
- Public or shared AI infrastructure
- Direct income support
- Universal services
- Taxation of AI-related rents
- Competition and interoperability policy
- New institutions for meaning, status, and social contribution

The deepest shift is from an employment-centered welfare state toward a prosperity state built around ownership, access, services, and democratic control over highly productive capital.
