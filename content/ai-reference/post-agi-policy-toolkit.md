A stable [[post-agi-economy|post-AGI economy]] likely needs a portfolio of policies rather than a single instrument. The core policy goal is to distribute AI-era prosperity at the level of capital, compute, services, and governance, not only through retraining for the next job.

## Universal Basic Income

UBI is viable as a floor, but probably not sufficient as the whole settlement. It is administratively simple, universal, dignified, and useful for demand support during volatile transitions.

Its limits are fiscal scale, inflation risk in bottleneck sectors, and failure to address ownership, political power, status, or purpose. If housing, healthcare, energy, or education remain supply-constrained, cash transfers can bid up prices unless paired with supply expansion or public provision.

The [OpenResearch unconditional cash study](https://www.openresearchlab.org/projects/unconditional-cash-study) is useful evidence for cash transfers, not a full test of post-AGI UBI. It found increased flexibility, some education and entrepreneurship signals, and modest reductions in employment and hours.

## Wage Support

Negative income taxes, wage subsidies, wage insurance, and portable benefits are useful if many people still work but earnings become volatile or low. They preserve some work incentives and smooth transition.

They become less central if wages stop being the main income source.

OpenAI's industrial policy paper proposes adaptive safety nets that scale expanded unemployment benefits, fast cash assistance, wage insurance, and training vouchers when real-time displacement metrics cross thresholds.

Anthropic's policy-response post discusses AI adjustment assistance modeled on trade adjustment assistance, potentially funded by taxes on AI-driven revenues from large firms.

## Sovereign Wealth Funds And Citizen Dividends

Public wealth funds, sovereign wealth funds, and citizen-capital funds directly address the capital ownership problem. They can convert AI rents into broad citizen income and political buy-in.

Risks include governance failure, underfunding, capture by incumbent firms, and national inequality if only wealthy countries can build them.

OpenAI proposes a Public Wealth Fund that would give every citizen, including people outside financial markets, a stake in AI-driven growth. Anthropic's fast-moving scenario policy menu also includes sovereign wealth funds with stakes in AI.

## Public Or Shared AI Infrastructure

Public or shared ownership of AI infrastructure could include public stakes in data centers, compute clouds, energy assets, public-interest models, or chip supply chains.

The advantage is that society captures upside at the asset level. The danger is politicized allocation, slow bureaucracy, or dependence on a few private contractors.

## Universal Basic Compute

Universal basic compute gives people direct access to productive AI capability rather than only money. It could help entrepreneurs, students, artists, researchers, local governments, and nonprofits.

Compute access is not equally valuable to everyone unless paired with education, interfaces, tools, marketplaces, safety rules, and abuse controls.

OpenAI's "right to AI" framing is adjacent to universal basic compute: affordable baseline access to foundational models plus education, connectivity, and training so workers, small businesses, schools, libraries, and underserved communities are not excluded.

## AI-Related Taxes

Possible tax instruments include robot taxes, automation taxes, compute taxes, token taxes, digital services taxes, VATs, business wealth taxes, windfall taxes, and direct taxation of AGI capital.

Design matters. Korinek and Lockwood distinguish early-stage taxes on final AI services, such as token, robot-service, and digital-service taxes, from later-stage taxes on AGI capital, compute, or robots. Taxes on productive inputs can discourage investment before capital itself becomes the appropriate tax base.

## Universal Services

Universal healthcare, housing support, education, transport, child care, elder care, and AI tutoring reduce dependence on cash for essentials. This matters when scarce sectors remain expensive even as many digital goods get cheaper.

## Competition And Interoperability

Antitrust, interoperability rules, open standards, data portability, public procurement, and limits on exclusive infrastructure control reduce the risk that AGI rents become permanent oligopoly rents.

## Civic Contribution And Status

Paid work provides income, time structure, identity, status, social networks, and contribution. A post-labor economy must replace more than wages.

Institutions may need to create respected forms of contribution outside the labor market, such as civic service, local governance, caregiving credentials, arts and research grants, community awards, public-interest entrepreneurship, and democratic participation in AI governance.
