## 1. Investment thesis (5 bullets)

1. **1.6T transceiver supercycle is just starting and Coherent has the EML.** Q2 FY26 (Feb 4 reported) had datacom +36% Y/Y, datacenter book-to-bill >4x, with bookings extending into calendar 2027 and "some customers' forecasts into 2028." 1.6T volume ramps in 2H CY26 on EML and SiPh platforms, then VCSEL variants follow. Coherent's six-inch InP wafer line in Sherman, TX (and Järfälla, Sweden) hit "80% of target to double internal capacity by year-end" with wafer starts up >4x sequentially in Q2 — this is the key gating resource for the entire 1.6T pluggable market.

2. **Networking gross-margin recovery is materially in front of us.** Non-GAAP GM was 39.0% in Q2 FY26 vs. 37.9% FY25 vs. ~34% pre-restructuring. The Datacom mix shift, six-inch yield ramp, and the Munich product-division divestiture (announced as immediately accretive to GM and EPS) all push toward management's stated ~42–45% non-GAAP GM long-term goal. Each 100 bps of GM at $7B+ run-rate revenue is ~$70M of incremental gross profit — high incremental flow-through given OpEx is roughly fixed.

3. **EML supply scarcity is a real moat for at least 18 months.** Coherent vs. Lumentum, Sumitomo, and Mitsubishi: COHR is alone in transitioning EML/CW/photodiode production to six-inch InP at scale (LITE is "still racing to match," per OFC trade press). EML is the gating component for 200G-per-lane optics — the building block for both 1.6T and 3.2T. Bookings visibility into 2028 is direct evidence the supply imbalance is being priced in by hyperscaler customers locking allocation.

4. **Silicon photonics + OCS + CPO are option value, not in the price.** Coherent has 10+ OCS customer engagements (backlog skewed to 320×320), an "exceptionally large" CPO purchase order from a market-leading AI customer with first revenue in late CY26, and a credible 3.2T pluggable demo at OFC 2026. Even if pluggable EML stays dominant, Coherent participates in the CPO step-up; if CPO accelerates, Coherent's SiPh PIC capability is a hedge rather than a stranded asset.

5. **Deleveraging + Bain overhang clearing = re-rating fuel.** Net leverage stepped down from 2.3x to 1.7x in 4 quarters; total debt ~$3.2B (down from a $4.2B peak post-merger); Bain's Series B preferred has begun converting (7.75M shares converted Nov 2025) and Bain has been monetizing common stock in $1B+ blocks. Each block sale removes a known supply overhang. Combined with the Munich divestiture and aerospace/defense closure, the balance sheet and shareholder register are cleaner than at any point since the II-VI/Coherent merger.

## 2. Business overview

Coherent Corp. (COHR), formerly II-VI Incorporated, is a vertically integrated photonics & compound-semiconductor company headquartered in Saxonburg, PA. The company **rebranded its segments in FY2026** from the legacy three-segment structure (Networking / Materials / Lasers) to a two-segment structure:

| Segment (FY26) | Legacy mapping | What's in it | Q2 FY26 mix |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Datacenter & Communications (DC&C)** | Old "Networking" | Datacom transceivers (800G/1.6T pluggables, EML/SiPh/VCSEL), telecom (ROADM, coherent DSP optics), OCS, CPO modules | >70% of revenue ^[inferred from Q2 transcript: "Data Center & Communications now over 70%"] |
| **Industrial** | Old "Materials" + "Lasers" | Compound-semi materials (SiC, ceramics), industrial lasers, semicap, defense (residual after divestiture), life sciences | ~30% of revenue |

The two-segment structure simplifies the SOTP (no more arguing whether Materials should be valued like an industrial or a semi) and makes the AI-DC exposure cleaner.

**Customer concentration (Networking / DC&C).** Direct module/component customers and design-in OEMs include Cisco, Arista, Juniper, Nvidia (transceivers shipped with GB200/GB300 reference designs), Broadcom (EML supply for Tomahawk-based switch ecosystems), AMD, plus the hyperscaler self-builds (Microsoft Azure, Google, Meta, AWS, Oracle). Coherent has not disclosed exact 10%+ customer names in the most recent 10-Q; the FY25 10-K noted no single customer >10% on a consolidated basis but Networking-segment concentration is materially higher ^[inferred].

**Key facilities.** Sherman, TX (six-inch InP fab — strategic asset); Järfälla, Sweden (six-inch InP); Pittsburgh / Saxonburg, PA (corporate + SiC); Newton Aycliffe, UK; Mexico (transceiver assembly); Vietnam, Thailand, China (assembly via Fabrinet-style partners). The site footprint has been actively rationalized — 33 sites exited over six quarters per Q2 FY26 commentary.

## 3. AI buildout exposure

Datacom inside DC&C is the AI growth engine. Working from the disclosed numbers:

- Q1 FY26 DC&C: **$1.09B**, of which datacenter was +23% Y/Y and communications +55% Y/Y.
- Q2 FY26: DC&C grew +11% Q/Q and +34% Y/Y, with datacenter specifically +14% Q/Q and +36% Y/Y.
- Implied Q2 FY26 DC&C revenue: ~$1.21B ^[inferred from Q1 base and disclosed sequential growth].
- Q2 FY26 total revenue: $1.69B → DC&C is ~72% of company revenue.
- Within DC&C, datacom (AI-DC) is ~75–80% with telecom the balance ^[inferred; management calls out datacenter as the dominant growth driver but does not break out a percentage].

**Net AI-DC exposure.** Roughly **55–58% of total company revenue is AI-datacenter levered today**, up from ~35–40% at the time of the II-VI/Coherent merger close (CY23). On the 12-month forward basis, with DC&C compounding faster than Industrial, AI-DC exposure is heading toward ~60–65% of revenue ^[inferred]. This is more concentrated AI exposure than Lumentum (which has heavier telecom/3D-sensing mix) and less than Credo (CRDO, which is essentially a pure AI-DC name).

## 5. Supply / capacity

**Six-inch InP transition is the moat.**
- Production live at Sherman, TX and Järfälla, Sweden as of Sep 2025.
- Three components in production on six-inch: EMLs, CW lasers, photodiodes.
- Initial six-inch yields are reported **higher** than mature three-inch yields — non-trivial, this almost never happens in a wafer-size step.
- Q2 FY26: 80% of target to double total internal InP capacity by end of CY26; wafer starts >4x'd Q1→Q2.
- Capex Q2 FY26 $154M (up from $104M Q1) — the spend is being absorbed without breaking the deleveraging curve, which is the bull tell.

**Lead times.** Pluggable optics lead times are reported elsewhere in the channel at 40+ weeks for 800G/1.6T; Coherent's bookings into 2027/28 are consistent with that. EML chip allocations specifically are constrained industry-wide.

**Gross margin trajectory.** Networking/DC&C non-GAAP GM was depressed at ~30–35% during the II-VI integration. Company-level non-GAAP GM trajectory:

| Period | Non-GAAP GM |
|---|---|
| FY24 | 34.3% |
| FY25 | 37.9% |
| Q1 FY26 | 38.7% |
| Q2 FY26 | 39.0% |
| Q3 FY26 guide | 38.5–40.5% |
| Long-term target | 42–45% ^[management] |

Six-inch InP yield maturation, Munich divestiture (closing in CY26), datacom mix shift, and ongoing site rationalization (33 sites already exited) should put 41% on the table within 12 months and 42–43% within 18–24 months ^[inferred].

## 9. Bear case

1. **1.6T pluggable ramp slips 2 quarters.** Hyperscaler capex digestion (already a debate after Q1 CY26) could push 1.6T deployments from late CY26 to early CY27. DC&C sequential growth slows to mid-single-digits. Multiple compresses fast — GM doesn't expand as quickly because the favorable mix shift is delayed.

2. **CPO displaces pluggables faster than expected.** Microsoft has publicly pushed CPO; Broadcom Tomahawk-CPO ships in volume in CY26/27. If hyperscalers shift sooner than the consensus pluggable-then-CPO sequencing, Coherent's pluggable revenue base shrinks 12–18 months before CPO can fill it. Coherent participates in CPO but at lower content per port and less established margin.

3. **EML competition normalizes.** Lumentum is targeting six-inch InP; Sumitomo and Mitsubishi continue to expand. By late CY27, EML supply could swing from constrained to balanced, capping ASPs and allocation premiums. The "capacity moat" is a 6–18 month story, not a 5-year story.

4. **Hyperscaler DSP/optics insourcing.** Microsoft Azure's silicon photonics work, Broadcom's vertical integration via Tomahawk-CPO, Marvell's optical DSP roadmap — all directionally compress the merchant transceiver TAM at the high end. Coherent's component (laser/EML/PIC) position is more defensible than the module position.

5. **Industrial cyclical roll.** Semicap is volatile; SiC pricing has been weak through CY25/26 (consumer-EV pull-through softer than expected). Industrial segment could go from flat to -10% Y/Y if global manufacturing PMIs weaken, dragging consolidated GM and operating leverage.

6. **Supply-chain or yield disappointment at six-inch.** Yield optimism has been a bull narrative; any wafer-scale step is operationally risky. A bad quarter on yield or a Sherman/Järfälla disruption would undo the GM-expansion thesis quickly.

**Bear-case 12-month price target: ~$185** (40% downside).

## 10. Position-sizing notes

- **Mid-cap, β ≈ 1.4–1.5.** Higher than peer LITE (β ~1.3) and CIEN (~1.1); similar to CRDO (~1.5) and FN (~1.4).
- **Volatility**: implied vol regularly prints in the 50–60 range around earnings. The stock fell 4.3% on 27-Apr-26 with no specific news — the AI-DC complex trades together. A 1% S&P move can move COHR 1.5–2%.
- **Correlations** (rolling 90-day, ^[inferred]): COHR–LITE ~0.85, COHR–CRDO ~0.75, COHR–AVGO ~0.65, COHR–FN ~0.70. **Diversification within an AI-DC sleeve is limited** — sizing should treat COHR + LITE + CRDO + FN as a single thematic exposure.
- **COHR vs. LITE.** Coherent has more EML/InP capacity (six-inch transition), more diverse end-markets (Industrial provides cyclical offset), larger absolute revenue base ($7B vs. ~$2.5B run-rate ^[inferred]). LITE is more telecom-mixed and historically more 3D-sensing exposed (Apple). LITE has run further on 12-month basis (~+150% vs. COHR ~+165%). Spread trade (long COHR / short LITE) has worked over the past 6 months but the convergence is largely played out — pair exposure is now closer to even.
- **Suggested sizing** (Chris's framework — see [[ai-investment-public-company-scorecard]]): 3–5% of an AI-infrastructure sleeve as a high-conviction core holding; do not exceed 7% absent a sub-$250 entry. Consider averaging in across earnings prints rather than single-shot entry given the compressed risk/reward at current price.
- **Hedge candidates**: short LITE for a within-optics pair; long XLK puts for systemic AI-capex hedge; the cheapest hedge is sizing discipline.
