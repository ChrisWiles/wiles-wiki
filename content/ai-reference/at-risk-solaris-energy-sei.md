## Header block

- **Ticker:** SEI (NYSE) — Solaris Energy Infrastructure, Inc. (formerly Solaris Oilfield Infrastructure, rebranded after Solaris Power Solutions acquisition).
- **Reference price:** ~$32 / share (close 2026-04-29). ^[extracted]
- **Q1 2026 print (reported 2026-04-27):** management reiterated 2 GW+ of contracted power-solutions capacity across signed long-term lease and service agreements, with incremental customer expansion into the AI-data-center vertical. ^[extracted]
- **Recommendation:** avoid / underweight, qualified. Conviction medium. The stock is a legitimate way to express the speed-to-power thesis, and SEI is a holding inside [[situational-awareness-ai-infrastructure-portfolio|Situational Awareness LP's]] disclosed book — but the rerating since the Solaris Power Solutions deal already prices in flawless execution, while the underlying business model is more fragile than the headline backlog suggests.

## What makes the long case popular

The bull narrative is genuinely strong, which is why this page is "avoid", not "short":

- **2 GW+ contracted backlog with high-quality counterparties.** Q1 2026 disclosures point to roughly 2 GW+ of contracted mobile / behind-the-meter power capacity, with customers anchored in investment-grade global tech and hyperscale data-center operators. ^[extracted] That is a step-function from the legacy oilfield-services revenue base.
- **Behind-the-meter speed-to-power.** SEI's gas-turbine and modular power packages let a hyperscaler or AI training campus skip the multi-year interconnection queue (see [[us-power-region-deep-dive]] for ERCOT / PJM queue dynamics). In a world where the binding constraint is power, not silicon, that optionality is real.
- **Situational Awareness LP exposure.** SEI shows up alongside Bloom Energy, EQT, Power Solutions International, Liberty Energy, and other speed-to-power names in the Aschenbrenner LP disclosed portfolio (see [[situational-awareness-ai-infrastructure-portfolio]]). For investors who treat that portfolio as a thematic signal, SEI is part of the same thesis cluster.
- **High-beta optionality.** The combination of long-duration take-or-pay contracts, asset-heavy growth, and a small float makes SEI mechanically high-beta to the AI infrastructure trade. If hyperscaler power capex steps up another leg, SEI's revenue and EBITDA could compound faster than peer fabless or services-asset-light names.
- **Cross-sell into oilfield base.** The legacy logistics franchise (sand silos, top-fill, chemical systems) gives SEI a pre-existing field organization, leasing infrastructure, and customer relationships in the Permian and Haynesville — useful adjacencies for distributed power.

These are the reasons the stock has rerated. None of them are wrong. The bear case is that the rerating itself has eaten the margin of safety.

## Bear case (5 reasons)

### 1. Customer concentration risk

The 2 GW+ figure is impressive, but it sits across a small number of investment-grade counterparties on the BTM gas + power-solutions side. ^[inferred] The 10-K customer-concentration disclosures historically showed top-3 customers driving a meaningful majority of revenue in the legacy oilfield segment, and the new power-solutions book is, if anything, more concentrated because the addressable customer set (hyperscalers + a handful of co-locators + a few neoclouds — see [[neocloud-and-power-first-operator-diligence]]) is structurally narrow. ^[ambiguous] One renegotiation, one project deferral, or one customer in-sourcing decision can move the multi-year revenue forecast by 10-25%.

### 2. Capital intensity: deploy-then-monetize requires balance-sheet leverage

Mobile gas turbines and modular generation packages are real, expensive, long-lead-time assets. SEI's growth model is closer to a leasing company than a pure services franchise: you buy the iron, deploy it under a multi-year contract, and recover capital over the contract life. That requires either:

- Increasing net debt (and accepting covenant constraints — see filings for revolver and term-loan covenants), or
- Equity issuance at a moment when the market may have already paid for the growth.

Either path compresses returns to existing equity holders if execution slips, lead times stretch, or customer ramps are pushed right. Compare to a capital-light backlog story like Vertiv (see [[pick-vertiv-vrt]]): VRT's working capital scales with the order book; SEI's gross PP&E scales with the order book.

### 3. Behind-the-meter normalization

The BTM thesis is strongest when grid power is unavailable, slow, or expensive. As ERCOT and PJM clear capacity auctions higher (PJM 2025/26 BRA cleared at ~$269.92/MW-day; ERCOT continues to refine ORDC and reliability mechanisms), grid-delivered power becomes a more credible alternative for hyperscalers willing to wait an extra 12-24 months. ^[extracted] The "speed premium" SEI is paid is a function of the spread between BTM cost-of-power and grid-delivered cost-of-power *plus* time-value-of-power. If grid clears higher, that spread compresses on the cost side, and if interconnect queues unstick (FERC Order 2023 implementation, regional reforms), the time premium also compresses. SEI is short both legs of that spread.

### 4. Lease vs purchase economics — customer can flip to direct ownership

Hyperscalers have balance sheets larger than every IPP in North America combined. Once a hyperscaler has lived with SEI's fleet for 18-36 months, has operational data, has identified preferred OEMs (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Solar Turbines, Mitsubishi Power), and has internalized siting / permitting expertise, the natural next step is to buy the turbines directly and let SEI keep only the O&M wrap — or skip SEI altogether on the next campus. The customer is structurally short capital cost and long control; SEI's lease economics depend on the customer choosing *not* to vertically integrate. The longer the contracts run, the more attractive the buy-vs-lease math becomes for the customer.

### 5. The recent rerating means execution is priced in

SEI's multiple has expanded materially since the Solaris Power Solutions transaction and the AI-data-center pivot was articulated. ^[inferred] At current prices, the stock is implicitly assuming:

- Backlog converts to revenue on schedule (no major project slips).
- Gross margins on the power book hold or expand (despite turbine OEM pricing power).
- The next 2-3 GW of incremental contracts come at terms similar to the first 2 GW.
- No material customer renegotiation or churn.

Any one of those slipping — and all four are credible — leaves a stock that has already paid for the upside. This is the classic "right thesis, wrong entry price" risk, which is why the call is *avoid / underweight* rather than *short*.

## What would force a re-rating downward

These are the tripwires to monitor — they correspond to entries in [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]:

- **Major customer renegotiation or deferral.** Watch for 8-K disclosures of contract amendments, deployment schedule revisions, or customer-side change orders. A single anchor customer pushing 12 months right would meaningfully reset the NPV.
- **Gas-turbine lead times shorten.** Today's bull case partly rests on 24-36 month OEM lead times for aero-derivative and frame turbines. If GE Vernova (see [[pick-ge-vernova-gev]]), Siemens Energy, and Mitsubishi Power expand capacity faster than expected, hyperscalers can buy direct and SEI's scarcity premium compresses.
- **BTM regulatory restrictions.** State-level pushback on hyperscaler BTM gas (Virginia, Texas, Ohio, Arizona) — air permits, methane rules, environmental-justice siting reviews — could slow new deployments or restrict siting. ERCOT-side rule changes on BTM interconnection or load registration would also bite.
- **Capacity-auction prices roll lower.** If PJM 2026/27 or 2027/28 BRAs clear meaningfully below the 2025/26 print, or if ERCOT reserve margins improve, the case for paying SEI's premium for speed weakens.
- **Equity issuance to fund growth capex.** A secondary offering — especially at a discount — would confirm the capital-intensity bear case and reset multiple-of-EBITDA expectations.

## What would invalidate the avoid call

This is the steel-man — the conditions under which I would flip:

- **Customer diversification.** New disclosed contracts with a third or fourth distinct hyperscaler / global-tech counterparty, ideally with at least one credit-enhanced or guaranteed by a parent investment-grade entity, would reduce concentration risk to the point where the lease-company multiple is defensible.
- **Long-duration leases with takeout protection.** If SEI signs 7-10 year leases with hard make-whole / termination payments or asset-puts back to the customer at fair value, the deploy-then-monetize risk gets transferred away from the SEI balance sheet.
- **Sustained gross-margin expansion.** Three or more quarters of power-segment gross margin expanding (rather than holding) — implying real pricing power against turbine OEMs and not just operating leverage on a fixed customer slate.
- **Capital-light model evolution.** If SEI moves toward an O&M / orchestration / asset-light wrap (where the customer or a finance partner owns the iron and SEI runs it), the multiple deserves to rerate up, not down. Watch for JV announcements with infrastructure funds, IPPs, or YieldCos.
- **Grid stays broken longer than expected.** If FERC Order 2023 implementation slips, if interconnection queues do not unstick by 2027, and if PJM/ERCOT capacity prices stay at or above current strip, the BTM premium persists and SEI's contracted backlog converts at full economics.

## Position-sizing implication

For a portfolio expressing the [[ai-data-center-buildout-investment-memo|AI data center buildout thesis]], I would not own SEI at the current price as a standalone expression of speed-to-power. Cleaner alternatives:

- **Power equipment with broader customer set:** [[pick-eaton-etn-5y|ETN]], [[pick-vertiv-vrt-5y|VRT]], [[pick-ge-vernova-gev|GEV]], [[pick-powell-industries-powl|POWL]].
- **Power generation / utility-scale:** [[pick-constellation-energy-ceg|CEG]].
- **Speed-to-power with optionality but better diversification:** broader EPC / mechanical names like [[pick-quanta-services-pwr|PWR]] and [[pick-comfort-systems-fix|FIX]].

If forced to own SEI, size it as a high-beta call-option — single-digit % of an AI-infra sleeve — and pair with a longer-duration, more-diversified anchor.

## Aschenbrenner LP caveat

SEI is in the [[situational-awareness-ai-infrastructure-portfolio|Situational Awareness LP disclosed book]]. That is a thoughtful signal and one of the better-curated "AI infrastructure as scarce resource" portfolios in public 13F land. Two reasons that does not flip this call:

1. The LP's book is thematic, not price-sensitive. A 13F snapshot does not tell you sizing logic, hedges, options overlays, or entry price. Following it as a list is not the same as following it as a strategy.
2. The portfolio holds *both* SEI and Bloom Energy (see [[at-risk-bloom-energy-be]] for the parallel risk write-up). Owning the basket diversifies single-name customer-concentration risk in a way that holding SEI alone does not.

The right read is: the *theme* is real (Aschenbrenner is right that power is the binding constraint), but the *single-name expression* of that theme via SEI at current prices carries asymmetric downside.
