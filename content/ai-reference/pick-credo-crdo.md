## 1. Investment thesis

1. **AEC ramp is still in inning two.** Credo invented the Active Electrical Cable (AEC) category and remains the volume leader. AECs displace passive DACs above 800G because the reach/SerDes physics of plain copper run out around 2-3m at 200G/lane, and they displace short-reach optics on cost, power, and reliability. LightCounting / Credo's own numbers put the AEC TAM at ~$4B by 2028 vs. a SAM that was effectively zero in 2022. Credo enters that TAM with the only volume-shipped 1.6T ZeroFlap AECs and a multi-year head start in field-proven reliability data. ^[extracted/inferred]
2. **Customer concentration is structurally improving in real time.** Top-1 customer was 67% of FY25 revenue (a single hyperscaler — widely understood to be Amazon). In Q3 FY26 the top three customers were 39% / 32% / 17% — three hyperscalers each >10%, and management says a 5th hyperscaler is now ramping. The "hostage to one buyer" narrative that capped the multiple in 2024 is being retired quarter by quarter. ^[extracted]
3. **200G/lane DSP cycle is a second growth leg.** Credo's 3nm Bluebird optical DSP at 224 Gb/s/lane (1.6T modules) and 100G/lane retimers are now in volume. The 800G optical DSP design win disclosed in FY25 (a "largest ever" cited by management) is in production ramp through FY26-27, and 1.6T design-win count is growing. Optical is targeted at >$500M in FY27 alone, post-DustPhotonics. ^[extracted]
4. **Optical platform expansion via DustPhotonics is a credible CPO hedge.** The April 2026 acquisition (up to $1.3B; $750M cash + ~0.92M shares + up to ~3.21M earnout shares) buys vertically integrated silicon photonics PIC IP across 400G/800G/1.6T with a 3.2T roadmap. This converts Credo from an AEC house with a DSP add-on into a SerDes + DSP + SiPho + system-integration stack — exactly the surface area that survives whether the next-gen scale-out fabric is copper-attached, pluggable optics, linear-drive, or co-packaged. ^[extracted]
5. **Best-in-class margin structure in connectivity silicon.** Q3 FY26 GAAP gross margin 68.5% / non-GAAP 68.6%; management's long-term framework is 63-65% (with mix tilting toward higher-volume AEC/optical product, partly offset by IP licensing decline). Non-GAAP operating margin in Q3 was ~50%; non-GAAP net margin ~51%. This is the tape of a company that has fixed-cost leverage running at the same time revenue is doubling. ^[extracted]

## 2. Business overview

### What Credo sells

Credo is a fabless connectivity-silicon company built around a proprietary high-speed SerDes IP core. It monetizes the SerDes through five product families:

- **Active Electrical Cables (AECs).** The category Credo pioneered. A copper cable with a Credo SerDes/equalizer/retimer at each end — ~50-60% of revenue currently; the largest single contributor to the FY25→FY26 explosion. Use cases: rack-to-rack, ToR-to-NIC, and increasingly switch-to-switch at 800G and 1.6T where pure copper has run out of reach. Microsoft's HiWire dual-ToR architecture is the flagship reference design; Amazon, Meta, Google, Tesla, Oracle, and xAI are all in the customer book. ^[extracted]
- **Optical DSPs.** 50G/100G/200G-per-lane PAM4 DSPs that drive pluggable optical modules (400G, 800G, 1.6T). The 1.6T Bluebird family is in volume. Customers are both hyperscalers (direct module-design relationships) and module makers. ^[extracted]
- **Line-card / chip-on-board retimers.** 100G/lane and 200G/lane retimers used inside switches and AI servers. Direct competition with Astera Labs' Aries family. ^[extracted]
- **SerDes IP licensing.** Licenses Credo's core SerDes IP into customer SoCs. Historically a meaningful but declining mix item; Credo has shifted toward selling silicon rather than IP because product margins are higher. ^[extracted]
- **Optical transceivers (ZeroFlap) + SiPho (DustPhotonics).** Newest expansion: full pluggable transceivers (rather than just the DSP inside someone else's transceiver) and, from H2 calendar 2026, integrated silicon photonics PICs.

### Customer mix (Q3 FY26)

- **Customer A:** 39% of revenue — widely understood to be Amazon (legacy AEC anchor at 800G AEC and 200G/lane upgrade) ^[ambiguous; not named in 10-Q]
- **Customer B:** 32% — widely understood to be Microsoft (HiWire dual-ToR ramp) ^[ambiguous]
- **Customer C:** 17% — third hyperscaler each >10% — likely xAI or Meta ^[ambiguous]
- Plus a fifth hyperscaler in early ramp (called out on the Q3 FY26 call) and additional design wins at Tesla and Oracle disclosed in trade press. ^[extracted]
- Quoted top-1 % trajectory: **FY25 67% → Q4 FY25 ~61% → Q3 FY26 39%**. That is a textbook diversification curve. ^[extracted]

### Where Credo sits in the stack

Credo is the **physical-layer interconnect** layer between a switch ASIC (Broadcom Tomahawk/Jericho, NVIDIA Spectrum-X, hyperscaler custom) and the optics or copper that crosses the rack/row. It sits adjacent to — not on top of — Marvell, Astera Labs, Broadcom, and the optical-module ODMs (Coherent, Lumentum, Innolight, Eoptolink, Accelink). The post-DustPhotonics roadmap puts Credo into direct competition with Marvell on 1.6T optical DSPs and into Coherent/Lumentum's territory on photonics PICs.

### AEC vs. CPO threat (the big debate)

This is the single most-debated question on the name and worth slowing down on.

- **The bear story.** Co-packaged optics (CPO) puts the optical engine directly on the switch-ASIC package, eliminating the pluggable-module slot and the front-of-rack copper that connects to it. If CPO ramps fast, the AEC TAM growth curve flattens by FY28. Bears point at NVIDIA's Quantum-X CPO and Broadcom's Tomahawk 6-CPO disclosures and argue 2027 is the inflection.
- **The bull / structural counter.**
  - **Reach physics.** Even with CPO inside the switch, you still have to get from the switch to the GPU/NIC. That's still 1-3 meters at 1.6T+ — the AEC sweet spot. CPO replaces front-panel pluggable optics, not in-rack copper.
  - **Reliability data.** Hyperscalers run on field-MTBF data. AECs ship today with a >5x reliability advantage versus pluggable optics in the same workload. CPO has no production fleet yet — the qualification cycle alone is 18-24 months once first silicon ships.
  - **Power.** At 200G/lane an AEC link consumes ~6-8W; an equivalent pluggable optic plus retimer is 12-15W. CPO closes part but not all of that gap, and adds reliability/serviceability cost.
  - **Tomahawk 6 endorsement.** In early 2026 Broadcom (the dominant switch-ASIC vendor) publicly framed copper-attached as the volume path at 1.6T, with CPO addressing only specific reach/power-sensitive segments. That is a directional read on at least 2026-2028 architecture choices.
  - **OCP roadmap.** The OCP scale-out fabric reference designs through 2027 are predominantly copper-attached at 1.6T, transitioning to LPO + AEC hybrid before any meaningful CPO penetration.
- **Credo's response:** DustPhotonics. The acquisition explicitly buys silicon photonics PICs and a 3.2T roadmap, putting Credo *inside* the CPO supply chain rather than fighting it from the outside. The strategic ambition: be the PHY-layer connectivity vendor regardless of whether the next architecture is AEC, pluggable optics, LPO, or CPO. ^[extracted]
- **Net.** CPO is a real medium-term threat to a single product family (front-panel pluggable AEC); it is not a broad threat to Credo's overall connectivity SAM through FY28, and DustPhotonics meaningfully reshapes the FY29-30 risk profile. The 12-month investment window is not where this risk shows up in earnings.

## 9. Bear case

The shortest version of the bear case is "the multiple is fragile and the customer book is still narrow."

1. **Customer concentration is improving but still elevated.** Top 3 = 88% of Q3 FY26 revenue. A push-out from any one of MSFT, AMZN, or the third hyperscaler could swing a single quarter by 15-25%. Single-quarter re-rates in CRDO are often 20%+ moves.
2. **CPO timeline accelerates.** If Broadcom's Tomahawk 6-CPO and NVIDIA's Quantum-X CPO ship in volume in late 2026 / early 2027 rather than 2027-2028, the 1.6T AEC TAM caps lower than the bull thesis assumes. DustPhotonics is a hedge but not a same-year revenue offset.
3. **Marvell + Astera + ASIC houses crowd the retimer/DSP fight.** ALAB's Aries retimer is the head-on competitor for the 100G/200G retimer slot. MRVL is the entrenched optical-DSP leader at 800G and is investing aggressively in 1.6T. Cisco's Acacia coherent-DSP unit is an outside threat. Pricing pressure on retimers and DSPs lands first; AEC follows 2-3 quarters behind.
4. **Multiple compression on growth deceleration.** A 200%-Y/Y grower trading at 75x P/E re-rates violently when growth steps to 50%. The math: at 50% Y/Y with 35% FCF margin, "fair" multiple is ~30-35x P/E, not 75x. Even successful execution on the base case implies the multiple compresses *as* earnings beat — the question is whether earnings grow faster than the multiple compresses. From $173, bear math is $130 at 30x FY27E EPS of ~$4.30.
5. **Insider selling tape is real.** CEO Brennan Form 144 for 155,964 shares; CFO Fleming Form 144 for 8,562 shares — both in early January 2026. Coordinated April 5 sales by CEO/COO/CFO/CTO at ~$101.45. While most of this is 10b5-1, the magnitude (134 disposal transactions in the trailing 3 months, zero buys) is a sentiment-flag at a name carrying a 75x multiple. ^[extracted]
6. **DustPhotonics integration risk.** $1.3B all-in; first ever material acquisition for Credo; 70 photonics engineers in Israel layered onto a SerDes/AEC culture. Integration friction in the first 4-6 quarters is the base case for any deal of this scale, and the first synergy print needs to land cleanly to defend the optical-revenue >$500M FY27 number.
7. **Macro / Taiwan tail.** Single-source TSMC, SoCal/Bay Area + Israel + Taipei employment base. Anything that disrupts TSMC 3nm allocation or cross-strait freight is a low-probability / high-severity tail.

## 10. Position-sizing notes

- **Volatility.** Realized 30-day vol routinely 60-90%; 5%+ daily moves are normal, 15%+ around earnings or M&A.
- **Beta.** ~2.7 on a 1y window; ~3.7 on shorter windows (post-DustPhotonics-deal volatility cluster). Treat as 2.5-3x SPX on systematic risk.
- **Correlations:**
  - **AVGO** (~0.6-0.7) — both are "AI infra silicon" and trade together on hyperscaler capex prints
  - **ALAB** (~0.7-0.85) — closest pure-play comp; trades as a pair with CRDO on most days
  - **MRVL** (~0.5-0.6) — AI revenue narrative correlated; non-AI MRVL business dilutes the link
  - **ANET** (~0.5-0.6) — switch demand → AEC demand chain
  - **COHR / LITE** (~0.4-0.55) — optical-module exposure is partially substitutionary; correlation falls when copper-vs-optics narrative diverges
- **Pair-trade ideas.**
  - Long CRDO / short MRVL on optical-DSP share-shift thesis (high-conviction, high-vol)
  - Long CRDO / short COHR on AEC-displaces-optical-modules thesis (cleaner copper-vs-optics expression)
  - Long CRDO / long AVGO basket as the "1.6T physical-layer winners" pair
- **Sizing template:**
  - Conviction-high names in this sleeve: 2-4% of an AI-infra book
  - For CRDO specifically — given vol — start at 1.5%, scale to 3% on a print, hard-stop at 4%
  - Re-evaluate sizing if top-1 customer concentration *re-tightens* (any single name >45% of revenue is a yellow flag)
- **Hedges.** Vertical put spreads through earnings have generally priced cheaper than the realized move; a 10-delta put-spread structure 2-3 weeks pre-print has been a positive-EV hedge in 2025-26. (Backtest, not a recommendation.)

## 11. Tripwires (when to lighten)

- Top-1 customer share back above 50% on any quarter
- Sequential revenue declines or guide-down (especially sequential, not just Y/Y)
- Non-GAAP gross margin below 62% for two consecutive quarters
- Open-market insider *purchases* zero with sales pace materially accelerating beyond 10b5-1 schedules
- Broadcom or Marvell announcing a same-spec 1.6T AEC silicon win at MSFT or AMZN
- CPO volume timeline pull-in to 2026 (TH6-CPO shipping in volume by year-end 2026)
- DustPhotonics integration miss visible in FY27 optical-revenue commentary < $400M

## Appendix A — what would change my mind

In order of priority of signal strength:

1. **A 1.6T AEC design loss at MSFT or AWS to Marvell.** Cleanest leading indicator that the AEC moat is breached. Would cut the bull case to base case.
2. **A second hyperscaler announcing a CPO-first 1.6T deployment for 2027.** Would pull forward the CPO threat one model year. Would cut base case to bear.
3. **DustPhotonics integration miss (revenue or technical milestone).** First post-close print with optical revenue tracking below ~$120M run-rate by Q2 FY27 would call the SiPho thesis into question.
4. **Top-1 customer share re-tightening above 50%.** Indicates either a second hyperscaler pulled back or AWS bought a giant additional batch. The latter is fine; the former is a serious concentration warning.
5. **Gross-margin step-down to 62-63% on mix.** The long-term framework is 63-65% but if it lands faster than ramp dilutes it, op-leverage thesis weakens.
6. **Major insider open-market sale outside 10b5-1.** Different signal than scheduled selling.
