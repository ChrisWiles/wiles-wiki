## Investment thesis (5 bullets)

1. **Liquid-cooling pure play with #1 share.** Dell'Oro Group puts Vertiv tied with Schneider Electric for #1 in data-center liquid cooling (Q1 2025), and Vertiv leads the broader DC thermal segment with ~11.3% share of the overall liquid-cooling market in 2025. ^[extracted] Within the $7.74B Coolant Distribution Unit (CDU) market, Vertiv sits in the top three alongside Schneider, Delta, nVent, and DCX. ^[extracted] Unlike its diversified peers (Schneider has electrification + automation; nVent has enclosures + electrical connection; JCI has HVAC building-automation), Vertiv is >80% data-center exposed — purity, in a regime where DC TAM is expanding faster than every other end market, deserves a multiple premium (see [[ai-investment-public-company-scorecard]]).

2. **Order book has gone vertical, twice.** Q4 2025 organic orders +252% YoY and +117% QoQ; backlog $15.0B, up 109% YoY; Q4 book-to-bill ~2.9x. ^[extracted] Q1 2026 revenue +30% reported / +23% organic; trailing-twelve-month organic orders +81%. ^[extracted] Vertiv's pipeline duration has elongated into 2027 visibility, and management called the Q4 surge "broad-based across regions, technologies, and customers" rather than one mega-deal. The order step-up is structural — it tracks the hyperscaler shift from air to liquid as rack densities crossed 100 kW.

3. **Customer wallet share is rising as architecture shifts.** Three concurrent transitions are pulling Vertiv content per MW higher: (a) **air → single-phase liquid → two-phase / direct-to-chip** as densities go from 30 kW → 100 kW → 250+ kW per rack ^[inferred]; (b) **rack-level reference designs** (SmartRun, One Core, prefabricated power-and-cooling skids) that bundle UPS + busway + manifold + CDU + rear-door HX into a single Vertiv SKU; (c) **800-volt DC architecture** launches in H2 2026 that compete for rack-power wallet currently held by competitors at lower voltages. ^[extracted] Each transition lifts $/MW of Vertiv content and raises the switching cost for the hyperscaler.

4. **Gross-margin expansion path with credible 2029 endpoint.** FY26 adj. operating margin guide 23.3% (+290 bps YoY at midpoint). ^[extracted] Long-term target 25% by 2029, with stated 30-35% incremental margins on incremental volume. ^[extracted] Q1 2026 already printed 20.8% adj. operating margin (+430 bps YoY) ^[extracted], and management held that the Q4 2025 print of 23.2% was achievable through a tariff cycle. Margin upside is from operating leverage on capacity that's already paid for, services attach on the installed base, and pricing on liquid-cooling SKUs where qualified-supplier sets are very small.

5. **Critical-power business is durable, services compound.** UPS, busway, switchgear, and rack PDU ship into the same data-center footprint as the cooling stack and represent recurring renewal opportunity in the installed base. The Services segment is the under-appreciated compounder — every cooling deployment generates 5-10 years of maintenance, monitoring, and lifecycle revenue at margins above the product average. ^[inferred] As the installed base of liquid-cooled AI halls scales, services revenue compounds with low capital intensity.

### Why this is a 12-month pick, not a buy-and-hold

The 12-month thesis is specifically about (a) the **backlog conversion window** — most of the $15B Q4 2025 book is scheduled to ship in 2026-2027, and the conversion arithmetic is mechanical; (b) the **margin compounding pattern** — every quarter Vertiv has met-or-beat the prior margin guide, and the FY26 guide raise to 23.3% is conservative versus realized Q4'25 of 23.2% with a tariff drag; and (c) **multiple-vs-numbers offset** — even with the ~38x fwd P/E and ~22x EBITDA, FY26 numbers are likely to grind higher with each quarterly print, making the multiple compress passively while the price stays flat or rises. Compare this to a 5-year view where the 2028+ commoditization risk and competitive entry from Eaton/Boyd matter more (see [[pick-vertiv-vrt-5y]]).

## 1) Business overview

Vertiv was carved out of Emerson Network Power in 2016, taken public via SPAC merger in 2020 (Goldman Sachs Acquisition Holdings II / David Cote vehicle), and has spent the last five years repositioning from a diversified telecom-and-IT power vendor into a data-center-pure infrastructure platform. Headquartered in Westerville, Ohio. CEO Giordano Albertazzi (since 2023); Executive Chairman David Cote (ex-Honeywell).

EMEA's Q1 2026 -29% is project-timing rather than demand collapse — a couple of large 2025 EMEA shipments pulled forward, and management guided to H2 2026 EMEA recovery. ^[extracted] The Americas segment is now ~68% of revenue and growing fastest, driven by hyperscale and colocation in PJM, MISO, and ERCOT (see [[us-power-region-deep-dive]]).

**Product portfolio (functional):**

- **Critical power** — UPS (static and rotary), busway, switchgear, transfer switches, rack PDUs.
- **Thermal management** — air handlers (CRAC/CRAH), chillers, rear-door heat exchangers, CDUs, manifolds, immersion tanks, rear-door coolers, dry coolers, and (post-STL acquisition) cold-plate / chip-level cooling design IP.
- **Integrated rack systems** — SmartRun (modular rack-row power-and-cooling), One Core, prefabricated power skids, prefab data-center modules.
- **Services** — installation, commissioning, lifecycle maintenance, monitoring (Vertiv LIFE Services), spares, retrofits.

**Customer mix.** Top-5 hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Amazon/AWS, Google, Oracle) plus the major colocation operators (Digital Realty, Equinix, QTS, CyrusOne/CoreSite, Stack, Aligned, Vantage, Compass, Iron Mountain) represent a majority of the order book. ^[inferred] Vertiv is also a named cooling supplier on Stargate Abilene and a key partner on CoreWeave's neocloud build (see [[hyperscaler-constraint-map]]). Customer concentration is real but diversified across the hyperscaler set; no single customer >15% of revenue (estimated). ^[ambiguous]

**Capital structure (post Q1 2026).**

- Investment-grade ratings since Feb 2026: Moody's Baa3, S&P BBB-. ^[extracted]
- $2.1B senior unsecured notes issuance (March 2026); proceeds repaid the term loan and ABL revolver. ^[extracted]
- New $2.5B revolving credit facility. ^[extracted]
- Net leverage ~0.2x at Q1 2026. ^[extracted]
- The IG rating is a structural change — it lowers go-forward cost of debt and gives Vertiv flexibility for additional cooling/services M&A without putting the rating at risk.

**Capital allocation pattern.** Q1 2026 disclosed adjusted FCF of $653M (+$147M YoY) and FY26 FCF guide of $2.2B. ^[extracted] The FCF trajectory is the under-appreciated story — at FY27 ~$2.75B FCF on the base case ^[inferred], Vertiv has the firepower for $1-2B/year of bolt-on M&A plus continued buybacks without straining the balance sheet. Recent capital allocation has favored tuck-in M&A (CoolTera, Strategic Thermal Labs, Thermal Key) over large-scale buybacks, which makes sense while the cooling-IP land grab is open.

## 2) AI buildout exposure

Vertiv does not break out "AI revenue" as a segment, but the disclosed and inferred exposure is unusually high:

- **>80% of total revenue is data-center end-market.** ^[extracted] (The remainder is communications/edge and commercial-industrial.)

The Q4 jump is the inflection point. Several large hyperscale and colocation customers placed multi-year frame agreements in late 2025, locking in capacity slots through 2027. Backlog elongation (12-18 month delivery windows on parts of the book) is now a feature, not a bug — it gives Vertiv revenue visibility deep into FY27.

### Liquid-cooling adoption rate

- **Single-phase liquid (rear-door HX, in-row CDU)** — ~30 kW/rack, deployed broadly 2023-2025 in colocation and enterprise AI.
- **Direct-to-chip / cold-plate (single-phase)** — ~70-100 kW/rack, the dominant 2025-2026 architecture for Hopper/Blackwell-class GPUs.
- **Two-phase / hybrid immersion** — 150-250+ kW/rack, ramping 2026-2028 for Rubin/Rubin Ultra and competitive ASIC platforms. ^[inferred]
- **Full immersion (1-phase / 2-phase tanks)** — 250+ kW/rack, niche today, expected to grow with 800V DC rack architectures and next-gen accelerators. ^[inferred]

Each step up the density curve raises Vertiv's content per kW: a 250 kW/rack liquid-cooled deployment carries ~3-5x the cooling content of an equivalent 30 kW/rack air-cooled deployment. ^[inferred] This is the single-most-important non-financial fact in the thesis: density isn't just driving more cooling units, it's driving more dollars per unit.

### Specific customer/program wins (publicly identifiable)

- **Stargate / Abilene, TX** — Vertiv is a named cooling supplier on the OpenAI/Oracle/Crusoe Stargate flagship site. ^[extracted] The 2026 Q1 cooling-system incident at Abilene is a brand risk but also a confirmation of Vertiv centrality to the project. ^[extracted]
- **CoreWeave** — Vertiv is a key partner on neocloud builds (cooling, rack power). ^[extracted]
- **Microsoft, Meta, Oracle, Amazon** — all named in [[hyperscaler-constraint-map]] as Vertiv-dependent for cooling/rack power; specific contract values undisclosed. ^[ambiguous]
- **NVIDIA reference designs** — Vertiv has been named in NVIDIA reference architectures for GB200 NVL72 and successor systems; this is a wallet-share signal, not a contract. ^[inferred]

### What's pulling the order curve up

Three independent demand vectors converged in late 2025:

1. **Generation-shift orders.** Hyperscalers placing 2027-2028 capacity reservations as Blackwell systems came online and Rubin became a 2026-design-target. Once you commit to a GPU generation that requires liquid cooling at scale, the Vertiv order has to be placed 18-24 months ahead.
2. **Colocation pre-builds.** Colos (Digital Realty, Equinix, Stack, Aligned, Vantage) ordering speculative liquid-ready capacity to compete for hyperscaler awards — a fundamental shift from "build to suit" to "build ahead of suit," which front-loads Vertiv orders versus historic colo timing. ^[inferred]
3. **Neocloud build cycle.** CoreWeave, Crusoe, Lambda, Nscale, and similar AI-cloud-only operators expanding power-first sites that need full-stack rack power + liquid cooling delivered fast — typically Vertiv prefab + integrated rack systems. (See [[neocloud-and-power-first-operator-diligence]].)

**Lead times.** Vertiv has not disclosed product-level lead times publicly, but channel commentary suggests CDUs and integrated rack systems are 30-50 weeks today versus 8-16 weeks pre-2023. ^[inferred] This is consistent with the broader [[electrical-equipment-deep-dive|electrical-equipment lead-time stretch]] documented across switchgear, transformers, and PDUs.

The competitive claim that matters: in 2026, the only two vendors that can deliver rack-row-and-power as an integrated reference design at hyperscale velocity are Vertiv and Schneider. Eaton-Boyd is catching up; nVent and JCI are credible #4-5; everyone else is a component supplier. That's a defendable structural position — not a moat in the Buffett sense, but a 3-year head start that is hard to compress without a major M&A move by a competitor.

Asymmetry: beat-and-raise-and-elongate pattern has been intact for 6 consecutive quarters; the bear-case downside requires a hyperscaler capex air-pocket, which the [[ai-data-center-buildout-investment-memo|buildout memo]] argues is structurally hard before 2028.

### Why the multiple isn't necessarily expensive

But the comparison that matters is to growth-and-quality benchmarks: pure-play industrials that grow revenue 25%+ with 23%+ margins and 30-35% incremental margins typically trade in the 25-35x EBITDA range. VRT is below that band. The relevant Trane comparison (~22-23x EBITDA on lower revenue growth) suggests the current multiple is rational rather than bubble-priced. ^[inferred]

## 8) Bear case

Risks ranked roughly in order of severity:

1. **Hyperscaler insourcing of cooling.** Microsoft, Meta, and Google have internal cooling-engineering teams and have published reference designs (e.g., Open Compute liquid manifolds, Microsoft's microfluidic chip cooling research). The bear scenario: hyperscalers commoditize the CDU, manifold, and rear-door HX into open hardware and source from cheaper Asian ODMs (Delta, ASE, Foxconn). Vertiv is reduced to a contract integrator. Mitigant: hyperscalers have published open hardware before (OCP servers, switches) and the named-vendor incumbents have generally retained dollar share even as designs commoditized. Vertiv's services attach + lifecycle revenue is also harder to insource.

2. **AI capex pause / digestion year.** The structural argument from the [[ai-data-center-buildout-investment-memo]] is that 2026-2028 is supply-bound rather than demand-bound, but if frontier-model economics underperform (training plateaus, inference monetization disappoints), one or more hyperscalers could revise capex down 15-25%. VRT order book would air-pocket within 1-2 quarters. ^[ambiguous] Mitigant: backlog elongation through 2027 cushions the immediate revenue hit, and colocation customer mix is less correlated to any single hyperscaler's capex cycle.

3. **Margin mean-reversion / commoditization.** Liquid cooling in 2026 has the margin profile of a scarce engineered product because qualified-supplier sets are small. As capacity comes online (Vertiv, Schneider, nVent, Boyd-Eaton, Asian ODMs), competitive intensity rises. The 25% LT margin target may get pushed out from 2029 to 2031+. Mitigant: services and software (Vertiv LIFE) dilute the commoditization risk; integrated reference designs (SmartRun, One Core) raise switching costs; 800V DC is a fresh white space.

4. **Eaton/Boyd integration risk to Vertiv share.** Eaton's $9.5B Boyd Corporation acquisition (2025) added ~$1.5B in liquid-cooling revenue and gives Eaton a credible #3 position. ^[extracted] If Eaton can leverage its electrical-equipment relationships with hyperscalers (UPS, switchgear) to package power+cooling, Vertiv loses share at integration. Mitigant: Vertiv's installed-base, services depth, and end-to-end thermal IP (post-STL) are still ahead.

5. **Schneider Electric scale advantage.** Schneider is 4-5x Vertiv's revenue with comparable DC market position; if SU decides to compete harder on DC pricing, Vertiv's margin path slows. Mitigant: Schneider's DC business is ~30% of total — they have less reason to "win at any cost" than Vertiv, which is fighting for its existential market.

6. **Customer concentration & cooling-system reliability events.** The Stargate Abilene Q1 2026 cooling incident is a reputational data point. ^[extracted] A second high-profile failure could cost a hyperscaler relationship.

7. **Tariff and supply-chain.** US tariffs on Chinese-origin components hit some Vertiv inputs; the company has been pricing through, but a step-change in tariff rates could compress margin in any quarter. ^[extracted]

8. **Valuation.** ~22x FY26 EBITDA / ~48x FY26 EPS leaves no margin for a guide miss. Multiple compression on a single soft quarter could mean -15% to -25% in days, even without a thesis break.

9. **Cooling-failure tail risk at frontier scale.** The Stargate Abilene incident (Q1 2026 winter cooling-system failure) was contained but real ^[extracted]; a second high-profile failure on a flagship cluster — particularly if it interacts with insurance, uptime SLAs, or hyperscaler warranty terms — could compress order momentum and force pricing concessions. Liquid-cooling system reliability at >100 kW/rack densities is genuinely frontier engineering, and field-failure modes (leak detection, micro-fluidic clogging, manifold pressure differentials, glycol degradation) are still being characterized. Mitigant: this is an industry-wide risk, not Vertiv-specific; the alternative vendors face the same engineering envelope.

10. **Macro / rate-cycle drag.** VRT trades like a high-beta growth name. A material rate shock (10y >5.5% sustained) or a US recession scare would compress the multiple regardless of fundamentals. The IG balance sheet helps on the operating side but does not protect the multiple.

## 9) Position-sizing

- **Cap profile:** Mid-large cap (~$117B). Liquidity is deep; daily volume well over $1B notional. ^[inferred]
- **Beta:** ~1.5 vs SPX ^[ambiguous]; will move 1.5-2x the market's AI-infrastructure factor.
- **Correlations:** ~0.7 with MOD (data-center cooling beta), ~0.6 with NVT, ~0.5 with ETN, ~0.4 with NVDA. ^[ambiguous] In a sharp AI-capex drawdown, expect VRT-MOD to move together at -30% or worse intraday-to-day.
- **Concentration cap suggestion:** No more than 5-7% of an AI-thematic equity sleeve in a single name. Vertiv is the obvious anchor — but pair it (don't replace it) with [[at-risk-modine-mod|MOD]], an electrical name (ETN/POWL), and a power name (CEG/VST).
- **Drawdown plan:** A 20-25% pullback on no thesis-break event (e.g., a market-wide AI-capex scare without VRT-specific news) is a re-load opportunity. A thesis-break event (orders Y/Y collapses, hyperscaler capex revision) is an exit, not a re-load.
- **Hedges:** Short-dated VRT puts are expensive given realized volatility; cleaner hedges are NVDA puts (correlation hedge on the AI-capex factor) or pair-trade against a less-pure cooling peer.

### What we'd need to see to upgrade conviction further

- TTM organic orders growth holding above +60% through Q2 and Q3 2026 prints.
- Adj. operating margin sustaining 22%+ even on quarters with elevated tariff drag.
- Services revenue mix moving toward 25-30% of total (currently ~20%).
- A second IG ratings upgrade (from Baa3/BBB- to mid-IG) within 18 months, signaling balance-sheet durability.
- A non-Big-Four hyperscaler announcement (e.g., a sovereign-AI program in EMEA or APAC) booking >$500M of Vertiv content — diversifies away from US hyperscaler dependence.

### What we'd need to see to exit

- TTM organic orders <+20% for two consecutive quarters.
- Adj. operating margin compression Y/Y, sustained.
- A Microsoft-or-Meta capex revision lower of >10%.
- Loss of a top-3 hyperscaler relationship (would likely show up first in book-to-bill).
- A multiple re-rating below 14x EBITDA on no-news that doesn't snap back within 30 days (signals factor-flip).

## 11) Cross-checks: what would have to be wrong

Every long thesis is implicitly a set of bets on the inputs. Listing them explicitly:

- **Bet 1: AI capex sustains $400B+/year (cumulative top-5 hyperscaler) through 2028.** If 2026 hyperscaler capex prints below ~$350B aggregate, the order book elongation that makes this thesis work is at risk. This is tracked against [[ai-capital-ownership-and-purchasing-power]] and [[hyperscaler-constraint-map]].
- **Bet 2: Liquid cooling continues taking share from air at >5 GW/yr of incremental data-center capacity.** Air-cooled limits cap at ~50 kW/rack; once GPUs cross that line, the substitution is forced. The bet is that GPU roadmaps stay above the line — Blackwell (700W/GPU), Rubin (~1.2-1.5 kW/GPU expected ^[inferred]) both clear it.
- **Bet 3: Vertiv holds its share of the liquid-cooling market at >10%.** Schneider, Eaton-Boyd, nVent, Asian ODMs all want a bigger share. The bet is that hyperscaler reference-design relationships and services-attach economics keep Vertiv at parity-or-better with Schneider for at least 24 months.
- **Bet 4: Margin expansion path holds.** 23.3% (FY26 guide) → 25% (FY29 target). The bet is that volume leverage and services mix outpace the natural commoditization of mature SKUs.
- **Bet 5: Multiple compresses passively, not violently.** 22x EBITDA → 18-19x EBITDA over two years as numbers grind higher; price stays flat or up modestly. The bet is the market doesn't decide AI infrastructure is a "value trap" before earnings catch up.

If two or more of these bets break, this is no longer a 12-month long. The single highest-probability break is bet 5 (multiple compression on no-news), which is why the position-sizing cap is firm at 5-7% of an AI sleeve.
