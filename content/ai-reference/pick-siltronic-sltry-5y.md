## 1. Header and one-line thesis

Siltronic AG (Frankfurt: WAF.DE; OTC ADR: SLTRY; ratio 1 ADR : 0.2 ordinary share) is the **#3 global producer of monocrystalline silicon wafers**, the foundational substrate every semiconductor is built on. The company is a true **pure-play**: 100% of revenue comes from 200mm and 300mm polished and epitaxial silicon wafers shipped to logic and memory fabs.^[extracted from Siltronic FY2025 annual report] Market share is approximately **13-15% of the global silicon-wafer market by revenue**, behind Shin-Etsu Handotai (~30%) and SUMCO (~22-24%), and ahead of GlobalWafers (~12-14%) and SK Siltron (~10-12%).^[inferred from SEMI / TECHCET 2025 share data]

The 5-year setup hinges on a single inflection: **silicon wafers are the next AI-cycle bottleneck**. SemiAnalysis published a thread on **2026-04-30** explicitly flagging silicon-wafer makers as the next constraint, with the binding mechanics laid out below. Siltronic is the **highest-beta way to play this cycle** because (a) it is a pure-play with no diluting non-wafer segments, (b) Singapore **Wafer Fab 5** — the company's flagship 300mm epi expansion — was completed 2024-2025 and is ramping into a tightening market, (c) FY24-FY25 was the absolute trough for wafer pricing, so EBITDA margin starts from a depressed base of ~22-25% and has 800-1,200bps of upside as utilization, mix, and ASPs all turn together, and (d) the equity is mid-cap with limited float, which means cycle moves of 2-3x rerating are historically normal (the 2016-17 upcycle saw the stock run from ~€40 to >€130 in 18 months).^[extracted from historical Frankfurt price series]

If SemiAnalysis is directionally right that ≤7nm logic wafer demand inflects in 2027 and reaches ~1M wafers per month by CY28, that supply is **structurally capped** because (i) industry capex is running ~70% below the 2023 peak, (ii) lead times for new epi reactors are 6+ months, (iii) no new greenfield capacity comes online before 2H27, and (iv) long-term agreements (LTAs) lock 2025-2027 ASPs at trough levels but **2028+ LTAs are being signed today at ~+20% epi ASPs**.^[extracted from SemiAnalysis 2026-04-30 thread] Siltronic is positioned to print FY30 revenue **~50-90% above FY25 trough** with EBITDA margin re-expanding to peak-cycle levels of 30%+, against an equity that today trades at ~0.7-0.8x sales and ~4-5x peak-cycle EBITDA.

## 2. Thesis in five points

1. **Pure-play silicon wafer #3 globally with no segment dilution.** Siltronic has one product line (silicon wafers), three plants (Singapore, Burghausen Germany, Portland Oregon), and a customer book that reads like the global semiconductor org chart — Samsung, SK hynix, Intel, Infineon, STMicro, GlobalFoundries, NXP, Texas Instruments, Micron, TSMC.^[inferred from public customer references and industry attribution; Siltronic does not disclose customer-level revenue] Investors who want pure wafer beta cannot get cleaner exposure. Shin-Etsu Chemical (SHECY) buries the segment inside a chemicals conglomerate; SUMCO trades in Tokyo with worse U.S. liquidity; GlobalWafers is heavily exposed to Taiwan + has a contested U.S. greenfield (Sherman, TX). Siltronic is the cleanest mid-cap European-listed pure-play.
2. **Highest pure-play leverage to the ASP turn.** Wafer manufacturing has very high fixed costs (clean rooms, crystal pullers, epi reactors, polishers). Once a fab is paid for, the **incremental gross margin on each additional wafer or each €1 of ASP uplift is 80-90%**.^[inferred from Siltronic's quarterly margin walk vs. revenue walk 2017-2025] Every 1% ASP move flows ~85%+ to gross profit; every 1% utilization point at Singapore Fab 5 adds €15-20M to EBITDA at base capex.^[inferred] In a cycle where SemiAnalysis flags 2028+ LTAs at +20% ASPs, that is gross-margin expansion of ~1,500-1,700bps before any volume contribution.
3. **Singapore Wafer Fab 5 lands into a tightening market — operating leverage thesis.** The Singapore epi expansion (300mm), greenlit 2020-2021 and ramped through 2024-2025, added meaningful 300mm epi capacity to Siltronic's footprint at a moment when the rest of the industry has frozen capex. Capacity that was an underutilization headwind in FY24-FY25 (the depreciation hit absorbed nearly 800bps of EBITDA margin) becomes the **operating leverage engine** in FY26-FY30 as utilization climbs from ~70-75% trough toward 90%+ at the cycle peak.^[inferred from Siltronic FY24-FY25 capacity-utilization commentary]
4. **SemiAnalysis 2026-04-30 thesis: leading-edge wafer demand inflecting 2027; supply capped; 2028+ LTAs at +20% ASPs.** Patel's team laid out the mechanics: ≤7nm logic wafer demand reaches ~1M wpm by CY28; HBM unit growth pulls hard on bulk polished wafers (HBM is wafer-intensive on the bulk side because each HBM stack consumes 8-16 base + core dies, and base-die area is non-trivial); CapEx is capped 70% below 2023 peak; epi-reactor lead times are 6+ months; new capacity not until 2H27; LTAs lock pricing through 2027 but **2028+ LTAs are repricing at ~+20% on epi**. Siltronic is the clearest beneficiary because it is mostly polished + epi, weighted toward 300mm, and the Singapore capacity is already built.
5. **Cyclical recovery from FY24-FY25 trough — 2016-17 template implies 2-3x rerating potential.** In the 2016-17 upcycle, Siltronic ran from ~€40 (Q1 2016) to ~€130 (Q4 2017), a >3x move on EBITDA margin expansion from ~15% to ~37% and revenue growth from ~€930M to ~€1.18B in 18 months.^[extracted from historical Siltronic financials] FY26 starts with revenue at ~€1.4B and EBITDA margin at ~22-25%; the FY30 base lands at €2.0-2.6B revenue and 30%+ EBITDA margin. Same shape, larger absolute base. EPS goes from ~€2.50 FY25 trough to **€10-15 FY30 base / €18-22 FY30 bull**, against a current ~€62 quote.

## 3. Business overview

Siltronic AG is a Munich-headquartered, Frankfurt-listed (Prime Standard, MDAX) silicon-wafer manufacturer founded in its modern form in 1968 as a Wacker Chemie subsidiary, IPO'd in 2015. Wacker Chemie retains a strategic stake (~30% historically; check the latest annual report for current holdings as buyback dynamics may have shifted this).^[ambiguous — stake size has moved over time] Siltronic employs ~4,300 people across three manufacturing sites and a global sales / R&D footprint.

### Product mix

| Product | What it is | Wafer size | Customer base | Mix (FY25) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 300mm epitaxial (epi) | Polished wafer with a thin epitaxial silicon layer grown on top — required for ≤14nm logic, all leading-edge logic | 300mm | TSMC, Samsung Foundry, Intel, GlobalFoundries | ~35-40% of revenue^[inferred] |
| 300mm polished (Prime / Annealed) | Bulk polished wafer, used for DRAM, NAND, HBM base/core dies, mature logic | 300mm | Samsung, SK hynix, Micron, mature logic | ~35-40% of revenue^[inferred] |
| 200mm polished + epi | All applications at 200mm — analog, power, automotive, MCU, RF, MEMS | 200mm | Infineon, STMicro, NXP, TI, ON, Renesas | ~20-25% of revenue^[inferred] |
| Specialty (FZ, ultra-thin, high-resistivity) | Niche float-zone, RF / power-specialty wafers | 200mm + 300mm | Power semis, RF | <5% of revenue^[inferred] |

### Geography (manufacturing footprint)

- **Singapore (largest site)** — 200mm + 300mm. Houses **Wafer Fab 5**, the new 300mm epi expansion. Shared site with Siltronic-Samsung JV historical context (now fully Siltronic-controlled). Singapore is the company's lowest-cost 300mm production base.
- **Burghausen, Germany** — 200mm + 300mm + crystal pulling + R&D headquarters. Co-located with Wacker Chemie polysilicon supply. Modernization investments running through 2026.
- **Portland, Oregon (Hillsboro / Vancouver WA cluster nearby)** — 200mm primarily. Local supply for U.S. fabs (Intel Oregon / Arizona, GlobalFoundries Vermont, TI). Strategic in a CHIPS Act "U.S.-content" world even though the fab itself is small.

### Customer mix

Siltronic does not disclose customer revenue. Industry attribution and earnings-call commentary suggest the top 5 customers are roughly **45-55% of revenue**, with no single customer above ~15%.^[inferred] Implied list (alphabetical): GlobalFoundries, Infineon, Intel, Micron, NXP, Samsung Electronics (foundry + memory), SK hynix, STMicroelectronics, Texas Instruments, TSMC. Memory customers (Samsung memory + SK hynix + Micron) are ~25-35% of revenue, leading-edge logic foundries (TSMC + Samsung Foundry + Intel + GlobalFoundries) are ~30-40%, and analog / power / automotive (Infineon, ST, NXP, TI, ON, Renesas) is ~25-35%.^[inferred] This diversification is a feature, not a bug — Siltronic is less concentrated than SUMCO (heavier memory) or GlobalWafers (heavier Taiwan).

### Segments

Single segment — **silicon wafers**. Reporting currency is **EUR** (Frankfurt domicile, December fiscal year-end). Translation exposure: USD (Singapore + Portland + customer-pricing in USD), SGD (Singapore costs), JPY (input materials), CNY (some Chinese-fab customers). The structural EUR/USD long is roughly 60-65% of revenue in USD vs. ~50% of cost in non-EUR.^[inferred]

### Competitive position

The silicon wafer industry is a **rational five-firm oligopoly** with high entry barriers:

- **Shin-Etsu Handotai (SEH, subsidiary of Shin-Etsu Chemical)** — ~30% share. Largest, most diversified, most leading-edge. Buried inside SHECY conglomerate.
- **SUMCO Corporation (3436.T)** — ~22-24% share. Tokyo-listed pure-play. Heavier memory customer mix than Siltronic.
- **Siltronic (WAF.DE / SLTRY)** — **~13-15% share. The pick.**
- **GlobalWafers (6488.TWO)** — ~12-14% share. Taiwan-listed. Sherman, TX U.S. greenfield contested. Heavier Taiwan / TSMC customer mix.
- **SK Siltron** — ~10-12% share. Captive subsidiary of SK Group, primarily SK hynix supply.

**Entry barriers**: 5-7 year lead time for a new entrant to qualify at a leading-edge logic customer; >$5B capital cost for a credible 300mm fab; recipe / metallurgy IP built over decades; defect-density requirements at ≤7nm impossibly tight for a new entrant. Chinese entrants (Zhonghuan, NSIG, TCL Hua Xing) are years away from leading-edge qualification despite heavy state subsidy.

**Industry structure rationality**: The 2014-2017 cycle taught wafer-makers that capacity discipline pays. None of the top-5 has overbuilt since SunEdison Semiconductor (predecessor to GlobalWafers) collapsed in 2016. This is part of why CapEx is ~70% below 2023 peak today.

## 4. AI buildout exposure

100% of revenue is silicon wafers. AI exposure flows through the customer mix:

- **Memory customers** (Samsung memory + SK hynix + Micron) — directly AI-driven via HBM. HBM4 / HBM4E unit growth is the primary near-term driver of bulk polished 300mm wafer demand. Each HBM stack has 12-16 dies; base + core dies are bulk polished wafers; logic die (sometimes co-packaged) needs epi. SemiAnalysis's framing: HBM is wafer-intensive on the bulk polished side.
- **Logic customers** — Intel for AI ASICs (incl. internal Habana / Gaudi successors, foundry tenants), Samsung Foundry (hyperscaler ASICs e.g. Tesla Dojo, Google TPU manufacturing diversification), GlobalFoundries (custom AI silicon for hyperscalers — networking, smart NICs), TSMC (the dominant AI accelerator manufacturer, where Siltronic's 300mm epi share matters most).
- **Analog / power / automotive** — much less AI-directly-coupled near-term, but **AI data center power infrastructure** (PSU silicon, DC/DC converters, power management) does pull on Infineon / STMicro / TI volumes — second-order AI exposure via the [[electrical-equipment-deep-dive]] stack.

**Quantified AI exposure: ~30-40% of FY26 revenue indirectly tied to AI buildout.**^[inferred] This breaks down as:
- ~12-18% from leading-edge logic 300mm epi to AI-accelerator manufacturing (TSMC + Samsung Foundry + Intel + GFS)
- ~10-15% from HBM-driven memory bulk-polished demand at Samsung memory + SK hynix + Micron
- ~5-10% from AI-data-center power semiconductor pull at Infineon / ST / TI

This is **lower headline AI exposure than ASML or BESI but higher leverage to the ASP turn**, because wafer-makers have ~85% incremental margin on the upside while semicap-equipment vendors have 50-60%.^[inferred]

## 5. Demand drivers — the SemiAnalysis 2026-04-30 thesis

The proximate catalyst for this pick is the SemiAnalysis thread published on 2026-04-30. Key claims (framed as the public consensus picture; specific numbers tagged extracted-from-thread):

- **Leading-edge logic ≤7nm wafer demand inflecting 2027.** Pull-in driven by TSMC N2 / N2P / A16, Samsung SF2 / SF1.4, Intel 18A / 14A, all of which require 300mm epi wafers with tight defect specs.
- **~1M wafers-per-month (wpm) of ≤7nm logic by CY28.**^[extracted from SemiAnalysis 2026-04-30] For perspective, the global 300mm wafer market is roughly 7.0-7.5M wpm capacity^[inferred from SEMI data]; ≤7nm reaching 1M wpm means leading-edge alone consumes 13-15% of global 300mm capacity, which is unprecedented.
- **HBM is wafer-intensive on the bulk polished side.** SemiAnalysis explicitly flags that HBM unit growth pulls **bulk polished** demand, not just niche memory wafers. The math: each HBM4 stack (16-high reference) contains 16 DRAM dies + 1 base die. At ~30 HBM stacks per AI accelerator package and ~3-5M AI accelerators per year by CY28^[inferred], you get hundreds of thousands of wafers/year of bulk polished demand from HBM alone. This is the driver of SUMCO's polished-wafer order-book improvement and Siltronic's polished-wafer mix-shift.
- **CapEx capped 70% below 2023 peak.**^[extracted from SemiAnalysis] Industry wafer-makers cut capex hard in 2023-2025 in response to FY23-FY25 demand softness. None of the top-5 wafer-makers signed new greenfield expansions in 2024-2025.
- **6M+ lead times for epi reactors.** Tools to expand epi capacity (mostly from ASMI, Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron, Veeco) have lead times that mean any decision to expand today doesn't deliver wafers until 2H27 at earliest.
- **2028+ LTAs locking in ~20% higher epi ASPs.** This is the smoking gun. Customers are signing 2028+ supply agreements **today** at materially higher prices because they cannot get reliable supply otherwise.

### Per-wafer ASP math (the punchline)

A blended 300mm epi wafer prices at roughly **$120-150 per wafer** at trough today (FY24-FY25), **$160-180** mid-cycle, and **$200+** at cycle peak.^[inferred from peer disclosures and SEMI data] If 2028+ LTAs are repricing at +20% from current levels, the steady-state blended ASP moves from ~$135 today to ~$165 by FY28 — a **+$30/wafer** uplift on every 300mm epi wafer Siltronic ships. At Siltronic's run-rate of roughly **400-450k 300mm epi wpm × 12 months = ~5.0-5.4M wafers/yr** (post-Fab 5 ramp at 90% utilization)^[inferred], that's **~$150-160M of revenue uplift per year purely from ASP**, dropping ~85%+ to gross profit — i.e. **~$130M+ of incremental EBITDA** at full utilization, against FY25 EBITDA of ~€295M. The ASP turn alone is potentially +40-45% to EBITDA before any volume contribution.

### 2016-17 cycle context (pattern reference)

The 2016-17 wafer cycle is the closest historical analog. Sequence:
- 2014-2015: Wafer-maker consolidation (GlobalWafers acquires SunEdison Semiconductor; SUMCO restructures); industry capex frozen for 2-3 years.
- Mid-2016: ASPs inflect on tightening utilization. SUMCO and Shin-Etsu sign multi-year LTAs at materially higher prices.
- 2017: Spot prices up 20-30% YoY; LTA prices up 15-25%; gross margins expand 700-1,200bps; equities double or triple.
- Siltronic stock: **~€40 (Jan 2016) → ~€130 (Nov 2017)**, a >3x move in ~22 months on a similar setup.^[extracted from historical Frankfurt price series]

The 2026-2030 setup has a **larger absolute customer pull (AI accelerators + HBM)** layered on top of the same structural supply-side mechanics. The shape rhymes; the magnitude could exceed.

### Capacity vs. market

Siltronic's 300mm capacity post-Fab 5 is approximately **800-900k 300mm-equivalent wafers/month**.^[inferred from disclosed capex and industry attribution; Siltronic does not disclose precise capacity figures] This is roughly **11-13% of global 300mm capacity** (vs. ~30% Shin-Etsu, ~22-24% SUMCO, ~12-14% GlobalWafers, ~10-12% SK Siltron). 200mm capacity adds another ~400-500k wpm equivalent.^[inferred]

Global 300mm capacity is currently running at **~80-85% utilization** post-FY24-FY25 trough; Siltronic's utilization is in the **70-75% range** because Fab 5 is still ramping.^[inferred from earnings-call commentary] As industry utilization moves toward 90%+ on the SemiAnalysis-flagged demand inflection, Siltronic's utilization should rise faster than peers — that's the operating-leverage thesis.

### CapEx trajectory

- FY22-FY23: Peak capex ~€1.0-1.2B/yr funding Singapore Fab 5 + Burghausen modernization.^[extracted from Siltronic 2022-2023 annual reports]
- FY24: Stepped down to ~€500-600M as Fab 5 ramped.
- FY25: ~€300-400M, mostly maintenance + selective debottlenecking.^[inferred from FY25 disclosure]
- FY26-FY28: Maintenance-level **~€200-300M/yr** until the next greenfield decision. This is the "CapEx capped 70% below 2023 peak" pattern SemiAnalysis flags industry-wide.
- FY29-FY30: Likely re-acceleration if demand sustains and ASPs hold; new greenfield decision could come 2027-2028 (Singapore expansion phase 2 or U.S. greenfield with CHIPS Act incentive).

The cash flow consequence is large. Siltronic was FCF-negative or break-even FY22-FY24 because of Fab 5 capex; FY25 was the inflection to mildly positive FCF; **FY26-FY30 should produce €200-500M/yr in FCF** at depreciation that is now front-loaded.^[inferred] That funds dividend resumption (suspended 2023; guide for resumption framed as "subject to FY result and visibility") and de-leveraging.

## 10. Bear case

1. **Memory cycle reverses faster than ASP turn delivers.** The single biggest risk. If hyperscaler accelerator demand slows in 2027-2028 (training compute "AI bubble" deflation, GPU obsolescence cycle, scaling-laws disappointment), HBM growth flattens, memory wafer pull weakens, and ASPs stagnate before Siltronic's volume + utilization story plays out. In this world, FY27-FY28 EBITDA margin stays in the mid-20s and the multiple compresses. Stock could revisit €40-50.
2. **Chinese wafer entrants accelerating.** TCL Hua Xing (CSOT-affiliated), Zhonghuan Advanced (TCL-affiliated), and National Silicon Industry Group are all scaling 300mm domestic wafer capacity targeting Chinese fab tenants. They are unlikely to qualify at TSMC, Intel, or Samsung Foundry leading-edge for years, but they steal **trailing-edge memory and analog volume** from incumbents. SemiAnalysis flags this as a 2027-2030 risk; it caps trailing-edge ASP recovery.
3. **Customer concentration — Samsung is large.** Samsung memory + Samsung Foundry combined is plausibly 12-18% of revenue^[inferred]. A Samsung-specific issue (e.g. HBM4 qualification delay, foundry market-share loss to TSMC, Korean macro) would directly impact Siltronic. Same risk for SK hynix and Intel separately.
4. **Singapore Fab 5 underutilization risk.** If demand softens or LTA renegotiations come in unfavorable, Singapore Fab 5 runs below ~80% utilization for an extended period and the depreciation drag remains the dominant margin story. This is the most-direct way Siltronic underperforms peers because it is the most-leveraged to that single asset.
5. **FX (EUR/USD).** Siltronic reports in EUR but ~60-65% of revenue is USD-denominated and Singapore costs are partially SGD. A weaker USD vs. EUR is a direct headwind. A 5% USD depreciation is roughly a 3% revenue and ~5-7% EBITDA hit on translation alone.^[inferred]
6. **Multiple compression specific to mid-cap European semis.** German MDAX exposure historically de-rates against U.S. semicap names in risk-off, even when fundamentals are constructive. Liquidity-driven multiple gap could persist.
7. **CapEx re-acceleration.** If management decides to greenfield (Singapore phase 2 or U.S. CHIPS Act fab) in 2027-2028, FCF in the model compresses materially in FY28-FY30. The "free cash flow turns positive and dividend resumes" thesis gets pushed out by another 18-24 months.
8. **ADR liquidity-driven mispricing risk.** SLTRY ADRs trade thin. Wide bid/ask, occasional staleness vs. WAF.DE primary listing, and FX/ADR-fee drag mean the U.S.-based investor in the ADR underperforms the underlying. **This is why the recommendation is to use Frankfurt for size.**

### Tripwires that would change the thesis

Cross-reference [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]. Specific Siltronic-relevant tripwires:

- **TSMC, Samsung Foundry, or Intel cuts FY27 capex guidance by >15% YoY** — leading-edge wafer pull weakens, ≤7nm 1M-wpm CY28 path becomes implausible
- **HBM4 qualification delays at SK hynix or Samsung push HBM4 volume right by ≥2 quarters** — bulk polished wafer pull delayed, base case revenue trajectory slips
- **Spot wafer prices break trend lower in 2H 2026** despite the LTA framework — would imply customer over-ordering or demand softness
- **Two or more Chinese 300mm wafer-makers reach leading-edge qualification at a non-Chinese fab** — structural share-loss risk re-prices the multiple permanently lower
- **Siltronic Q2 or Q3 2026 prints with negative book-to-bill or weakening LTA commentary** — internal-execution / customer-specific concern

If two or more tripwires fire concurrently, reassess the long.
