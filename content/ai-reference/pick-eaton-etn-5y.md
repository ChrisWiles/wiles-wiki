## 1. Investment thesis (5 bullets)

1. **Broadest electrification moat in the public market.** Electrical Americas (~52% of revenue) + Electrical Global (~22%) together = ~74% of company revenue and ~80% of segment EBIT. Eaton sells the full electrical stack — switchgear, transformers, busway, breakers, UPS, power-distribution units, controls, software (Brightlayer), services — into every customer segment that matters: hyperscalers, colos, utilities, industrials, commercial real estate, residential, and infrastructure. No public peer has this breadth at this scale: Schneider (SU.PA) is broader globally but split between energy management and industrial automation; Hubbell (HUBB) is narrower (utility T&D, lighting); Vertiv (VRT) is narrower still (data-center thermal + power); GE Vernova (GEV) plays grid/turbine/wind, not low/medium-voltage electrical. **Eaton is the only one-stop shop for the electron-side of the AI build, the reshoring build, and the grid-hardening build simultaneously.**

2. **Structural electrification + reshoring + AI-DC + grid hardening + EV charging compound the same playbook over multiple cycles.** Each tailwind drives the same product set (switchgear, busway, breakers, UPS, controls). Eaton estimates total power demand from data centers, manufacturing reshoring, electrification of buildings/transport, and grid investment at a multi-trillion-dollar 10-year envelope ^[Investor Day Mar 2024, refreshed Mar 2025]. Eaton's "mega-projects" tracker (>$1B announced industrial/commercial/data-center projects in North America) crossed **$2.0T cumulative announced** by Q4 2025, of which only ~13% has begun execution. Multi-cycle visibility is unusual for an industrial cyclical.

3. **Margin expansion path is the compounding lever.** Adj. operating margin: FY22 18.0% → FY23 19.7% → FY24 21.5% → FY25 ~22.4% (guide midpoint hit) ^[extracted]. 2030 plan targets **23–25%** consolidated, with Electrical Americas already running at ~30% segment margin and Electrical Global ~19% with room to ~22%. Margin levers: (a) mix shift to data-center / large-project orders (higher ASP, higher software attach), (b) fixed-cost absorption from new capacity coming online into a backlog already booked, (c) Brightlayer software attach + service revenue (recurring, ~85% gross margin), (d) divestiture of lower-margin Vehicle ICE products. Each 100bps of consolidated margin at $30B+ revenue run-rate is ~$300M of EBIT, ~$0.55–0.60 of EPS.

4. **Capital allocation is shareholder-friendly without leverage stretch.** Net debt / adj. EBITDA ~1.0x (FY25). Eaton has raised the dividend for **16 consecutive years** (Feb 2026 raise: +11% to $4.32 annualized). Buyback authorization $3.5B refreshed Feb 2026. Tuck-in M&A discipline: NordicEPOD (custom modular data-center power, 2024), Exertherm (thermal monitoring, 2024), Resilient Power (containerized power, Q3 2025), and Fibrebond (modular concrete data-center buildings, announced Mar 2025, closed Q4 2025). Each acquisition is small, accretive, and bolts directly into the data-center value proposition rather than diversifying away from the core.

5. **Aerospace + eMobility are free options on top.** Aerospace (~14% of revenue) is a quietly excellent business: 75–80% aftermarket revenue, ~25% segment margin, levered to commercial and military OEM rate increases (737 MAX recovery, A320 family, F-35) and aftermarket utilization that's still mid-cycle. eMobility (~3% of revenue, currently dilutive) is a small drag now but the 2026 program ramp (DC-fast charging modules, vehicle inverters) flips it to break-even by FY27 and modestly accretive by FY29 ^[inferred from prior management guidance]. Vehicle (~9% of revenue) is the only segment in secular decline, but it's small and management has signaled they'll prune.

## 2. Business overview

Eaton Corporation plc is a power-management company headquartered in Dublin, Ireland (operational HQ Beachwood, OH). Five reporting segments:

| Segment | FY25 revenue ^[extracted] | FY25 segment EBIT ^[extracted] | FY25 segment margin | What's in it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Electrical Americas** | ~$13.0B | ~$3.9B | ~30.0% | LV/MV switchgear, busway, breakers, transformers, UPS (PDU/RPP), Brightlayer software, services. North America focus. |
| **Electrical Global** | ~$5.5B | ~$1.05B | ~19.0% | Same product set as Americas, sold into EMEA, APAC, LatAm. |
| **Aerospace** | ~$3.5B | ~$0.85B | ~24.5% | Hydraulic, fuel, motion control, fluid conveyance for commercial / military aircraft + space. |
| **Vehicle** | ~$2.3B | ~$0.36B | ~15.5% | Transmission, valvetrain, controls for ICE truck and commercial vehicle. Secular decline. |
| **eMobility** | ~$0.7B | ~($0.05B) | negative | EV inverters, DC-DC converters, charging power modules, fleet electrification. |
| **Total** | ~$25.0B | ~$5.6B (segment) | ~22.4% (adj. op margin) | |

**Customer concentration: low.** No single customer >10% of revenue. Top-10 customers ~25% of revenue (estimated; not disclosed in 10-K). Electrical Americas data-center customer mix includes the four U.S. hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, AWS, Meta), Oracle, the major colocation providers (Equinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne, Stack), and the EPCs that serve them (Jacobs, AECOM, Burns & McDonnell, M.A. Mortenson).

**Geographic mix.** ~70% North America, ~15% EMEA, ~10% APAC, ~5% LatAm. Eaton has been increasing North America mix structurally — the reshoring + AI-DC tailwinds are NA-concentrated, and management has explicitly de-emphasized China ex-MV power.

**Segment-by-segment 5-year framing:**

- **Electrical Americas** is the engine. The thesis lives or dies on this segment. Base case: $13.0B → ~$22B by FY30 (~11% CAGR), margin 30% → 32–33%, segment EBIT ~$3.9B → $7.0–7.5B. This single segment accounts for nearly all of the consolidated EBIT growth in the base case.
- **Electrical Global** is steady support: $5.5B → ~$8B (~8% CAGR), margin 19% → 22%. The European market is slower but not dead — French / German / Nordic data-center buildouts (e.g. AWS Frankfurt, GCP Belgium, MSFT Sweden) drive growth. UK and India are smaller but accelerating.
- **Aerospace** is the high-quality compounder: $3.5B → ~$5B (~7% CAGR), margin 24.5% → 26%. Mid-cycle commercial aerospace plus persistent defense (F-35, future programs) plus high aftermarket attach. Segment is structurally undervalued inside Eaton vs. pure-play aerospace comps (HEI, HWM trade at much higher multiples).
- **Vehicle** is the secular fade: $2.3B → ~$1.8B (-4% CAGR), margin 15.5% → 13–14%. Eventually divestable — likely a 2027–2029 portfolio decision once eMobility is profitable enough to absorb the segment realignment.
- **eMobility** is the option: $0.7B → ~$2.5B (~30% CAGR), margin -7% → +8%. Goes from drag to contributor. Not a thesis driver, but flips from headwind to tailwind by FY28.

**Manufacturing footprint.** ~280 manufacturing locations globally; key NA electrical sites include Houston TX (medium-voltage switchgear, expanding), Cleveland OH (LV products), Sumter SC (transformers, expanding), Reynosa Mexico (MV components), Lincoln IL (UPS), Pittsburgh PA, Nacogdoches TX, and Highland IL. Aerospace concentrated in Jackson MS, Costa Rica, Bedford UK.

## 3. AI buildout exposure

Eaton **does** disclose data-center exposure as a vertical inside Electrical Americas + Global. Working from disclosed and implied figures:

- **FY25 data-center revenue: ~$4.4–4.6B**, or roughly **17–19% of total company revenue** ^[extracted from analyst-day commentary and segment breakouts; management has guided "data center is mid-to-high teens % of revenue and the fastest-growing vertical"].
- Within Electrical Americas, data center is the largest single end-market at ~30% of segment revenue and growing 25%+ Y/Y; commercial/institutional is ~25%, industrial ~20%, residential ~15%, utility ~10%.
- Within Electrical Global, data center is ~20% of segment revenue, growing in the high-teens.
- Aerospace + Vehicle + eMobility have effectively no AI-DC exposure (Aerospace has some hyperscaler-edge / military-DC exposure but it's <5% of segment).

**Net AI-DC exposure heading into FY26:** ~17–19% of revenue today, heading toward **~22–25%** by FY28 as data-center orders disproportionately drive growth ^[inferred]. This is materially less concentrated than Vertiv (~75% AI-DC), Powell Industries (~50% AI-DC) or Modine (~40%), but materially more concentrated than Schneider (~12%) or Hubbell (~5%). **The right way to size Eaton's AI exposure is "the largest dollar amount among diversified electrical-equipment names" — Eaton's $4.5B of data-center revenue is bigger than POWL's entire company.**

**Product-level AI-DC exposure.**
- LV switchgear (Magnum, Power Defense): direct sale to hyperscalers and their EPCs.
- MV switchgear (VacClad-W, MEB): the gating product for data-center substations; lead times pushed out to 50–80 weeks at peak in 2024–2025, normalizing into 2026.
- Busway (Pow-R-Way III, Pow-R-Line): rapid ramp as hyperscalers move to 415V/480V busway distribution inside the data hall.
- UPS (93PM, BladeUPS, 9395XC): mid-tier share vs. Vertiv (Liebert) and Schneider (Galaxy). Strongest in colos and Tier 2 hyperscalers.
- PDU / RPP: standard data-hall distribution, high attach rate.
- Modular / pre-fab data-center power skids (NordicEPOD, Resilient, Fibrebond): the strategic prize — Eaton can ship factory-built power blocks to a hyperscaler greenfield site, compressing schedule by 6–12 months. **This is where Eaton differentiates structurally vs. component-only competitors.**
- Brightlayer Data Centers software: DCIM-adjacent monitoring/management. Software attach is small today but margin-accretive.

**Data-center vertical specifically.** Management commentary across Q2/Q3/Q4 2025 calls and the Mar 2025 Investor Day:
- Data center orders growing **30%+ Y/Y** for six consecutive quarters.
- "Visibility extends into 2027 across most product lines."
- Hyperscalers placing **multi-year frame orders** rather than project-by-project (a structural shift; locks in capacity, normalizes Eaton's revenue cadence, makes margin discipline easier).
- Lead times have peaked but remain elevated: MV switchgear 40–60 weeks (down from 60–80 in 2024), LV switchgear 24–36 weeks, busway 16–24 weeks. As lead times normalize, Eaton expects a modest book-to-bill compression but does NOT expect order cancellations because hyperscalers have signed up for capacity allocations under contract.

**Utility T&D / grid hardening.** Less levered than Hubbell or GE Vernova, but Eaton's MV/HV products (vacuum interrupters, recloser controls, distribution automation) participate in IIJA-funded utility grid spending. Run-rate utility revenue ~$1.5B and growing high single-digits.

**Residential / commercial.** ~30% of Electrical Americas. Cyclical exposure but offset by code-mandated panel upgrades (200A → 400A for EV/heat-pump loads), which are a multi-year refresh cycle. Eaton owns the BR/CH residential breaker franchise (alongside Square D / Schneider) and has high share with the Big Box channel (Home Depot, Lowe's). Channel commentary suggests residential POS bottomed in mid-2025 and is now gradually recovering as housing affordability improves and EV/heat-pump tax credits drive panel upgrades.

**EV charging infrastructure.** Eaton is a Tier-1 supplier of charging-site upstream equipment (transformers, switchgear, distribution) and a participant in DC-fast charging hardware via eMobility. NEVI program funding, fleet electrification, and corporate/retail charging buildouts are slow-moving but durable. Modest contributor to FY26–FY27 growth, but a meaningful FY28+ tailwind once charging deployments scale. **Eaton's edge is selling the *site* electrical scope, not just the chargers themselves** — a $2M charging site has ~$300–500K of upstream Eaton content.

## 5. Supply / capacity — where the bottleneck is

Eaton has been investing aggressively to expand capacity ahead of the curve. Announced and underway capex programs (FY24–FY27):

| Site | Product | Investment | Online | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Houston, TX | MV switchgear | ~$340M total program | Phase 1 mid-2024, Phase 2 2026 | Phase 1 ramping; Phase 2 on schedule |
| Cleveland, OH | LV switchgear | ~$70M | 2024–2025 | Online |
| Sumter, SC | Distribution transformers | ~$110M | 2025 | Online; phase 2 in eval |
| Lincoln, IL | UPS / 93PM | ~$50M | 2025 | Online |
| Reynosa, MX | MV components | ~$80M | 2025 | Online |
| Newton Aycliffe, UK | LV switchgear (EMEA) | ~$60M | 2026 | In construction |
| Modesto, CA (Fibrebond integration) | Modular power buildings | ~$45M (capex in support of acquisition) | 2025–2026 | Integrating |

Total electrical capex 2024–2027: **~$1.5B+** of announced expansion, on top of maintenance capex.

**Capacity / lead time framework.** Eaton's MV switchgear capacity in NA has effectively doubled from 2022 to mid-2026 once Houston Phase 2 fully ramps. Even with this doubling, management has signaled the market is still demand-bound for at least 18–24 more months. **The bull case for ETN is that capacity ramp converts directly into incremental revenue at higher-than-corporate margins because the new lines are sold out before they finish commissioning.**

**Margin expansion runway (2030 plan).** From the March 2025 Investor Day refresh:
- FY25 starting point: 22.4% adj. op margin
- FY30 target: **23–25%** (low-end conservative)
- Drivers: (a) mix to data-center/mega-project (~+50–100bps), (b) fixed-cost absorption on new capex (~+50bps), (c) Brightlayer software attach (~+30–50bps), (d) Aerospace aftermarket leverage (~+20–30bps), (e) Vehicle ICE prune (~+30–50bps), partially offset by (f) eMobility ramp drag (-20–30bps near term).

Long-term incremental margin guidance: **35%+** on Electrical Americas, **30%+** on Electrical Global. These are the metrics to track every quarter.

**Why the new capacity will hit the P&L at higher margins than the existing base:**
1. New lines are dedicated to data-center / mega-project SKUs (higher ASP, higher mix).
2. Capex was justified at hurdle returns (mid-teens IRR) at conservative price/volume assumptions; actual realized prices in 2024–2026 ran 10–20% above the originally underwritten case.
3. Greenfield labor productivity (Houston Phase 2) is structurally higher than the 30–50-year-old brownfield Cleveland/Pittsburgh footprint.
4. Software (Brightlayer) is sold as an attach to the new equipment with very high incremental margin.
5. Modular/pre-fab products (Fibrebond, NordicEPOD) carry premium pricing for schedule compression — hyperscalers pay up for delivery certainty.

**Risk to capacity ramp:** Skilled-labor availability (electricians, controls technicians, cad/cae engineers) and certain long-lead electrical-steel grades (GOES, M3/M4) remain constraints. Eaton has been training its own technicians (Eaton Skills Center, Pittsburgh) and locking multi-year electrical-steel supply contracts to derisk both inputs.

### 7d. Fair-value range (5-year price target band)

Base case: $19.30 FY30 EPS × 24x = **$463**, plus ~$25 of cumulative dividends = **~$490 total return over 5 years from $382**, ~28% total return, ~5.1% annualized — **but** the base case understates the compounding benefit because earnings growth is back-end loaded and the market typically prices forward.

Better framing: **Year-3 (FY28) fair value $15.50 × 26x = $403, plus dividends $13** = ~$416 by mid-2028 → from $382 today, that's the bear-acceptable IRR. Year-5 (FY30) base fair value $463–490. Bull case: $24.80 × 28x = **$695** → ~85% total return.

**Asymmetric 5-year return profile:**
- Bear: $300–340 (down 10–20%)
- Base: $460–500 (up 20–30%)
- Bull: $650–720 (up 70–90%)

Probability-weighted (15%/55%/30%): expected price ~$510, expected total return ~33%. Annualized ~5.9% **plus** dividend ~1.1% → ~7.0% annualized base expected return. **The case for Eaton is not "biggest 5-year return in the basket" — POWL or GEV beat that. The case is "highest probability of compounding 8–12% annualized with the lowest drawdown risk."**

**Sanity check vs. consensus:** Sell-side consensus FY26 EPS ~$12.20, FY27 ~$13.50, FY28 ~$15.00 ^[extracted, FactSet-style aggregate]. My base case is roughly in line through FY27 and slightly above consensus by FY28+ on the assumption that mega-project execution accelerates. Bull case requires consensus to migrate higher over the next 4–6 quarters as orders sustain.

**What's already in the price.** At ~31x NTM P/E, the market is paying a premium to the 5-year average (~22x) and to industrial peers. The market is pricing roughly base-case execution through FY27 and partial credit for FY28+. **The repriceable element is duration of growth, not magnitude** — if the AI-DC capex cycle proves to be 8+ years instead of 3–4, Eaton re-rates to a 28–30x terminal multiple rather than mean-reverting to 22x. That single multiple expansion is worth ~$80–120 per share over 5 years.

## 9. Bear case

Five things that could break the thesis (in rough order of probability):

1. **Cyclical electrical-equipment normalization.** Order growth has run 15–20% Y/Y; at some point that decelerates to mid-single-digits. If hyperscaler capex digests in 2027 (the GEV/POWL bear case), Eaton's data-center orders fall to flat-to-low-single-digit growth, mix dilutes, margin compresses 100–150bps, and the stock de-rates 4–6 multiple turns. Outcome: $300–340.

2. **Margin mean-reversion.** 22.4% adj. op margin is a 50-year high. Mean-reversion to ~20% (still above pre-COVID levels) on either price normalization, mix dilution, or input-cost pressure (copper, GOES electrical steel, semiconductors for controls) takes ~$1.50–2.00 off EPS. Combined with #1, this is the "garden variety industrial cyclical roll" outcome.

3. **Mega-project execution slip.** Of the $2.0T announced, only 13% has executed. If macro turns or financing costs stay high, projects get delayed, deferred, or canceled. Eaton's backlog converts but new bookings slow. The stock would react less than POWL or GEV in this scenario because backlog buys 18+ months of cushion — but the multiple compresses.

4. **Aerospace cyclical roll.** Commercial aerospace is mid-cycle with risk of an OEM rate slowdown (737 MAX delivery cap, A320 supply-chain hiccups, defense budget pressures). Aerospace EBIT is ~15% of company EBIT; a 15% segment EBIT decline is ~$0.30 of EPS.

5. **Vehicle / eMobility drag.** Vehicle ICE is in secular decline. eMobility is investment-mode and burning cash. Together they're ~12% of revenue and ~6% of EBIT. The bear case is they get worse before they get better — eMobility ramp delays, Vehicle ICE volume drops faster than productivity offsets. ~$0.20–0.40 EPS drag.

**Compounded bear case (multiple bullets hit simultaneously):** EPS FY27 ~$11.50 (vs. base $13.80), multiple 22x → $253. **Hard floor in a recession scenario: ~$240–270, ~30–35% drawdown.** This is the position-sizing constraint.

**What would change my mind on this thesis (kill criteria):**
- Two consecutive quarters of negative orders growth in Electrical Americas
- Margin compression >150bps Y/Y in Electrical Americas without a clear mix or one-time explanation
- Hyperscaler capex guides cut by >15% from MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN
- Mega-projects tracker rolls over (new announcements <$50B per quarter, or cancellations >$50B per quarter)
- Loss of share at a major hyperscaler frame contract

## 10. Position sizing

**Profile:** Mega-cap industrial. Beta ~1.10 (2-year). Liquid (~$1.5B ADV). Dividend aristocrat. S&P 500 / S&P 100 / Dow constituent (added Mar 2024).

**Role in portfolio:** **Anchor position in the electrical-equipment basket.** Eaton is the diversifier and the compounder — lower drawdown risk than POWL or VRT, broader moat than GEV, more growth than HUBB or SU.PA. Sized as a "core" rather than a "trading" position.

**Pairs trade:** ETN (mega-cap, broad, lower-beta) paired with **POWL** (small-cap, concentrated, higher-beta) gives barbell exposure to the same thesis. ETN is the high-floor leg; POWL is the high-ceiling leg. Roughly 2–3x the dollar weight of POWL in ETN if both are held.

**Pairs trade vs. GEV:** ETN is the "electron-side" anchor; GEV is the "molecule-side / generation" anchor. They are complementary, not redundant. ETN sells into utility T&D; GEV sells turbines, grid HV equipment, and wind/nuclear. Holding both is the cleanest two-name way to own the full electrical infrastructure stack.

**Pairs trade vs. VRT:** ETN is the broader, more diversified version of VRT's data-center thesis. If you have to pick one, ETN over VRT for a 5-year hold (lower concentration risk, broader product set, dividend, diversification into Aerospace). VRT is the better trade if the AI-DC capex cycle accelerates near-term.

**Sizing framework for a long-only book:**
- Base allocation: ETN at 4–6% of total portfolio if running concentrated; 2–3% if running diversified
- Add on: 25%+ pullbacks (e.g. $300 or below) → average down to up to 8% of book
- Trim on: parabolic moves to >$550 without earnings catch-up, or kill-criteria triggers

**Tax / structure note:** ETN is Irish-domiciled but trades as a U.S. NYSE ADR-equivalent. Dividends withheld at U.S. federal rates only (not Irish) for U.S. taxpayers — confirm with broker. No foreign-tax-credit complexity for most U.S. holders.

**Drawdown management:** Stop-loss is not appropriate for a 5-year compounder. Instead, monitor the kill criteria (Section 9) quarterly. Re-underwrite the thesis at every Q1 print (the cleanest quarter for orders trajectory) and at every Investor Day. Trim — not exit — on multiple expansion to >35x NTM P/E without commensurate earnings revisions; re-add on pullbacks to <25x NTM.
