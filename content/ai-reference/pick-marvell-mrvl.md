## 1. Investment thesis

1. **Custom AI ASIC franchise — the second of two scaled merchant-silicon shops.** Marvell shepherds the production chips that power AWS Trainium (T2 in volume in 2026, residual T3 interface IP), Microsoft Maia (Maia 2 ramping in calendar 2026 on 3nm; Maia 3 design-won, planned production CY27, ramp CY28), Meta SmartNIC, HBM4 advanced-logic chips, and 18 active production sockets across 10+ customers. Per management's June 2025 Custom AI Investor Event, the pipeline is now **50+ custom engagements** with **$75B of lifetime revenue potential**. ^[extracted]
2. **Optical DSP roadmap is the durable second leg.** Marvell shipped the industry's first 200G/lane (1.6T) PAM4 DSP — Nova in 5nm (2023), Ara in 3nm (2024) — and at OFC 2026 expanded the family to Ara X / Ara T / Petra / Aquila M, sampling Q1 CY26. Management on the Q4 FY26 call: **"We are the first company to productize 200 gigabit per lane technology, enabling the 1.6T transition now underway"** and **"we expect our 1.6T revenue to ramp very rapidly in fiscal 2027, with substantial additional growth projected in fiscal 2028."** Interconnect business guided to **>50% Y/Y** growth in FY27, well above the prior 30% expectation. ^[extracted]
3. **Data Center segment is now ~75% of revenue and accelerating.** FY26 data center revenue surpassed **$6B and grew 46% Y/Y**; Q4 FY26 data center was $1.65B, 74% of total. Management guides FY27 data center growth ~40% Y/Y vs. ~10% for Communications & Other — i.e. mix tilts further into AI. The non-AI ballast (carrier/enterprise/auto/industrial — now lumped into "Communications and Other") is no longer the story; the company has effectively become a Data Center pure play with a low-margin tail. ^[extracted]
4. **SemiAnalysis bottleneck call — the Patel-flagged supply constraint.** SemiAnalysis has repeatedly framed custom-ASIC physical-design bandwidth (Marvell + Alchip + AVGO + GUC) and 1.6T DSP allocation as the binding 2026-27 constraints on hyperscaler scale-out. The flip side: Patel/SemiAnalysis publicly graded Marvell **"the big loser"** on the Trainium 3 socket loss to Alchip ([SemiAnalysis: AWS Trainium 3 Deep Dive](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/aws-trainium3-deep-dive-a-potential)) — but that loss was largely priced into the December 2025 / March 2026 sell-off and the market is now re-rating on (a) Maia 2/3 confirmed at Microsoft, (b) Meta SmartNIC, (c) the 1.6T DSP cycle, (d) Nvidia's $2B strategic NVLink Fusion partnership investment. The "Marvell DSP Dilemma" SemiAnalysis piece argues the optical business is the **"more durable and predictable engine"** even as networking incumbents try to design Marvell out of switch silicon. ^[extracted/inferred]
5. **Through-cycle EPS step-change.** FY26 non-GAAP EPS $2.84 (a record) on 59.5% non-GAAP gross margin. Management's framework is gross margin holding 59-60% even as low-margin custom-ASIC silicon mixes up (volume-rich, content-rich, but ASP/cost compressed vs. legacy DSP). Base-case FY27 ~$3.40 non-GAAP EPS and FY28 ~$5.00 → at a 35-40x multiple (peer-band for AVGO custom-silicon segment) implies $175-200 12m fair value. The asymmetric upside is mix-driven: each $1B of incremental Maia/Trainium volume drops ~$0.30-0.40 of non-GAAP EPS through the cost base. ^[inferred]

## 2. Business overview

### What Marvell sells

Marvell is a fabless data-infrastructure semiconductor company. Beginning Q4 FY26 the company collapsed reporting into two segments — **Data Center** and **Communications and Other** — because Data Center is now the entire growth story.

Under the hood the product portfolio is:

- **Custom Silicon / XPU & Attach.** Hyperscaler-funded custom designs — AI accelerators (XPUs: Trainium, Maia), data-processing units (DPUs / SmartNICs), HBM4 advanced-logic chips, custom CPUs (Microsoft Cobalt-adjacent), specialized fabric switching. ~$1.5B FY26 revenue; doubled Y/Y; management plans **>20% Y/Y FY27, doubles in FY28, $2B+ run-rate by FY29 from NIC and CXL alone**. ^[extracted]
- **Electro-Optics (DSPs, TIAs, Drivers, CDRs).** Optical DSPs at 50G/100G/200G per lane PAM4 — Nova / Ara / Ara X / Ara T / Petra family — and coherent DSPs (Aquila family) for DCI / metro. This was the **Inphi acquisition asset** (closed 2021); SemiAnalysis frames it as Marvell's most transformative deal of the past five years. ^[extracted]
- **Switch silicon (Teralynx, Prestera).** Merchant Ethernet switch ASICs at 12.8T-51.2T. The smallest of the data-center sub-segments; under direct attack from Broadcom Tomahawk and the hyperscaler-custom switch trend SemiAnalysis flagged in the "DSP Dilemma" piece.
- **Storage.** SSD controllers, fibre-channel, NVMe-over-Fabric. Mature; cyclical; not a growth driver.
- **Carrier / Enterprise Networking, Automotive (residual).** Legacy MACsec, copper PHYs, base-station processors, and a small auto residual after the 2025 divestiture of the auto-Ethernet business. Now reported inside "Communications and Other."

## 4. Demand drivers — Patel-cited bottleneck names

This is the section to underwrite carefully because it's where the SemiAnalysis call directly informs the thesis.

### Custom ASIC physical design — the Patel "bottleneck" frame

Per SemiAnalysis ([Marvell Deep Dive 2022](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/marvelldeepdive2022) and follow-ups), the world has roughly **four scaled merchant custom-ASIC physical-design houses**: Broadcom (the gorilla), Marvell (the #2 with Inphi-derived optical depth), Alchip (the rapidly-rising #3 — won Trainium 3), and GUC (Taiwan; smaller). Apple's silicon team and the in-house hyperscaler design groups (Google's TPU team, Microsoft's Athena/Maia team) consume their own bandwidth.

Patel's running thesis: **the supply of competent ASIC backend / package / tapeout teams that can land a 3nm/2nm AI accelerator on schedule is the single tightest constraint on the 2026-27 hyperscaler buildout.** That constraint is structural — TSMC capacity is being unblocked but human capital in advanced-node physical design is not. Marvell sits inside that bottleneck whether it's winning specific sockets or not, and even Trainium 3 — which Marvell *lost* — still pays Marvell residual interface-IP royalties. ^[inferred from SemiAnalysis framing]

### Trainium 2 ramp (2025-2026)

- AWS shipped Trainium 2 in volume across CY25; FY26 shows the production-silicon revenue tail. SemiAnalysis ([Amazon's AI Self Sufficiency](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/amazons-ai-self-sufficiency-trainium2-architecture-networking)) detailed the T2 architecture and networking and identified Marvell as the design partner.
- Mgmt on Q4 FY26 call: AWS revenue has "no air pockets" through FY26 — i.e. T2 production volume holds up even as T3 design moves elsewhere.

### Trainium 3 (2026-2027) — the loss everyone misunderstands

- Marvell lost the T3 backend physical-design socket to Alchip — confirmed in [SemiAnalysis: AWS Trainium 3 Deep Dive](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/aws-trainium3-deep-dive-a-potential). Cause: T2 schedule slip, RDL interposer issues, and a chiplet-vs-monolithic architectural mismatch with the spec AWS chose.
- Patel's written assessment was unsubtle: Marvell is **"the big loser"** ([TipRanks summary](https://www.tipranks.com/news/the-fly/amazon-launches-trainium-3-marvell-ends-up-big-loser-semianalysis-says-thefly)).
- **What's actually lost:** T3 design fees + per-die NRE + a piece of the per-unit royalty/tape-out economics. Estimated revenue impact: **$300-700M of FY27-28 revenue we would otherwise have modeled** ^[inferred].
- **What's not lost:** AWS as a customer (Marvell still ships Trainium 2 + storage + DPU + optical content); Microsoft Maia 2/3; Meta SmartNIC; the 1.6T optical DSP into AWS racks. The Trainium-3 loss is a single-socket loss, not a customer loss.
- **Why it's already in the price:** the stock fell from ~$110 in November 2025 to ~$78 in December 2025 on the news ([TradingNews recap](https://www.tradingnews.com/news/marvell-stock-price-forecast-mrvl-at-78-usd-ai-reset)) before recovering to $156 by end-April 2026 as Maia 2/3 and the 1.6T cycle re-asserted.

### Microsoft Maia DSP / custom silicon — the offset

- Per JPMorgan and trade-press confirmation: **Maia 2 (Gen 2, 3nm) on track to ramp CY26**; **Maia 3 (Gen 3, 2nm/3nm chiplet, 3DSOIC) design-won and in early design** with production CY27 / ramp CY28 ([Benzinga / JPM](https://www.benzinga.com/25/05/45294370/marvells-custom-ai-chips-for-microsoft-and-aws-stay-on-track-analyst-sees-strong-growth-ahead)).
- Patel's framing of Microsoft's silicon ([Hyperscaler AI Custom Chips](https://www.datagravity.dev/p/hyperscaler-ai-custom-chips-asic)): Microsoft is the most ambitious of the hyperscalers in pulling silicon design in-house and uses Marvell's PAM DSP for networking; the Maia program is now Microsoft's flagship accelerator.
- Maia is plausibly **larger** than Trainium 2 over its lifetime because Microsoft's capex run-rate is now greater than AWS's, and because Maia 3 layers on top of Maia 2 rather than replacing it.

### 1.6T optical DSP — the durable second engine

- 200G/lane (1.6T-class) PAM4 DSPs are the gating component for the next generation of pluggable transceivers. Marvell's portfolio:
  - **Nova** — 5nm, 2023, the original 200G/lane PAM4 DSP. ^[extracted]
  - **Ara** — 3nm, 2024, integrates 8x 200G electrical + 8x 200G optical. Won "Interconnect Product of the Year" and Leading EDGE Award. ^[extracted]
  - **Ara X / Ara T / Petra / Aquila M** — sampling Q1 CY26. ^[extracted]
  - **1.6T LPO chipset** — released for short-reach scale-up fabric. ^[extracted]
- Mgmt: 1.6T revenue **"ramps very rapidly in fiscal 2027, with substantial additional growth projected in fiscal 2028."** ^[extracted]
- SemiAnalysis ("Marvell DSP Dilemma") framed the 1.6T optical business as **"the more durable and predictable engine"** even while flagging the longer-term competitive pressure from Broadcom + Macom + Nvidia's vertical-integration efforts. The 12-month investment window sits *before* that competitive pressure shows up in earnings; Marvell's ASP advantage at 200G/lane is real for FY27. ^[extracted/inferred]
- Co-packaged optics (CPO) for custom XPUs — Marvell announced a CPO architecture for custom AI accelerators (Jan 2025). The Polariton acquisition (April 2026) layers plasmonics-based silicon photonics into the roadmap, scaling toward 3.2T and beyond ([Polariton press release](https://investor.marvell.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1020/marvell-announces-acquisition-of-polariton-technologies-advancing-optical-performance-scaling-to-3-2t-and-beyond)). ^[extracted]

### Why this matters for the 12-month window

The asymmetry is: **the bear case (Trainium 3 loss + AVGO encroachment) is already in consensus** while the bull case (Maia 2 production ramp, Maia 3 win, 1.6T cycle, 50+ engagement pipeline, Nvidia partnership) hits prints in FY27 — i.e. between May 2026 and March 2027. The investment window is long the resolution of that asymmetry.

## 5. Supply / capacity

- **Fabless on TSMC.** All advanced-node silicon is TSMC: 5nm for Nova / Trainium 2 / Maia 1; 3nm for Ara / Maia 2 / next-gen Maia 3 chiplet pieces; 2nm allocations for the late-decade wave (Maia 3 chiplet / Trainium-class follow-ons that Marvell still has shots at). ^[inferred]
- **Capacity = TSMC allocation for advanced-node tape-outs.** Marvell as a hyperscaler-shepherd typically commits TSMC reservations on customer behalf as part of the design contract, so capacity risk is largely passed through. The bottleneck in 2026-27 is **CoWoS-S/L advanced packaging** (which Marvell shares with NVDA, AVGO, AMD) and **HBM allocation**. SemiAnalysis has been the loudest voice on CoWoS as the binding constraint. ^[inferred]
- **Lead times:** 26-52 weeks for advanced-node tape-outs; 9-13 months from physical-design freeze to production silicon. Trainium 2's slip — the cause of the Trainium 3 loss — illustrated how tight that window is. ^[inferred]
- **Gross margin trajectory:**
  - FY24 non-GAAP GM: ~62%
  - FY25 non-GAAP GM: ~60%
  - FY26 non-GAAP GM: 59.5% (Q4 59.0%)
  - Q1 FY27 guide: 58.25-59.25% non-GAAP
  - Long-term framework: ~59-60% as custom mix scales (custom is structurally lower-margin than merchant DSP — high revenue per chip but pass-through silicon economics)
  - Bull read: 60%+ if 1.6T DSP mix stays rich and custom doesn't compress harder than expected; bear read: 56-58% if custom mix accelerates and merchant pricing pressure intensifies. ^[extracted/inferred]
- **R&D:** non-GAAP R&D ~$430M/quarter Q4 FY26; FY26 total ~$1.7B. Tapeout cadence (3nm Ara, 5nm/3nm Maia 2, 3nm Trainium 2, HBM4 advanced logic) drives the cost base. Each modern tapeout: $40-60M+. ^[inferred]
- **Capex:** Fabless-light. Capex ~3% of revenue (~$240M FY26, mostly test infrastructure). ^[inferred]

## 7. Valuation framework

### Approach 1 — Sum-of-the-parts

The right way to value MRVL is to split the Data Center segment from "everything else" and assign different multiples.

**Data Center segment** (FY27E ~$8.4B at +40% growth, ~62% GM):
- AVGO networking-silicon segment trades ~13-15x EV/Sales as a custom-ASIC peer. ^[inferred]
- NVDA at ~12-14x EV/Sales (premium for ecosystem). MRVL deserves a discount to NVDA on growth and a discount to AVGO on customer concentration. Apply **9-12x EV/Sales** → $76-101B segment value.

**Communications and Other** (FY27E ~$2.6B at +10%, lower margin):
- Industrial / mature analog / networking peer band: **2-3x EV/Sales** → $5-8B segment value.

**Sum:**
- Bear: $76B + $5B - $1.8B net debt = **$79B → ~$92/share**
- Base: $90B + $7B - $1.8B = **$95B → ~$111/share** (from FY27 numbers; rolls forward as FY28 lands)
- Bull: $101B + $8B - $1.8B = **$107B → ~$125/share** (FY27); rolling onto FY28 base of $14.5B at 11x DC = $145B+ → $170+/share

The 12-month target should anchor on FY28-rolled SOTP because that's what the market will be discounting in mid-2026. Rolling base case onto FY28: $14.5B × 75% × 11x DC + $3.6B × 25% × 2.5x = ~$130B + ~$9B → ~$140B → **~$163/share base case**.

### Approach 2 — Forward P/E

- FY27 base-case non-GAAP EPS $3.40. At **40x** (a fair multiple for a 30%+ grower with high data-center mix and moderate beta), implied price **$136**. At **50x**, implied **$170**. At **60x** (current premium maintained on Maia 3 win narrative), implied **$204**.
- FY28 base-case non-GAAP EPS $5.00. At **35x** (decelerating growth) → **$175**.

### Approach 3 — DCF cross-check

Inputs:
- FY27-FY31 revenue per base case
- Terminal operating margin 33% non-GAAP, taxed at 17%
- Discount rate (WACC) 9.5% — beta ~1.5, lower customer-concentration premium than CRDO
- Terminal multiple 16x exit FCF (year 10)
- Net debt $1.8B (subtract)

Output: DCF fair value ~$155-180/share base; ~$95 bear; ~$220 bull.

### Triangulated 12-month fair-value range

- **Bear:** $75-95 (Trainium 3 loss + Maia 2 slip + 1.6T share loss → FY27 EPS $2.80 at 30x P/E + SOTP floor)
- **Base:** $135-170 (FY27 ~$11B at 10-11x DC EV/Sales; ~40-50x P/E on FY27 EPS $3.40; ~$5 FY28 EPS at 35x)
- **Bull:** $180-220 (Maia 3 design rev recognition + 1.6T DSP holds 60% share + Google socket conversion → FY28 EPS $5.80+ at 38x)

**Single-figure 12m target:** **~$170** — implies ~8% upside from $157 in base, with ~30% upside in bull and ~45% downside in bear. The risk-reward is asymmetric to the upside *because the bear case (Trainium 3 loss) is already in the December 2025 print*; the next 12 months are about discovering whether Maia 2/3 + 1.6T offsets the lost socket faster than consensus.

## 9. Bear case

The shortest version: **the merchant custom-silicon model is fragile when hyperscalers can buy backend services from Alchip / GUC at lower NRE and keep the IP themselves.**

1. **Customer concentration in custom is real even if disguised.** Microsoft Maia + AWS Trainium 2 likely represent ~70-80% of the $1.5B FY26 custom revenue. A push-out from either is a single-quarter 15-25% miss to data-center growth.
2. **AVGO is the structurally better custom-silicon house.** Broadcom has the longer relationships (Google TPU since 2016, Meta MTIA since first gen), the deeper packaging IP, and ~3x the R&D budget. SemiAnalysis has framed AVGO as the **gorilla** of merchant custom silicon. Every Marvell loss to AVGO is a permanent share shift.
3. **Trainium 3 was the first big public socket loss; more may follow.** If Maia 3 schedule slips and Microsoft entertains second-sourcing to Alchip or GUC, the Trainium-3 narrative repeats. The Benchmark downgrade ([Sherwood News, Dec 2025](https://sherwood.news/markets/marvell-sinks-after-benchmark-cuts-company-saying-that-it-lost-its-amazon/)) was explicit on this concern.
4. **CPO accelerates and disrupts pluggable DSP demand.** If NVDA Quantum-X CPO and AVGO Tomahawk 6-CPO ramp in volume in late 2026 / early 2027, the 1.6T pluggable DSP TAM caps lower than the bull case requires. Marvell's CPO + Polariton roadmap is a hedge but the revenue offset doesn't land in FY27. SemiAnalysis "DSP Dilemma" is the canonical bear read here.
5. **Networking incumbents are designing Marvell out.** Per SemiAnalysis: Broadcom, Nvidia, Arista, Microsoft, Meta, and Macom are all rallying to design Marvell out of switch silicon. Switch revenue is small but margin-accretive; pricing pressure on Teralynx is real.
6. **ASIC commoditization.** As more hyperscalers spin up internal silicon teams, the "merchant ASIC shepherd" role compresses to a packaging-services fee. Per-die economics shrink even if revenue doesn't.
7. **Macro semi cycle.** Marvell still has ~25% of revenue in cyclical Comms/Other. Carrier and enterprise networking are not insulated from a 2027 capex pause.
8. **Multiple compression on growth deceleration.** A 30% Y/Y grower trading at ~36x forward P/E re-rates fast if FY27 prints below $11B. The math: at 25% Y/Y with 18% FCF margin, "fair" multiple is ~25-30x — implies $85-100. From $157, bear math is $90-95 on a Q2 or Q3 FY27 miss.
