This is investment research, not financial advice. The page is a scorecard, not a recommendation list. Multiples, backlogs, and estimate-revision directions are pulled as of 2026-04-30 ± 1 month and will move with prints, guidance updates, and tape action. The structural columns — bucket, bottleneck controlled, AI/DC revenue exposure, customer concentration — are more durable than the valuation columns and are the better lens for thesis durability. Cells marked `n/d` are not disclosed by the company at the level of granularity required; cells marked `n/a` are not a meaningful metric for that name (typical of pre-EBITDA neoclouds and miners with HPC pivots).

## How to read this scorecard

- **Bottleneck controlled** is the single most important column. A name with no scarce asset is just an AI narrative.
- **Estimate-revision direction (12m)** matters more than the absolute multiple. A 30x P/E with rising estimates compresses faster than a 15x P/E with falling estimates. `↑` = consensus 2026/2027 EPS revised up over the trailing 12 months; `→` = flat; `↓` = down.
- **Purity discounts the multiple needed**. A pure-play (Vertiv, Modine data center segment, neoclouds) commands a higher multiple before it is "expensive" than a diversified industrial with 10% data center exposure.
- **Backlog or book-to-bill** > 1.0 with sequential growth is the cleanest leading indicator the bottleneck thesis is working. Watch sequential change, not just absolute level.

## Highest-conviction by bucket

- **Electrical**: ETN and SU.PA — only platforms able to deliver complete prefab electrical systems at hyperscale; backlog growth confirms; Boyd Thermal acq adds liquid-cooling content per MW. POWL is the small-cap pure expression.
- **Power gen**: GEV — gas turbine slots are the binding constraint on time-to-power and backlog visibility now extends to 2027+. ENR.DE is the European pair-trade with comparable Grid Tech profile.
- **Memory**: MU and 000660.KS — HBM/DRAM essentially sold out 2026; structural under-supply continues into 2027. Multiple is still cyclical-anchored despite secular setup.
- **Semis/equip**: ASML (EUV monopoly + €38.8B backlog) and BESI (hybrid bonding pure-play). For broader exposure, NVDA remains the volume engine but multiple compression risk is highest if hyperscaler capex wobbles.
- **Optics/networking**: COHR (vertically integrated, datacom + indium phosphide) and ANET (only credible Ethernet alternative to Nvidia networking at scale).
- **Cooling**: VRT (the only liquid-cooling pure play with $12.45B backlog) and MOD (highest growth purity at smaller cap).
- **EPC**: PWR (utility T&D + DC scope) and EME (cleanest DC backlog disclosure with 32% RPO growth).
- **Neocloud / power-first**: NBIS (cleanest balance sheet; $50B contracted backlog) and IREN (Microsoft-anchored 5-yr GPU cloud deal). CRWV is the largest but most leveraged.
- **Utilities/IPPs**: CEG and TLN — nuclear PPAs are the scarcest power product hyperscalers can buy; Vistra is the gas-and-nuclear pair.
- **Second-order**: FCX (copper deficit thesis), KMI (gas pipeline backlog tied to DC-driven gen).

## Caveats and disclosures

Multiples shown are NTM consensus where extractable, otherwise approximated from public commentary; they will move with every print and tape close. Backlogs are reported with a one-quarter lag and definitions vary (RPOs vs orders vs reservation agreements vs slot reservations are not directly comparable). 5-yr median multiples are eyeballed from public charts and labelled `n/m` for post-IPO or post-spin names where the history is too short. Customer concentration is rarely disclosed at top-1; many entries are inferred from SEC filings and conference commentary and labelled `^[ambiguous]`. Net leverage is approximated from the most recent 10-Q and excludes operating leases. Private-company peers (Schneider's private competitors in DCIM, private cooling specialists, private gas-turbine packagers, private contract miners) are out of scope. This is investment research, not a recommendation; do your own work.
