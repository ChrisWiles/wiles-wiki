## 1. Investment thesis

The 12-month case rests on five linked claims, in declining order of conviction:

1. **Power scarcity is structural, not cyclical, and GEV is the cleanest US-listed pure-play on it.** Q1 2026 took total gas-turbine equipment backlog plus slot-reservation agreements to **100 GW** across **90 customers in 24 countries**, up from 83 GW at year-end 2025. ^[extracted] Management guided to "at least 110 GW" of combined book by year-end 2026 and CEO Strazik stated on the Q1 call that he expects turbine reservations to be **sold out through 2030 by the end of 2026**. ^[extracted] Slot pricing in Q1 2026 was **10–20 points higher per kW** than Q4 2025 orders, on top of pricing that was already up materially through 2025. ^[extracted]
2. **Pricing is rising into a fixed cost base, so incremental margin is dropping to EBITDA at very high rates.** Q1 2026 Power-segment EBITDA margin expanded **+500 bps YoY to 16.3%** and Electrification expanded **+590 bps to 17.8%**. ^[extracted] Total adjusted EBITDA margin of 9.6% (+390 bps) is held back by the Wind drag; ex-Wind run-rate is materially higher.
3. **Prolec GE closed February 2, 2026, immediately accretive even before synergies.** $5.275B price for the remaining 50% of the joint venture, funded ~50/50 cash and a $2.6B senior notes offering. ^[extracted] Prolec is the largest North American large-power-transformer (LPT) franchise — five US plants and additional Mexican capacity, ~10,000 employees — into a market that NIAC has flagged as a national-reliability shortage. Q1 2026 Electrification booked **$2.4B of equipment orders specifically for data centers in one quarter — more than all of FY2025**. ^[extracted]
4. **Capital allocation has decisively turned shareholder-friendly.** Buyback authorization stepped up from $6B to **$10B** in December 2025, dividend doubled from $0.25 to $0.50/quarter, and management has committed to returning **at least one-third of cash generation** to shareholders. ^[extracted] Q1 2026 alone produced **$4.8B of FCF**, exceeding all of FY2025's $3.7B. ^[extracted]
5. **Optionality is not in the price.** BWRX-300 SMR (Hitachi-GE Nuclear JV) has a Darlington, Ontario unit under construction with COD targeted by end of 2029, and TVA's Clinch River construction-permit review is expected to complete by December 2026. ^[extracted] Hyperscaler "volume agreements" potentially extending to 2035 are being negotiated. ^[extracted] None of these are required for the 12-month thesis to work; all are skewed positive.

The 12-month risk/reward is asymmetric because consensus has not yet absorbed the December 2025 / Q1 2026 guidance ratchet (FY2028 revenue target raised from $45B to $52B and EBITDA margin from 14% to 20%). ^[extracted] The bull case is multiple expansion on already-rising estimates; the base case is hitting the new guidance; the bear case is a Wind impairment cycle plus Gas Power margin disappointment.

## 2. Business overview

GE Vernova was spun out of GE on April 2, 2024. Today it operates **three reportable segments**:

### Power
The largest segment by revenue and the thesis core. Includes:
- **Gas Power** — heavy-duty H-class and 7F/9F gas turbines, generators, services, plus aero-derivative LM and TM2500 fast-power units. The H-class fleet crossed three million operating hours in 2025. ^[extracted]
- **Steam Power** — non-nuclear steam turbines and generators. Largely a services-revenue stream on the existing fleet; new-build is small. Schenectady, NY is the steam/generator center of excellence ($41M expansion announced for 2025–2026). ^[extracted]
- **Hydro Power** — global hydro turbines, generators, and digital plant retrofits.
- **Nuclear Services** — services on the BWR fleet plus the BWRX-300 SMR program through GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy.

Q1 2026 Power segment: revenue **$5.0B (+12% YoY)**, orders **$10.0B (+59% YoY)**, segment EBITDA margin **16.3% (+500 bps)**. ^[extracted]

### Wind
Onshore (LM Wind blades, 2.x/3.x/5.x platforms, including the Cypress 6.x) plus Offshore (Haliade-X 14MW). The thesis treats this segment as a **controlled drag**:
- FY2025 Wind segment EBITDA loss ~$400M ^[extracted]
- FY2026 Wind segment EBIT loss guidance ~$400M, including $250–350M of estimated tariff costs ^[extracted]
- Q1 2026: revenue **−25% YoY**, EBITDA loss **$382M** ^[extracted]
- Restructuring program targeting $225M total costs, completion mid-2026 ^[extracted]

Wind is being scaled to demand, not to a growth target. Onshore US new-build remains soft on permitting and tariffs; Offshore is being managed to lower-loss execution rather than re-bid.

### Electrification
The fastest-growing segment and the second pillar of the thesis. Sub-businesses:
- **Grid Solutions** — high-voltage AC/DC switchgear, breakers, and HVDC converters
- **Power Conversion** — drives, motors, large electric machines
- **Solar & Storage Solutions** — utility-scale BESS plus PV inverters
- **Prolec GE** (consolidated as of Feb 2, 2026) — large power transformers, generator step-up units, distribution transformers, transformer services

Q1 2026 Electrification: segment EBITDA margin **17.8% (+590 bps YoY)**, equipment orders **$2.4B for data centers alone in the quarter**. ^[extracted]

### Top-line and geographic shape

- **FY2025 revenue: $38.1B (+9% YoY, organic +9%)** ^[extracted]
- **FY2025 adjusted EBITDA: $3.2B; margin 8.4%** (depressed by Wind) ^[extracted]
- **FY2025 orders: $59.3B (+34% organic)**, driven by gas-power equipment tripling YoY ^[extracted]
- **FY2025 backlog: $150B exiting the year, $163B at end-Q1 2026** (+$13B sequential) ^[extracted]
- **Services share of backlog: >55%**, providing recurring high-margin revenue visibility ^[extracted]
- **Geographic mix (FY2025): US ~50%, non-US ~50%**, with Europe ~13%, Middle East/Africa ~9%, Asia ~8%, Americas ex-US ~5%. ^[extracted]
- **2028 guidance: $52B revenue, 20% adjusted EBITDA margin, $22B+ cumulative 2025-2028 FCF**, total backlog ~$200B with Electrification doubling from $30B → $60B. ^[extracted]

## 3. AI buildout exposure — quantified

GEV's exposure to the AI data-center buildout is concentrated in two segments and one optionality bucket:

### Direct exposure: Gas Power
Of the 100 GW combined turbine book at end-Q1 2026, management explicitly tied **20% (20 GW) to data-center load**, with named customers including AWS, Microsoft, Meta and Crusoe in the data-center-tied bucket. ^[extracted] However, *incremental* slot demand is materially more concentrated in DCs than the cumulative book — the Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 incremental orders skew **30–50% to hyperscaler-backed gas-to-power offtake** ^[inferred] — because hyperscalers are the marginal price-setters paying reservation fees out to 2030 and beyond.

### Direct exposure: Electrification
Q1 2026 Electrification booked **$2.4B of data-center equipment orders in one quarter**, more than all of FY2025 combined. ^[extracted] At a run-rate that order line is approaching half of the segment's total intake. ^[inferred] Prolec GE's LPT slot book is expected to be heavily DC-tied as hyperscalers and the utilities serving them lock in transformer capacity through 2028+. ^[inferred]

### Off-thesis: Wind
Onshore wind is not part of any meaningful hyperscaler PPA mix in 2026; Offshore is even less so. Hyperscalers in 2025–2026 chose firm gas-to-power and grid upgrades over PPA-based renewables for *new* AI data centers, in line with the [[hyperscaler-constraint-map|hyperscaler power constraint map]].

### Optionality: Nuclear / SMR
BWRX-300 has Darlington under construction (COD ~2029) and TVA Clinch River permit review on track for December 2026. ^[extracted] If TVA dockets, that is a direct hyperscaler-adjacent catalyst given Tennessee's data-center demand profile and the implied template for replicated SMR siting at hyperscaler campuses.

**Aggregate AI-DC exposure share of FY2026E revenue: ~25–35% directly, with another ~10–15% in tightly correlated utility-scale gear bought in anticipation of DC load growth.** ^[inferred]

## 5. Supply / capacity

### Heavy-duty gas turbine capacity
- **Greenville, SC** is the H-class assembly hub. Production trajectory: 37 turbines in 2025, **62 in 2026**, **74 in 2027**. ^[extracted]
- **Annualized output: 20 GW by 3Q26**, expanding to **24 GW by 2028** through stretching of the existing two-plant footprint plus a new module-fab line. ^[extracted]
- **Capex commitments**: $160M Greenville investment announced 2025 (650+ jobs added); part of a broader **~$600M US-factory two-year program**. ^[extracted]
- **Hydrogen test capability** is being added at Greenville, signaling readiness for the dual-fuel transition required for late-decade decarbonized DC offtake. ^[extracted]

### Steam Power and generators
- **Schenectady, NY** is the steam/generator center of excellence. $41M / 50-job expansion announced. ^[extracted]
- Steam Power is principally a services-revenue stream against the global fleet rather than a meaningful new-build line.

### Prolec GE / large transformers
- Five US plants plus Mexican capacity (Apodaca, Monterrey area). ^[extracted]
- LPT lead times globally have stretched **40+ months**, with peers (Hitachi Energy, Siemens Energy, Hyundai HI, Mitsubishi Electric, MEPPI) all expanding capacity simultaneously. ^[extracted]
- The Prolec acquisition gives GEV control of pricing on a large fraction of the North American LPT slot book exactly as scarcity peaks.

### Nuclear / SMR
- BWRX-300 first-of-a-kind under construction at Darlington, Ontario; OPG cleared to construct in May 2025. ^[extracted]
- TVA NRC docket open; review expected complete by December 2026; site-prep could begin 2026. ^[extracted]

The bear case sees a fade to $300–500. The bull case sees a continued grind to $900–1,100+.

The 12-month conviction-high frame holds because the **modal path** is:
- 2026 prints inside the raised guidance,
- Multi-year guidance gets ratcheted again at the December 2026 Investor Update,
- Hyperscaler volume agreements (or AWS-style framework deals with one or two more names) close,
- Buyback compounds at $5–10B/yr against rising EPS,

…and any of those four is sufficient to keep the 21x blended forward EBITDA framework intact at higher absolute EBITDA dollars. Multiple compression is the principal risk on a 12-month view; structural demand erosion is not.

## 9. Bear case

The bear case has five components, in order of decreasing probability:

1. **Wind impairment cycle resumes (P ~30%)**. Despite the in-process restructuring, an additional $1–2B of Wind goodwill and asset impairments could land in 2026 or 2027 if Offshore project losses extend or Onshore demand stays soft. The drag is non-cash but optically painful and would compress reported EBITDA margin into year-end. Mitigant: Wind is already largely a "controlled-decline" segment in management's narrative; the market is partially desensitized.

2. **Gas Power margin disappointment (P ~20%)**. The market has priced in steady Gas Power margin expansion. A single quarter of margin disappointment — driven by mix shift to lower-margin equipment vs. services, or supply-chain inflation reabsorbing pricing gains — could trigger a multiple compression of 2–4 turns of forward EBITDA, equating to a 15–25% drawdown.

3. **Hyperscaler capex stall (P ~15%)**. If 1–2 hyperscalers materially trim 2027 capex (whether from training-cost crash, regulatory action, or financial conservatism), the marginal slot-reservation buyer disappears and slot pricing flattens or rolls. The cumulative book is locked in by reservation deposits, but *new* incremental orders would slow. The 12-month price impact would be bigger than the EBITDA impact because the multiple would compress on perceived end-of-cycle.

4. **Electrification multiple compression (P ~15%)**. If transformer market eases (e.g., GOES electrical-steel capacity catches up faster than expected, or import substitution accelerates), Hubbell/Eaton/GEV-Electrification multiples compress 3–5 turns. Prolec is the most exposed sub-business.

5. **Prolec integration slippage (P ~10%)**. $5.275B acquisitions integrate well or they don't. Potential frictions: cross-border (Mexico) labor/legal, IT integration, salesforce conflicts with existing GEV grid-solutions reps, accounting harmonization. Anything that delays the "immediately accretive even before synergies" claim creates bear-case fodder.

### Combined bear-case quantification
If two or three of the above land within 12 months (e.g., Wind impairment + Gas Power one-quarter margin miss + multiple compression), 2027 EBITDA estimates roll back to $5.5B and forward multiples compress to ~14x blended, implying **~$280–320/share** vs. current $1,069. That is a ~70% drawdown. Probability of that compound bear: ~10–15%, but the asymmetry warrants the position-sizing discipline below.

### Risk tripwires for this thesis
Watch list (also tracked in [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]):
- Two consecutive quarters of Power-segment EBITDA margin decline
- Hyperscaler capex guidance cuts of >10% at any of the top 5
- Wind segment cash burn worse than guided
- Prolec working capital deterioration in Q2/Q3 2026
- A Mitsubishi or Siemens Energy slot-pricing disclosure that contradicts GEV's "10–20 points up" pricing narrative

## 10. Position-sizing notes

GEV is a **large-cap volatile growth name**. Trailing 1-yr beta is approximately 1.2–1.4 ^[inferred]. At a ~$287B market cap it is one of the largest pure-play AI-power infrastructure names available on US exchanges.

### Basket construction
GEV occupies the **center** of the electrical-equipment basket:
- **GEV** — power generation OEM + grid + transformers; AI-DC primary beneficiary; basket cornerstone
- **POWL** ([[pick-powell-industries-powl]]) — small-cap leverage; medium-voltage switchgear concentration; higher beta to incremental DC backlog
- **ETN** ([[pick-eaton-etn-5y|ETN]]) — broadest electrical/diversified industrial; lower beta, full-stack exposure

A balanced basket (e.g., 50% GEV / 25% POWL / 25% ETN) gives:
- Generation + T&D + datacenter electrical exposure
- Mix of large-cap stability and small-cap convexity
- Multiple-expansion upside in the small-cap component if hyperscaler capex re-accelerates

### Sizing discipline
- 12-month conviction-high but mid-thesis; do not over-concentrate above ~15% of an electrical-equipment sleeve, ~5–7% of a total-portfolio book ^[inferred guidance]
- Stop-loss / re-evaluation triggers: violation of any two of the risk tripwires above; a Q2/Q3 2026 earnings print where Power-segment margin compresses sequentially without explicit one-time explanation
- Hedging: pair against an index short or a tactical TLT/utility-ETF long on rate-sensitivity events

### What GEV does not give you
- **No upstream gas exposure** — for that, see midstream / E&P picks
- **No semiconductor / accelerator exposure** — see [[semiconductor-supply-chain-deep-dive]]
- **No optical / interconnect exposure** — see [[optics-and-interconnect-deep-dive]]
