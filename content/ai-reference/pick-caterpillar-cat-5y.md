# [Pick] Caterpillar (CAT) — 5-year hold

> **Recommendation:** Long, 5-year hold. Conviction **medium-high**.
> **Reference price:** ~$395 per share (NYSE CAT, intraday 2026-04-30 post-Q1 print). ^[extracted]
> **Market cap:** ~$190B at reference price. ^[inferred]
> **Style fit:** Large-cap industrial cyclical with through-cycle compounder characteristics. Beta ~0.9-1.1.
> **Disclaimer:** Personal research notes only. Not investment advice. The author may hold positions in names referenced. Numbers reflect filings and market prints available as of 2026-04-30 and will go stale fast — re-underwrite each quarter.

## 1. Header and framing

Caterpillar is the largest yellow-iron and large-engine OEM in the world, with three industrial segments — Construction Industries (CI), Resource Industries (RI), Energy & Transportation (E&T) — plus a captive finance arm (Cat Financial). For the AI buildout investment universe, CAT is interesting because **E&T is now the structural growth segment**, and inside E&T, the Power Generation product line and Solar Turbines have become the public industrial's most direct exposure to data-center prime and standby power. ^[extracted]

The pitch is not "buy CAT because of AI." It is "buy CAT because three independent compounders rhyme into a five-year window: data-center generator and gas-turbine demand at peak orders, mining capex recovery from a decade of underinvestment, and a U.S. construction recovery from the 2024-2025 trough — all on top of the most aggressive buyback program in the company's history." ^[inferred] Data centers are a real but bounded share of E&T revenue (estimated 5-15% of E&T, ~$3.5B of company revenue in FY26 per Cummins-style disclosure analogues; CAT itself has begun disclosing a similar order-of-magnitude figure). ^[inferred]

The risk is that the cycle reverses on you. Construction Industries is cyclical-cyclical. Mining is super-cyclical. Data-center generator demand has the same "is this peak orders?" question that hangs over POWL, ETN, and VRT. The position-sizing implication is that CAT is the lower-volatility, lower-purity AI exposure relative to GEV or POWL — the sleeve in an AI infrastructure basket that you can hold larger because it can survive a hyperscaler capex pause.

## 2. Thesis in five bullets

1. **E&T at peak utilization with multi-year backlog.** Total company backlog ended FY25 at roughly $51-52B and grew further through Q1 2026 to **~$63B (+79% YoY)** — the largest in CAT's history. ^[extracted] E&T's segment backlog is the dominant share, reportedly **~$39.8B**, with Power Generation as the largest single driver. ^[extracted] This is multi-year visibility on a business that historically was lucky to have nine months of backlog.
2. **Standby + prime-power generator orders surging from data centers.** The Lafayette IN large-engine plant has been running at extreme utilization since mid-2024. Capacity expansion (announced in 2024-2025) is targeted to roughly **double** large-engine output by late 2026, with management guiding that the expanded capacity is already largely sold. ^[extracted] The "Monarch" 2 GW data-center engine framework agreement disclosed in March 2026 cemented hyperscaler demand at single-deal scale that would have been unthinkable two years ago. ^[extracted]
3. **Solar Turbines becomes the behind-the-meter answer.** Solar's industrial gas turbines (Mercury, Centaur, Taurus, Mars, Titan ranges, ~1-22 MW per unit) are the natural fit for behind-the-meter and bridge power for data centers that cannot wait for utility interconnection. Lead times have stretched from ~12-18 months to multi-year quoted. ^[inferred] This is structurally a higher-margin business than the rest of E&T.
4. **Construction Industries cyclical recovery 2026-2028.** CI was the segment that took the brunt of FY25's destocking and dealer rebalancing. Q1 2026 inflected — order rates and dealer inventory moves point toward a multi-quarter rebuild into a full cycle. ^[extracted] The U.S. infrastructure law and reshoring capex are slow-moving but durable demand sources that overlap the next 3-5 years.
5. **Capital allocation: rebuilt buyback at scale.** CAT bought back roughly $4-5B of stock per year through the cycle and is positioned to step that up materially as FCF inflects on the E&T mix shift and CI recovery. ^[inferred] Combined with a ~1.5% dividend, total shareholder yield runs 4-6% per year before any growth.

## 3. Business overview

### 3.1 Segment map

CAT reports four operating segments. For investors, the three industrial segments are what you model.

**Construction Industries (CI).**
- Excavators, wheel loaders, backhoes, articulated trucks, dozers, asphalt pavers, compactors.
- End markets: nonresidential construction, residential construction, infrastructure (roads, bridges, water), heavy civil.
- ~40-45% of industrial revenue at mid-cycle. ^[inferred]
- Most cyclical-cyclical of the three. Dealer inventory dynamics dominate quarterly noise.

**Resource Industries (RI).**
- Large mining trucks (797F, 798 AC), shovels, draglines, hard-rock and surface mining equipment, Cat Reman, autonomy software (Cat Command).
- End markets: copper, iron ore, gold, met coal, oil sands. Heavy-construction quarry and aggregates.
- ~20-25% of industrial revenue at mid-cycle. ^[inferred]
- Super-cyclical. The five-year window includes a likely mining capex up-cycle as commodity producers respond to >decade of underinvestment + electrification copper intensity.

**Energy & Transportation (E&T).**
- Four sub-product lines:
  - **Power Generation** — large reciprocating diesel and natural-gas engines (3500/3500B and the new 3500E, plus the larger G3500, G3600, and emerging gas units). Standby and prime-power gensets, generator sets sold through Cat Electric Power Division and packagers.
  - **Solar Turbines** — industrial gas turbines (1-22 MW class). Behind-the-meter, oil & gas mechanical drive, marine, distributed generation.
  - **Oil & Gas** — well-servicing, gas compression, drilling.
  - **Industrial** — agriculture and construction OEM engine sales (Perkins-equivalent volumes feed here partly).
  - **Rail & Marine** — Progress Rail (locomotives, rail services), marine engines.
- ~30-35% of industrial revenue and rising. ^[inferred] Highest segment margin in 2025-2026.
- This is the AI buildout exposure.

**Financial Products (Cat Financial).**
- Captive finance for dealer floor plan and customer end-user financing. Roughly $35B receivables book. ^[inferred] Steady spread business, not the equity story.

### 3.2 Channel and dealer system

CAT sells almost exclusively through **independent Cat dealers** — ~150 dealers globally, with regional exclusivity. The dealer network is a moat: it is the parts and service backbone that drives ~$25-30B of aftermarket revenue, and it is the deployment muscle that gets a 2.5 MW genset commissioned at a Northern Virginia data center. ^[inferred]

The dealer network is also the pacing variable for Q-by-Q noise. Dealer inventory rebuilds add to revenue; dealer destocking subtracts. Most of FY25 CI weakness was dealer-driven, not end-market.

### 3.3 Aftermarket and services

Services revenue (parts, rebuilds, Cat Reman, fleet services) was ~$24B in 2024 and management's stated target is **$28B by 2026**. ^[extracted] This is a structurally higher-margin, less-cyclical revenue layer that has grown faster than equipment revenue and is the reason CAT through-cycle margins keep rising. The data-center installed base of large engines means a multi-decade aftermarket annuity — every 3500 series engine commissioned today will throw off parts and service revenue for 25-40 years.

## 4. AI buildout exposure: where in CAT does the AI dollar land

### 4.1 Power Generation (Cat Electric Power)

Standby and prime-power gensets in the **2-4 MW class** built around the Cat 3500-series and (for the largest) 3600-series large-bore reciprocating engines. ^[extracted] Diesel units dominate standby; natural-gas units are increasingly used for prime power and bridge power where utility interconnection is delayed.

A 100 MW data-center campus typically requires **40-50 of the 2.5 MW class units** for N+1 redundancy on the IT load, plus mechanical and house loads. ^[inferred] The largest hyperscaler campuses being built today are 500 MW to 1 GW IT load — implying 200-400 large engines per campus.

**Lafayette, Indiana** is the headline plant: large-engine assembly for 60Hz prime/standby. CAT announced a capacity expansion in 2024 with completion phases through 2026. ^[extracted] The disclosed expansion roughly doubles large-engine output. Two-year capacity expansion of ~125% has been cited in the second-order beneficiaries page on this wiki. ^[extracted]

### 4.2 Solar Turbines

Industrial gas turbines (~1-22 MW) — Mercury 50 (~4.6 MW), Centaur 40/50, Taurus 60/65/70, Mars 90/100, Titan 130/250 (~22 MW). ^[extracted] Production is at San Diego with global service centers.

The data-center pull on Solar is **behind-the-meter** and **bridge power** — sites that cannot get a utility interconnect on the schedule the customer needs (Northern Virginia, Phoenix, parts of Texas) or sites that explicitly want to be islanded. Solar Turbines competes against Mitsubishi Power (~MGS small-frame), Siemens Energy (SGT-A series, smaller industrial turbines), and Wartsila (reciprocating engines, distinct technology). At <30 MW per site, Solar is generally the volume leader. ^[inferred]

### 4.3 Sizing the AI exposure

CAT has begun disclosing data-center revenue but coverage has been imprecise — management language on calls in late 2025 and Q1 2026 references "data center order book continues to expand" without putting a hard number in print like Cummins did with its $3.5B 2026 disclosure. ^[ambiguous] Triangulating:

- E&T full-year revenue is ~$30-32B in 2025-2026. ^[inferred]
- Power Generation is ~25-30% of E&T (~$8-9B). ^[inferred]
- Data centers are estimated **40-55% of Power Generation** in 2026 — i.e., ~$3.5-4.5B of company revenue. ^[inferred]
- Solar Turbines runs ~$3-4B in revenue, of which behind-the-meter / data-center-attributable is plausibly ~$0.5-1.0B and growing. ^[inferred]

So a reasonable estimate is **$4-5.5B of CAT FY26 revenue is data-center-attributable** — high single digits of company revenue, but >70% of E&T's incremental growth, with materially higher gross margin than blended company average. ^[inferred] That is the right way to think about CAT's AI exposure: not the largest absolute revenue line, but the marginal driver and the segment-mix-shift driver.

### 4.4 Customers (data-center side)

Mix of hyperscalers and colos. Public references include the "Monarch" 2 GW framework deal (March 2026 disclosure), QTS, CyrusOne, Equinix, Iron Mountain, EdgeConneX, and the Stargate-related buildouts where CAT is named in vendor lists. ^[extracted] Hyperscaler direct names (MSFT, AMZN, GOOG, META) buy through their EPCs and through the Cat dealer network — direct attribution is murky on purpose.

## 5. Demand drivers: the quarter-by-quarter and the structural

### 5.1 E&T order book trajectory

The E&T order book — best inferred from CAT's segment backlog disclosure and management commentary — has been the standout. Q-by-Q sketch:

- Q1 2025: E&T orders strong; Power Generation orders >2x prior-year run rate. ^[extracted]
- Q2 2025: orders held; Solar Turbines book-to-bill ~1.5x. ^[inferred]
- Q3 2025: Lafayette running flat-out; backlog stretching past 2026 deliveries. ^[extracted]
- Q4 2025: order strength continued; full-year 2025 ended with $51B+ company backlog. ^[extracted]
- Q1 2026: company backlog grew to ~$63B (+79% YoY); E&T ~$39.8B; Power Gen leading. ^[extracted] Earnings beat on Q1 2026 print drove a ~5-7% pop on April 30, 2026. ^[extracted]

The pattern is clean: the bookings rate has not yet decelerated. The leading indicator if/when this starts to roll is hyperscaler capex YoY growth (see [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]).

### 5.2 Specific data-center customer wins

- **"Monarch" 2 GW framework** (March 2026) — multi-year supply agreement for data-center generator capacity. The single largest disclosed CAT data-center deal. ^[extracted] Customer identity not publicly named; speculated to be a hyperscaler or hyperscaler-anchored colo.
- **Solar Turbines bridge-power sites** — multiple <50 MW behind-the-meter installations in Texas and Virginia called out in trade press through 2025. ^[inferred]
- Multiple multi-hundred-MW dealer-channel orders not individually disclosed.

### 5.3 Behind-the-meter gas-turbine orders (Solar Turbines)

Behind-the-meter is structurally where utility-frustrated customers go. The constraint stack is: a hyperscaler that cannot get a utility interconnection in <36 months, that needs >100 MW now, signs a Solar Turbines order for a 50-100 MW gas-turbine campus and runs it on pipeline gas while it waits for the utility interconnection — or in some cases just keeps it as the long-run answer. The order trajectory through 2025 was the strongest on record for Solar. ^[inferred]

### 5.4 Mining recovery (Resource Industries)

Mining capex was suppressed for ~10 years post-2014 commodity downturn. Three forces are turning it:
1. **Copper electrification demand** — utility transformer copper, EV copper, data-center copper.
2. **Replacement** — the global mining truck fleet is aging into mandatory replacement cycles in 2026-2028.
3. **Autonomy upgrades** — Cat Command (autonomous haul) is a tailwind for both new sales and parts/service.

RI orders inflected positive in late 2025 and Q1 2026. The five-year case includes RI normalizing back to a higher-trend level. ^[inferred]

### 5.5 Construction recovery (Construction Industries)

CI is the cyclical-cyclical wildcard. The 2024-2025 destock was painful; Q1 2026 saw the first clean inflection in U.S. dealer order rates in 18 months. ^[extracted] If the U.S. economy avoids recession through 2026, CI compounds back to mid-cycle revenue/margin over 2026-2028.

## 6. Supply and capacity: the bottleneck side

### 6.1 Lafayette, Indiana — large-engine assembly

The most-watched CAT industrial site for AI exposure. Lafayette assembles the 3500-series large reciprocating engines that are the heart of data-center gensets. The plant has been running at full utilization since mid-2024. ^[extracted]

Announced expansions take phased capacity steps through 2026 with the goal of roughly **doubling large-engine output**. Approximately ~125% expansion of data-center engine manufacturing capacity has been referenced over a two-year window. ^[extracted] The expansion includes additional shifts, brownfield additions to existing footprint, and new tooling for the 3500E variant.

The risk: capacity expansion is itself a 12-24 month process and can slip. If Lafayette slips by 6 months, several billion dollars of revenue slides into FY27.

### 6.2 Solar Turbines — San Diego

Solar's San Diego campus is geographically constrained — physical expansion is harder than at Lafayette. Solar is responding via additional shifts and supply-chain investments to debottleneck. Lead times have stretched, which translates into pricing power. ^[inferred]

### 6.3 Capacity expansion broadly

Management has signaled additional capex for E&T capacity beyond Lafayette and Solar, including supplier qualification expansions and capacity at sub-tier engine component plants. The capital cost is in the high-single-digit-billions range cumulatively. ^[inferred] FCF impact: capex is elevated relative to historical run rate but easily covered by operating cash flow at current margin levels.

### 6.4 Backlog conversion timing

Backlog is multi-year. The mechanical question is the pace at which backlog converts to revenue:
- **Power Gen**: ~12-24 months order-to-ship for a typical data-center order. Capacity-bound — expansion timing controls revenue timing.
- **Solar**: 18-36 months for a turbine package. Bookings made today land in 2027-2028 revenue.
- **CI**: ~3-6 months order-to-ship in normal conditions.
- **RI**: 9-18 months for large mining trucks.

This means the FY26-FY28 revenue trajectory is largely already booked in E&T. The variable is FY29-FY30, which depends on whether bookings hold at current cadence.

## 10. Bear case

Five things to underwrite against:

### 10.1 Macro recession / construction roll

CI is the largest segment by revenue. A U.S. recession in 2027-2028 would cut CI revenue 15-25% and segment margin 400-600 bps. Even with E&T strong, total company op margin would compress to 16-17%, EPS to ~$18-20, and the multiple would compress with it. **Quantified bear:** 25-30% drawdown over 12 months. The hedge is that mining and E&T provide diversification CAT did not have in prior recessions.

### 10.2 Mining capex weakness

If commodity prices break (e.g., copper to $3/lb, iron ore to $80/t), the mining capex cycle that should help RI in 2026-2028 stalls instead. RI revenue and margin both compress. The five-year IRR loses ~2-3 points. ^[inferred]

### 10.3 Data-center gas-turbine substitution to other vendors

For the **Solar Turbines** behind-the-meter exposure, the competitive set is real:
- **Mitsubishi Power** (small/medium-frame industrial gas turbines, MGS reciprocating engines).
- **Siemens Energy** (SGT-A series, smaller industrial turbines).
- **Wartsila** (large reciprocating gas engines, distinct technology vs. Solar's turbines).
- **GE Vernova** (LM-class aeroderivative turbines for larger 30-50 MW behind-the-meter).

For **reciprocating engines**, the head-to-head is:
- **Cummins** (HSK and QSK series) — direct competitor, increasingly aggressive on data-center mix.
- **MTU / Rolls-Royce Power Systems** (Series 4000 engines).
- **Mitsubishi Heavy MGS**.
- **Kohler** (private).
- **Generac** (smaller and entering 2-3 MW class with new lineup, Enercon Engineering acquisition).

CAT has share, brand, and dealer reach but no monopoly. If CMI or GNRC capacity ramps faster than CAT's, marginal hyperscaler orders go to CMI. The bear case here trims CAT's data-center growth rate from high teens to single digits and softens E&T mix premium. ^[inferred]

### 10.4 Margin mean-reversion

E&T at 23-25% segment margin is high. History says industrial cyclicals revert. If pricing power abates as supply catches demand in 2027-2028, E&T margin reverts to ~20% and total-company margin reverts toward 17-18%. EPS lands $4-5 below base case in the back half of the model.

### 10.5 Tariff and trade

CAT has ~$50-60B revenue with material non-U.S. exposure (~45-50% revenue international). Sustained U.S./China trade friction or a broad tariff regime change can compress margin via input cost (steel, components from Asia) and revenue (Chinese mining and construction). The 2025 tariff regime has been a manageable headwind so far; a step-up would not be. ^[inferred]

### 10.6 China construction structural

Chinese construction equipment demand has been depressed since 2022. CAT's China CI franchise is a small share of CI revenue today but a meaningful drag on growth comparisons. If China structural demand stays depressed, CI segment growth tops out below the historical full-cycle peak.

## 11. Position-sizing and portfolio fit

- **Liquidity:** Mega-cap, ~$190B market cap, dollar volume in the billions per day. Sizes up to 5-7% of an AI infrastructure basket comfortably.
- **Volatility:** β ~0.9-1.1 vs. SPX. Realized 30-day vol typically 18-25%. Materially lower than POWL (30-50% realized vol) or CRDO (50-80%).
- **Drawdown profile:** Historic 2008-09 drew down ~70%. 2014-15 mining bust drew down ~40%. 2020 drew down ~40%. Underwrite for 30-40% drawdown in a stress event.
- **Pairs and basket fit:**
  - **CAT + CMI**: dual-engine basket. CMI is the cleaner data-center pure-play; CAT is the diversified compounder. 60/40 CAT/CMI captures the engine theme with margin diversity.
  - **CAT + GEV + ETN**: three-leg power and electrical basket. CAT covers behind-the-meter and standby; GEV covers utility-scale gas turbines and grid; ETN covers electrical distribution and switchgear. Each is the leader in its domain.
  - **CAT vs POWL**: the volatility complement. POWL is the smaller, higher-beta, higher-purity version of the same theme. Run them as a barbell (small POWL, larger CAT).
- **Why not bigger:** the position-sizing constraint is the cyclical chassis. CAT in a 2008-style recession will draw down more than the market. Sizing implies you have conviction to hold through a 30-40% drawdown.
- **Why not smaller:** the five-year base case at +60-70% total return with mid-teens IRR upside on bull is the cleanest large-cap industrial AI sleeve available with the volatility to actually fit a long-only portfolio.

## 12. What would change the recommendation

Re-underwrite (and likely cut) if any of:

- **E&T orders decelerate sequentially** for two consecutive quarters with no capacity-constraint explanation.
- **Hyperscaler capex YoY growth** drops below +20% (see [[risk-tripwires-and-warning-indicators]]).
- **Lafayette expansion slips** by more than two quarters from current schedule.
- **Mining capex announcements** from BHP/Rio/Vale/Freeport reverse course in 2026-2027.
- **Cummins or Generac** announce capacity additions that dilute CAT pricing power.
- **CI dealer inventory** rebuilds beyond historical norms — sets up a 2027-2028 destock.
