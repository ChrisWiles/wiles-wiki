The optimistic mechanism for a [[post-agi-economy|post-AGI economy]] is straightforward: AGI lowers production costs, prices fall, real living standards rise, and people need less income to live well.

The pessimistic mechanism is also straightforward: AGI lowers labor demand, wages fall, capital income rises, and owners of AI infrastructure capture most gains.

## Scarce Assets

AI can make many digital and service goods cheaper, but not every important good falls to zero cost. Scarce assets may include:

- Compute
- Energy
- Chips
- Robotics
- Land and housing
- Health systems
- Physical infrastructure
- Data and intellectual property
- Brand trust and distribution
- Regulatory permissions
- Security
- Political access

Profits likely accrue to owners of bottlenecks: frontier model companies, chipmakers, cloud providers, data-center operators, energy suppliers, robotics firms, owners of proprietary data, distribution platforms, and holders of scarce land or licenses.

OpenAI's April 2026 industrial policy paper explicitly acknowledges the risk that economic gains concentrate within a small number of firms, including OpenAI itself, even if AI becomes more powerful and widely used.

## Prosperity Formula

A useful shorthand:

> Real prosperity = access to low-cost goods and services + claims on scarce assets + political power over the rules.

Falling prices help everyone only if the goods that matter become cheap enough. If AI makes entertainment, tutoring, coding, and basic services cheap while housing, energy, healthcare, and status remain expensive, people still need income, ownership, or public provision.

## Purchasing Power Without Wages

If fewer people earn wages, income and access can come from several channels:

- Capital income and citizen dividends from sovereign wealth funds, public stakes, land-value taxes, windfall-sharing mechanisms, or public ownership of AI infrastructure
- Cash transfers such as UBI, negative income taxes, child allowances, old-age pensions, disability payments, and emergency cash
- Universal services including healthcare, education, housing support, transit, child care, elder care, and digital access
- Universal basic compute or direct access to productive AI capabilities
- Public employment or civic contribution in care, education, environmental restoration, local governance, arts, science support, and community work
- Grants and capital endowments such as startup grants, education grants, housing grants, or universal basic capital accounts

## Demand Fragility

In a normal capitalist economy, wages are both a production cost and the main source of consumer demand. AGI can reduce the cost side while weakening the income channel.

Whether demand collapses depends on how much prices fall, how concentrated capital income becomes, and how quickly governments or ownership institutions recycle AGI profits back to households.
