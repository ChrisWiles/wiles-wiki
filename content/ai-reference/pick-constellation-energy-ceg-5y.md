## 2. Thesis — multi-decade nuclear scarcity

The 5-year thesis rests on six structural arguments, each independently sufficient and stacking multiplicatively:

1. **Nuclear is functionally unbuildable on a 5-year horizon in North America.** Vogtle 3 and Vogtle 4 took ~14 years and ~$36B for ~2.2 GW. No new large reactor is in active US construction. SMR FOAK deployments target 2030-2032 at sub-GW scale. Meanwhile, the US fleet is shrinking (Indian Point, Palisades shutdown, Diablo Canyon scheduled shutdown deferred but not extended). On the demand side, hyperscaler AI load is adding the equivalent of a Vogtle every ~6 months in PJM alone ^[inferred from PJM load forecasts]. The supply / demand mismatch is the most extreme it has ever been in any US power market.
2. **License extensions to 80 years are the asset-life multiplier.** NRC's Subsequent License Renewal (SLR) framework allows operating reactors to extend from 60 → 80 years, subject to environmental and aging-management review. Peach Bottom (Exelon legacy, now CEG) was first to receive SLR; Surry, Turkey Point, North Anna followed. CEG's roadmap takes Calvert Cliffs, Limerick, Bryon, Braidwood, LaSalle, Dresden, Quad Cities, Clinton, Nine Mile Point, Ginna, FitzPatrick, and Salem/Hope Creek (minority) through SLR over the 5-year window ^[extracted/inferred from NRC SLR pipeline]. **Each successful SLR extends the discounted cash-flow window by 20 years on assets that earn $80-$130/MWh PPA pricing — a step-function NPV event.**
3. **Structural PPA repricing across multiple cycles.** Today, ~10% of CEG nuclear MWh are under long-dated bilateral hyperscaler PPAs (Crane / Microsoft is the cleanest example). By 2031, we model 60-75% of the fleet under long-dated PPAs at average prices of $90-$110/MWh (escalator-adjusted) — a 5-year fleet-wide repricing. The mechanism is not one large PPA but a sequence of 2-4 GW PPAs signed each year as existing hub-priced output rolls into new contracts. Each cycle resets the comp set higher: Crane at $110-$115/MWh has already redefined what hyperscalers will pay for firm 24×7 carbon-free power ^[extracted/inferred].
4. **Crane (TMI Unit 1) restart in 2027-2028 is the proof point.** ~835 MW PWR comes back online with a 20-year Microsoft offtake at premium pricing. Beyond the direct EBITDA contribution (~$700-900M annual gross margin pre-PTC), Crane's restart sets the precedent for additional reactor-restart candidates: Palisades (Holtec/Entergy-owned, but the playbook is portable), Duane Arnold (Iowa, NextEra-owned, partial decommissioning advanced), Diablo Canyon (PG&E, life extended through 2030 with renewal option). CEG is positioned as the most credible operator-of-record for any restart situation that cannot be self-operated by the asset owner ^[inferred].
5. **Calpine integration adds 26 GW of dispatchable + storage and unlocks the 24×7 product.** The Calpine acquisition (closed Q1 2026, ~$26.6B equity + ~$12.7B assumed debt) gives CEG a uniquely competitive product: bundled long-dated nuclear baseload + gas peaking + battery storage, sold as a single firm 24×7 carbon-near-zero product. No pure-nuclear competitor (TLN, PEG-nuclear, NEE-merchant) and no pure-gas competitor (NRG, VST-gas) can match the structure. Synergy guide of ~$2B run-rate annual EBITDA accretion is achievable across the 5-year window ^[extracted/inferred].
6. **SMR / advanced-nuclear optionality matures from 2030+.** CEG's X-Energy (Xe-100, ~80 MW HTGR with TRISO fuel) MOU and Holtec SMR-300 (PWR, ~300 MW) site evaluations position the company as the most likely commercial operator of post-FOAK SMR deployments. Existing CEG nuclear sites already have grid interconnection, water rights, security, workforce, community acceptance, and decommissioning trust funds — the four most expensive components of any new nuclear project are already paid for. By 2031 we could see 0.5-2 GW of SMR online at CEG sites ^[inferred].

**What gets the stock to $650-$725 in 5 years:** SLRs progress on 8-12 reactors; 60-75% of fleet under long-dated hyperscaler PPAs at $90-$110/MWh average; Crane online and contributing full-year EBITDA from 2028; Calpine $2B synergy fully realized by 2028; PJM capacity revenues stabilize at $300-$500/MW-day RTO-wide; 1-2 SMR commercial commitments with first concrete pour by 2030; balance sheet delevered to ~2.5x net debt / EBITDA; $5-7B annual buyback through-cycle.

2. **Wholesale length absorption.** Calpine's wholesale gas length lands inside Constellation NewEnergy's existing retail book, reducing market-clearing risk.
3. **Margin layer.** Retail margins are typically $3-7/MWh on competitive C&I — a meaningful contribution at 165+ TWh of volume.

### SMR / advanced nuclear pipeline

### Storage and renewables

Calpine brings a meaningful battery storage development pipeline (~1-2 GW visible by 2028 ^[inferred]), focused on ERCOT and CAISO. CEG's existing wind/solar exposure (~1 GW) is small and not the franchise; we model it at flat to slow growth across the period.

The 12-month dossier framed AI exposure as three vectors: explicit Crane PPA, implicit capacity-price reset, and pipeline visibility. Over 5 years, the picture matures into a *structural* exposure — every part of the business has AI demand pricing baked in.

Each $100/MW-day on top of the current cap is roughly $800M of incremental EBITDA at ~22 GW UCAP, with very little incremental cost ^[inferred]. Even modest cap relaxation produces meaningful 5-year EBITDA upside.

### c) Hyperscaler load growth in CEG geography

The PJM mid-Atlantic data-center alley (Loudoun, Prince William, the Mid-Atlantic interconnection queue) overlaps directly with CEG's Limerick, Peach Bottom, Calvert Cliffs reactors. Northern Illinois (Cook, DuPage, Will counties around Chicago) is becoming a secondary data-center hub; CEG's Bryon, Braidwood, Dresden, and LaSalle reactors are the only material clean baseload supply in that footprint.

By 2031, we estimate ~45-60% of forward FY31 EBITDA is AI/hyperscaler-PPA-dependent (directly or via capacity prices set by AI-driven load growth) ^[inferred] — vs ~25-35% in FY27. This is a structural mix shift, not a cycle.

### d) Co-located large-load policy

The March 2025 FERC rejection of the Talen / AWS Susquehanna amended ISA created a precedent that behind-the-meter PPAs may need to pay full transmission costs ^[extracted]. Over the 5-year window, FERC and PJM will work through this in stakeholder proceedings. Two scenarios:

- **Tighter rule (bear):** behind-the-meter co-location requires full network upgrade payment; addressable market for high-margin co-located PPAs caps. Front-of-meter PPAs (Crane / Microsoft model) still work but at slightly tighter margins.
- **Workable rule (base/bull):** FERC, PJM, and state regulators arrive at a hybrid construct (Bring-Your-Own-Generation, capacity-only reservation, partial transmission charge). Co-location optionality adds 1-3 GW of high-margin product at CEG sites.

We model the base case assuming a workable rule by 2028.

## 5. Demand drivers (5-year)

### Nuclear PTC durability

The IRA Section 45U PTC for existing nuclear is ~$15/MWh in 2024 dollars, escalated by CPI, phased to zero above gross-receipts thresholds, available through 2032 ^[extracted].

Three 5-year considerations:

1. **Floor, not ceiling.** When CEG sells into an $80-$110/MWh PPA, the PTC is largely de minimis. The PTC matters most in scenarios where PPA penetration stalls and merchant prices fall — i.e., the bear case. This makes the PTC structurally counter-cyclical to thesis risk.
2. **Post-2032 cliff.** The current statute sunsets the PTC after 2032. The 5-year base case assumes PPA pricing is high enough that the cliff has minimal impact on FY32 EBITDA. Bear case: PPA penetration stalls and the PTC cliff becomes a material 2033 EBITDA hit.
3. **Stacking and gross-receipts definitions.** IRS guidance on whether PPA revenue counts as "gross receipts" for the phase-out, and whether PTC stacks with corporate PPA pricing, is the key 12-24 month policy variable. Final rules in 2026-2027 are the reference window.

Over a 5-year hold, the PTC matters less than any individual auction or PPA print — which is why the 5-year setup is more durable than the 12-month one.

### License extensions

Every successful SLR extends a reactor's revenue tail by 20 years. With ~1 GW per year of CEG fleet capacity going through SLR over 2026-2031, the cumulative NPV uplift is large. Approximate per-reactor mechanics:

- 1 GW reactor × 95% CF × 8,760 hr × $50/MWh net gross margin (post-variable cost, post-fuel, post-O&M, weighted across hub + PPA) = ~$420M annual gross margin ^[inferred]
- 20 additional years × 7% discount = ~$4.5B per-reactor NPV uplift ^[inferred]
- 8-12 reactors going through SLR over 5 years = ~$36-54B cumulative NPV uplift
- Per share (310M dil): ~$120-$175

Today, the market only partially capitalizes this. Each successful SLR is a discrete catalyst that progressively forces the multiple higher.

### PJM capacity auction trajectory

(See section 4.b above.) Across the 5-year window, the sequence of BRA prints (2028/29, 2029/30, 2030/31) is the most direct way for the market to validate the AI-driven supply-shortage narrative. Each auction is a public price signal that reprices the entire fleet. Across three or four auctions, the multi-year mean is more meaningful than any single print — which is the durability advantage of the 5-year horizon.

### Calpine merchant gas re-rating

Through-cycle, the gas merchant complex (NRG, VST gas, Calpine standalone) trades at 7-9× EV/EBITDA. Inside CEG, Calpine has the opportunity to re-rate to 9-11× because of the bundling premium discussed above. The 5-year SOTP captures this re-rate.

### SMR FOAK then NOAK economics

If Vogtle ($36B / 2.2 GW = $16,400/kW) is the high-water mark of large-light-water economics, FOAK SMR economics likely land at $10,000-$14,000/kW and NOAK economics target $5,000-$7,000/kW ^[inferred from DOE ARDP framing and X-Energy/Holtec public commentary]. CEG's strategic position is to deploy NOAK SMR at existing nuclear sites starting 2031-2034 — within the 5-year hold horizon for entry, with the EBITDA contribution arriving in years 6-10 of the holding period (i.e., beyond the formal 5-year valuation window but priced into the option layer of fair value).

### Coal retirements

PJM and MISO coal retirements continue at 8-15 GW per year through 2030 ^[inferred from EIA retirement schedule]. Most of this capacity does not get directly replaced with firm baseload — it is replaced with intermittent renewables or natural gas. Each GW of coal retirement raises the marginal value of CEG's nuclear and Calpine gas portfolio.

## 6. Supply / capacity profile — what CEG can add

### Crane Clean Energy Center restart

- ~835 MW PWR (TMI Unit 1, original Babcock & Wilcox plant, separate from the 1979 Unit 2 incident) ^[extracted]
- Restart capex: management guided ~$1.6B at deal announcement ^[extracted]
- Critical-path items: NRC reactivation (filed; on track), main transformer (long-lead, ordered), turbine refurbishment, license amendments, simulator and training, staff rehire
- Target in-service: 2027-2028; first full year of EBITDA contribution: 2028 or 2029
- Full-year gross margin contribution: ~$700-$900M pre-PTC ^[inferred]

### Other restart candidates

The 5-year question: how many additional restart situations are credible, and what is CEG's role?

- **Palisades (Holtec/Entergy-owned, MI):** ~800 MW PWR; in active restart, DOE LPO loan finalized 2024-2025. CEG is not the operator but the precedent informs Crane's downstream economics.
- **Duane Arnold (NextEra-owned, IA):** ~620 MW BWR; NextEra publicly explored restart in 2024-2025. Status ambiguous; CEG could play an operator-of-record role if NextEra chooses to monetize ^[ambiguous].
- **Diablo Canyon (PG&E, CA):** 2.2 GW; life extended through 2030, with renewal optionality. Not a CEG asset; outside thesis but a comp data point.
- **Indian Point (decommissioned NY):** decommissioning advanced; not realistically reversible.
- **San Onofre (decommissioned CA):** not realistically reversible.

The realistic 5-year restart inventory in North America beyond Crane and Palisades is small (~1-2 GW). This shortage is precisely what gives the existing fleet its pricing power.

### Uprates

Aggregate possible uprate capacity across the CEG fleet over 5 years: ~300-500 MW ^[inferred]. Historical uprate capex is $1,500-$3,000/kW, well below new-build cost. Targets:

- Bryon 1/2: ~1-2% incremental
- Braidwood 1/2: ~1-2% incremental
- LaSalle 1/2: ~1-2% incremental
- Limerick 1/2: ~2-3% incremental (most upside)
- Peach Bottom 2/3: ~1-2% incremental

These are not glamorous but each MW added is firm 24×7 carbon-free supply at marginal cost — extremely valuable in the AI-demand context.

### SMR deployment timeline

Realistic 5-year milestones:

- **2026-2027:** site selection for FOAK CEG-hosted SMR (Bryon, Limerick, or Calvert Cliffs candidate sites)
- **2027-2028:** COL application filing
- **2028-2029:** NRC review, site preparation, supply-chain procurement
- **2029-2030:** first concrete (FOAK CEG-site SMR)
- **2031-2032:** commissioning, low-power testing, first commercial generation

The 5-year hold period captures the *pre-revenue* stages — site selection through first-of-a-kind construction. The economic contribution to FY30/FY31 EBITDA is small; the *option value* visible in FY31 share price is large.

### License extensions through the holding period

By April 2031, we expect:

- **8-12 reactors** through SLR or in advanced SLR review, extending licenses to 80 years
- **Cumulative NPV uplift:** ~$36-54B fleet-wide (~$120-$175/share) ^[inferred]
- **Multiple catalysts:** each successful SLR is a discrete print that progressively repriced the fleet

## 8. Valuation — sum-of-parts long-form

We value CEG as a sum-of-parts: nuclear fleet DCF, Crane restart NPV, Calpine standalone, SMR option layer, less net debt. All values are 5-year forward (i.e., implied FY31 fair value), because the 5-year hold thesis monetizes the SLR cycle and PPA penetration over multiple printing periods.

### a) Existing nuclear fleet — DCF on per-MWh PPA + merchant

Approach: build a simple DCF on the nuclear fleet treating PPA-contracted MWh and merchant MWh separately, with explicit PTC modeling, then discount.

Assumptions (FY30E base case):
- Total nuclear MWh: ~31 GW × 95% CF × 8,760 hr ≈ 258 TWh ^[extracted/inferred]
- PPA-contracted share: ~65% (= 168 TWh) at average $100/MWh = $16.8B PPA revenue
- Merchant share: ~35% (= 90 TWh) at average $55/MWh hub price + capacity = $5.0B
- Variable cost (fuel, O&M, refueling outage allocation): ~$22/MWh × 258 TWh = $5.7B
- Capacity revenue (~22 GW UCAP × $400/MW-day × 365): $3.2B
- PTC contribution: small (<$0.5B at FY30 given PPA penetration)
- **Nuclear segment EBITDA FY30E base: ~$8.5-9.5B**

DCF: 20-year forward stream from FY30, with year-on-year growth ~3% (mix shift to higher-priced PPAs, escalators), terminal growth 1.5%, WACC 7%:
- Implied EV: ~$100-$120B
- Per share (290M FY30 dil): **$345-$415**

### b) Crane Clean Energy Center — NPV

Approach: discounted cash flow on the Microsoft 20-year PPA, net of restart capex and ongoing operating cost. The Crane PPA is cleaner to model than the rest of the fleet because the contract is signed and disclosed in commercial terms.

Assumptions:
- PPA price: ~$110-$115/MWh (with CPI escalator) ^[extracted/inferred]
- Output: 835 MW × 95% CF × 8,760 hr ≈ 6.95 TWh/year
- Variable cost: ~$30-40/MWh
- Annual gross margin: ~$490-$555M; ramps to full year by 2028
- 20-year DCF, 9% WACC: ~$3.5-4.5B NPV
- Less remaining restart capex: ~$0.9-1.2B
- **Net NPV: ~$2.6-3.3B**
- Per share: **$9-$11**

### c) Calpine — gas + storage + geothermal

Approach: standalone EBITDA × peer multiple, with re-rate from "diversified gas merchant" multiple to "bundled clean firm" multiple as integration matures.

Assumptions (FY30E base case):
- Calpine standalone EBITDA: ~$4.5-5.5B (synergized) ^[inferred]
- Apply 10× EV/EBITDA (between gas merchant 7-9× and regulated utility 12-14×, reflecting bundling premium)
- Implied EV: ~$45-55B
- Per share: **$155-$190**

Cross-check: CEG paid ~$26.6B equity + ~$12.7B assumed debt for Calpine ^[inferred]. Our 5-year base value of ~$50B EV implies the deal earned ~$10-15B of NPV across the holding period — consistent with the announced ~$2B synergy guide capitalized at 10×.

### d) SMR / advanced nuclear option (Black-Scholes-ish)

Approach: probability-weighted credit using a simple option-value framework (no commitment in base case).

Inputs:
- Underlying: 1-2 GW of SMR online by ~2032-2033
- Steady-state EBITDA at maturity: ~$1.5-2.0B (1 GW × $1.5-2.0B/GW EBITDA at NOAK economics) ^[inferred]
- Steady-state EV at 12× = $18-24B
- Probability of execution at this scale (5-year forward): ~40% ^[inferred, up from 25% in 12-month dossier as more milestones land]
- Time to revenue: ~5-7 years from 2031
- Discount factor (9% WACC, 6 years): ~0.60
- Option value: 0.4 × $20B × 0.6 = ~$4.8B
- Per share: **$15-$20**

The option-value framing is conservative. The "real" option value is higher because successful FOAK SMR deployment unlocks NOAK economics on multiple subsequent units at the same site — a scaling factor we do not capture in the simple Black-Scholes-like model.

### e) Net debt subtraction

- Pro forma post-Calpine net debt FY26: ~$18-20B
- Expected FY30 net debt with $8-10B of cumulative paydown: ~$10-12B
- Per share: **−$35 to −$42**

| Component | Per-share value ($) |
|---|---:|
| Nuclear fleet DCF | 380 |
| Crane (Microsoft PPA NPV, net) | 10 |

We anchor the **base 5-year target at $650-$725**, with the lower end of the range reflecting more conservative SLR pacing and PPA penetration.

### Bear ($300-$340) and bull ($1,000-$1,150) goalposts

- **Bear (5-year):** SLRs slow, only 4-6 successful renewals; PPA penetration stalls at ~30%; PJM cap holds; Calpine integration mediocre; SMR program quietly tabled. Multiple compresses to ~9× EV/EBITDA on nuclear and ~7× on gas. PTC cliff hits in FY33. Fair value ~$300-$340 — flat to modestly above current price; modest dividend yield over 5 years.
- **Bull (5-year):** 12+ SLRs through review, 80% PPA penetration at $115/MWh average, PJM cap removed and clearing $500-$600/MW-day, Calpine $2B synergy + bundled premium fully realized, 1-2 GW SMR FOAK on schedule with offtake signed. Multiples expand to 18× nuclear / 12× gas. Fair value ~$1,000-$1,150.

### Cross-check: implied multiples

At a base 5-year target of $700, on FY30 base EBITDA of ~$14.8B and ~$10-12B net debt, implied EV/EBITDA is ~14× and forward P/E on FY30 EPS of $20 is ~35×. That sounds rich, but: the multi-decade asset life (post-SLR through 2060+), the structurally unbuildable supply, and the post-2030 SMR optionality all justify a regulated-utility-like multiple on a merchant business with regulated-utility-like cash-flow stability. Comps: NEE trades at ~22× forward P/E; SO trades at ~21×; CEG's growth in FY26-FY30 is ~20% EPS CAGR vs ~6-8% for the regulated utilities — a multiple gap is rational.

## 10. Bear case — 5-year stresses

The 12-month bear case focused on near-term execution risk. The 5-year bear case widens the aperture:

1. **Nuclear safety / outage event.** A serious incident at any US reactor — even a peer-operated one — generates political contagion. A regulatory clamp-down on operating licenses or a state-level moratorium could cap PPA pricing or even force premature shutdowns. Mitigant: CEG operates ~22 reactors with industry-leading capacity factors; track record is strongly mitigating but not eliminating tail risk. **Probability:** low (5-10%); **Impact:** very high (50-70% drawdown).
2. **Calpine integration disappointment.** Synergy realization slips, Calpine fleet underperforms in ERCOT scarcity events, gas-side culture clashes drive operating-team attrition. Across 5 years, integration friction tends to fade — by year 3, Calpine is fully embedded or fully separable. Risk is concentrated in years 1-2. **Probability:** medium (15-25%); **Impact:** moderate (10-20% drawdown).
3. **PJM capacity prices roll.** If PJM 2028/29 BRA reform produces lower rather than higher cap, capacity revenues compress. Less likely than the consensus, but not impossible if PJM prioritizes consumer cost over generator economics. **Probability:** low-medium (10-20%); **Impact:** moderate (10-15% drawdown).
4. **PTC rule reversal or restrictive guidance.** IRS narrows the PTC floor or constrains stacking with PPAs. Most relevant in low-PPA-penetration scenarios. The 5-year base case is largely insulated because PPA pricing dominates by 2030. **Probability:** low (5-10%); **Impact:** small (3-5% drawdown).
5. **Macro rate-cycle multiple compression.** The merchant-nuclear thesis is a long-duration cash-flow story. A sharp and sustained rise in long rates compresses the multiple. Even with 100-150 bps of multiple compression, base-case fair value still sits above the current price — but the IRR drops materially. **Probability:** medium (20-30%); **Impact:** moderate (15-25% multiple compression).
6. **Hyperscaler capex pullback.** If Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon meaningfully cut AI capex over multiple years, the PPA pipeline slows. The 12-month bear case treats this as a flat-line risk; the 5-year bear case has to seriously consider whether the *AI scaling thesis itself* fades. If model-improvement returns to capex flatten by 2028-2029, hyperscaler PPA premium pricing degrades. **Probability:** low-medium (10-20%); **Impact:** large (25-40% drawdown).
7. **FERC co-located large-load tightening.** The Talen/AWS Susquehanna ISA precedent extends; addressable market for high-margin co-located PPAs caps. **Probability:** medium (20-30%); **Impact:** small-moderate (5-10%).
8. **SMR program cancellation.** X-Energy or Holtec stumbles on FOAK economics, DOE LPO support tightens, NRC regulatory pace slows. Removes ~$15-20/share of option value. **Probability:** medium-high (30-40%); **Impact:** small (3-5%).
9. **State-level political risk.** Illinois, Maryland, Pennsylvania, New York policy shifts (capacity market design, retail competition rules, tax abatements). Medium-low probability across 5 years; stable historical track record.
10. **Climate-related operational risk.** Heat waves reducing river-water cooling availability, severe weather forcing extended outages, or wildfire risk (Calpine California assets). Quantifiable but small relative to fleet scale.

**Bear case fair value: $300-$340** (essentially flat; modest IRR from dividend yield only). The asymmetry over 5 years is highly favorable: the bull case (+250-280%) is roughly 4-5× the magnitude of the bear case (flat to mild positive).
